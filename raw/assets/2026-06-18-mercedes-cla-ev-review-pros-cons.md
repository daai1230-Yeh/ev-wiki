# The 2026 Mercedes CLA EV Is Really Impressive. But It Also Drove Me Nuts

**Author**: Mack Hogan | **Date**: Jun 17, 2026

---

The Mercedes CLA EV marks the start of a new era in electric Mercedes design. The EQ cars and their various compromises will soon be gone, replaced by modern, 800-volt, software-defined EVs like the CLA 250+ with EQ Technology. Yet the experience felt much like all recent Mercedes tests: pleasantly surprised by the driving dynamics, then increasingly agitated by design decisions.

**Specs**: Base price $48,600 | Battery 85 kWh | Range up to 374 miles (317 as tested) | 10-80% charge in 22 minutes | 268 hp / 247 lb-ft | 2-speed transmission

**PRO: Excellent driving experience** — The CLA feels light and athletic. It may weigh 4,530 pounds but its eager turn-in and lithe dynamics make it a delight on a twisty backroad. The 268 hp rear-wheel-drive setup gives a 0-60 time of 6.6 seconds, but its two-speed gearbox means it doesn't run out of steam at higher speeds.

**PRO: Range and charging** — Up to 374 miles EPA range and 800-volt architecture with a peak of 320 kW charging (10-80% in 22 minutes). Note: peaks at only 100 kW on 400-volt Tesla Superchargers, so expect slower charging there.

**CON: Weird usability quirks** — No steering wheel skip button for music. Can't see the song title in the gauge cluster. These feel like oversights in a $64,780 car.

**CON: Everything about the windows** — The worst window switch setup in the industry: two driver switches plus a "rear" toggle instead of four individual switches. Rear windows lack auto-up. The pillar-less door design, while stylish, caused rear windows to occasionally fail to seal properly at highway speeds.

**CON: AI assistant doesn't live up to the hype** — Asked for restaurants near a destination, it showed ones near current location. Asked for fast chargers near public parks, it just showed nearby chargers. Conversational navigation didn't work reliably.

**PRO: Great lane-centering** — About as confident and competent as GM's hands-off Super Cruise. Knows to slow down when lanes merge. A hands-on point-to-point system (MB.Drive Assist Pro, using Nvidia DRIVE AV) will come via OTA update.

**CON: Too many error messages** — Almost every start triggered an error that driver assistance systems were unavailable. All cleared within 2-5 minutes, suggesting no real problem, but unsettling.

**Verdict**: A vision of the future, for better and worse. Lovely to drive, impressive range and charging, but too many usability compromises and the AI assistant needs work. It's a great car for buyers who want a tech-forward luxury EV — but it doesn't yet deliver the relentless refinement Mercedes has historically stood for.
