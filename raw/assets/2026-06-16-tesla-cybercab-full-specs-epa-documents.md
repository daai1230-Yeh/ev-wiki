# Full Tesla Cybercab Specs: EPA Documents Confirm Battery Size, Weight, Power, And Preliminary Range

**Author**: Mack Hogan | **Date**: 2026-06-15

---

The Tesla Cybercab just got one step closer to mass production. Official EPA filings confirm:

- **Battery**: 48 kWh (146 Ah at 326V)
- **Motor**: Single front-mounted permanent magnet, 169 hp (163 kW) — Tesla's first front-wheel-drive vehicle
- **Weight**: 3,113 lbs — ~700 lbs lighter than the lightest Model 3, comparable to a Honda Civic
- **GVWR**: 3,730 lbs (payload capacity: ~617 lbs)
- **Estimated EPA range**: ~293 miles (lab-tested 418 miles × 0.7 adjustment factor)

The Cybercab is set to launch "before 2027" at a price under $30,000. It was originally planned without steering wheel or pedals but test units have been fitted with both. Tesla has begun production but unit count is unclear.

The biggest open questions remain: when it will actually go on sale, what it will cost, and whether Tesla's FSD autonomous driving software will be ready for the nationwide robotaxi rollout Elon Musk has long promised.
