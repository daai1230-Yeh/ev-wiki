# Rivian's New AI Voice Assistant Is Rolling Out. Here's What It Can Do

**Author**: Tim Levin | **Date**: May 12, 2026

---

Rivian announced on Tuesday that its in-house AI voice assistant — the Rivian Assistant — is now rolling out via the company's latest over-the-air software update. It is available on all Gen 1 and Gen 2 R1S and R1T vehicles, provided owners maintain a connectivity subscription, and the R2 will receive it when it reaches customers in the coming weeks. The launch follows the announcement made at Rivian's Autonomy & AI Day in December, when the company committed to shipping the assistant in early 2026.

Rather than relying on phone-mirroring voice tools, the Rivian Assistant is built directly into the vehicle's hardware and software, and it draws on a mix of in-house and external AI models. Drivers can summon it with the wake phrase "Hey Rivian" or a steering-wheel button, and use it to switch drive modes, view vehicle cameras, surface points of interest, or run multi-step commands such as sending restaurant suggestions and an ETA from the navigation system. It also retains conversational context and can field general-knowledge questions in the manner of a chatbot. Rivian additionally pitches the assistant as an in-car owner's manual, letting drivers ask the vehicle questions like how to change a tire instead of paging through documentation.

The agentic features are what Rivian highlights most. The first third-party integration is Google Calendar, allowing connected users to reschedule meetings, navigate to appointments, and relay calendar info via voice while driving. The release lands amid a wider industry push: General Motors, Volvo, and Polestar recently signed up for Google Gemini in their cars, while newer Teslas use xAI's Grok.
