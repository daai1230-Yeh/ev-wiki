# Foxconn and EMP to begin producing electric SUVs in Poland from 2029

**Date**: 2026-06-18

---

Polish PM Donald Tusk has confirmed reports of an EV factory planned by ElectroMobility Poland (EMP) and Foxconn in southern Poland. Up to 400,000 electric SUVs will be produced annually onsite.

## Factory Details

- **Location**: Jaworzno, southern Poland
- **Production start**: 2029
- **Capacity**: Up to 400,000 electric SUVs per year
- **Construction start**: Planned for spring 2027
- **Scope**: Factory + new R&D centre

## Partnership Structure

The joint venture involves Foxconn and its subsidiary Foxtron, which provides ready-made reference designs for electric vehicles. The plan includes three mid-range EV models, including SUVs. Second-generation platforms are expected to be developed with over 70% involvement from Polish engineers.

## Context

ElectroMobility Poland has been working toward Poland's first domestic electric vehicle for years, with previous production timelines repeatedly delayed. This Foxconn partnership gives the project a credible manufacturing partner with proven EV production experience (Foxtron produces EVs for multiple brands in Asia).

The factory would make Poland a significant EV manufacturing hub in Central Europe, with scale rivalling major existing EV plants on the continent.
