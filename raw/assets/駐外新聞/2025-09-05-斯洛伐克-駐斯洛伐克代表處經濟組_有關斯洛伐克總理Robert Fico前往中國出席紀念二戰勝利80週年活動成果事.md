附件斯洛伐克總理府刊登總理Robert Fico前往中國出席紀念二戰勝利80週年活動成果英譯I concluded my two-day visit to China with an official meeting with Chinese President Xi Jinping, after official talks with Russian President Vladimir Putin, Vietnamese President Luong Cuong, and the President of Serbia Aleksandar Vučić, as well as informal meetings with a dozen other heads of state.I emphasized to President Xi that China and Slovakia share the same views on peace, as well as opposition to fascism, which in Slovakia found expression in the Slovak National Uprising (SNP). Without any exaggeration, I can state that Sino-Slovak relations are getting closer and closer, and we are beginning to play an active role in EU–China relations as well. I last met President Xi in November of last year, so we were able to assess the progress achieved in our bilateral ties.Among other things, I welcome the fact that in 2026 Slovak tourists will still be able to travel to China visa-free. Over the past ten months, the number of Slovak tourists has thus increased by almost 15,000, and interest continues to grow—so much so that we again discussed a direct air route between Bratislava and Beijing. I especially recommend to the hardheads in the opposition and the anti-state, anti-government media that they take this opportunity and visit China. Their jaws will be hanging open for two weeks after they return.President Xi promised Slovakia cooperation in issuing licenses for the export of Chinese rare earths and minerals to Slovakia, which we urgently need for our industry to function. Following the launch of the investment by the battery manufacturer Gotion, the Chinese government is interested in supporting other major Chinese investors in activities in Slovakia. Our good political and economic dialogue will continue to develop thanks also to the strategic partnership we signed last November, to which both governments are giving due attention. I believe that before long the Slovak Cultural Institute in Beijing and a Chinese institute in Bratislava will also become reality.Another proof of mutual trust is President Xi’s announcement that, during a visit to Europe, he will come to Slovakia.The talks with President Xi also had an important political component concerning peace in Ukraine, reform of the international community, and an assessment of the impact of yesterday’s commemorations in Beijing on the international political situation. I reiterated that while others may focus on the military parade, I personally consider the political messages voiced yesterday to be more essential. These cannot be overlooked or ignored. EU prime ministers can stick their heads in the sand as much as they like. Today, thanks to its short-sightedness in world politics and infatuation with itself, the EU is not on the second, but perhaps already on the third track.At the very end of our visit to China we have been invited to a dinner hosted by Chinese Vice President Han Zheng. And, as already confirmed, we are heading straight to eastern Slovakia so that tomorrow we can be available for talks with Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy and Prime Minister Yuliia Svyrydenko. Since European Council President António Costa will also be there at that time, an informal meeting with him cannot be ruled out. Have a nice day in Slovakia.
