# Ganfeng starts pilot production of 500 Wh/kg solid-state batteries

**Author**: Carla Westerheide | **Date**: 2026-05-21

---

Chinese battery materials specialist Ganfeng Lithium has reported progress in the development of solid-state batteries for electric mobility applications, including the start of small-batch production of a lithium-metal cell with an energy density of 500 Wh/kg and a cycle life milestone for its 400 Wh/kg battery platform.

## Key Milestones

- **500 Wh/kg lithium-metal battery**: Ganfeng's 10 Ah lithium-metal battery has entered small-batch (pilot) production. Described as the world's first 10 Ah solid-state product to achieve 500 Wh/kg, setting an industry benchmark for future commercialisation of lithium-metal batteries.
- **400 Wh/kg solid-state battery**: Has exceeded 1,100 charge cycles and completed engineering validation.
- This comes only three months after Ganfeng announced production of a semi-solid-state battery with an energy density of 650 Wh/kg.

## Technology Routes

Ganfeng is pursuing two parallel solid-state battery technology routes:
1. **Silicon-carbon anodes** — products ranging from 320 to 480 Wh/kg energy density
2. **Lithium-metal anodes** — seen as the key route to higher energy density and faster commercialisation

The 320 Wh/kg battery has already exceeded 1,000 charging cycles; development of the 480 Wh/kg version is still ongoing.

## Target Markets

Premium electric vehicles, drones, eVTOL aircraft, robotics, and consumer electronics.

## Customer Partnerships

Ganfeng supplies Hyundai and Changan. Dongfeng has begun extreme-cold testing of vehicles with 350 Wh/kg solid-state batteries. Changan plans trial installations of 400 Wh/kg solid-state batteries during 2026.
