# Lucid Cosmos: Tesla Model Y and Rivian R2 Rival Revealed In Patent Images

**Author**: Suvrat Kothari | **Date**: June 16, 2026

---

The roughly $50,000 Cosmos is supposed to go on sale by the end of this year. It will be the brand's first mass-market EV.

## Key Points

- Patent images filed with the European Union Intellectual Property Office (EUIPO) by "Atieva Inc." (Lucid's older name) reveal the Cosmos' design
- Starting price: ~$50,000
- Production begins in Saudi Arabia by end of 2026; Arizona factory production 6–12 months later
- First Lucid model with mass-market potential

## Design

The Cosmos looks like a shrunken Gravity with a cab-forward design and a large Lucid emblem on the nose. It has a sculpted, aggressive look. The roof is sharply raked with a spoiler cutting across the rear glass, requiring a digital rearview camera for visibility. "Lucid" is spelled out in large letters across the back.

The design is more sporty than the R2 or Model Y — comparable to the Audi Q6 Sportback e-tron.

## Key Specs (Announced at Investor Day)

- **Drag coefficient**: 0.22
- **Projected range**: Over 300 miles
- **Charging**: 800-volt architecture, 200 miles added in 14 minutes
- **Bidirectional charging**: Vehicle-to-home (V2H) and vehicle-to-grid (V2G) support
- **Interior**: Single large dashboard-spanning screen (departure from Air/Gravity's multi-screen layout)

## Strategic Importance

Just as the Model 3 and Model Y catapulted Tesla into the mainstream, Lucid is placing a similar bet on the Cosmos. After years of burning cash with the Air and Gravity (both excellent but niche products), the Cosmos is needed for Lucid to reach profitability.

The Cosmos will also form the basis for robotaxis as part of Lucid's partnership with Uber. EVs on the midsize platform will roll out as robotaxis.
