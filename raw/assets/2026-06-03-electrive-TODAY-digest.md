# electrive TODAY — 3 June 2026

**Source**: electrive global (newsletter@electrive.com)

---

## Exclusive
- **HPC push – ChargePoint vies for market share in Europe** — ChargePoint stepping up European expansion as it looks beyond a slowing US market. CEO Rick Wilmer said Europe could generate around half of future revenue, helped by new HPC hardware and government EV support.

## News
- **MG plans Spanish factory for EU expansion** — MG Motor (SAIC) intends to build its first European factory in Spain, targeting annual production of 120,000 vehicles as part of an "in Europe, for Europe" strategy, with ~€200 million investment.
- **Mulhouse to build Peugeot models on STLA One** — Stellantis confirmed a billion-euro investment in its Mulhouse plant; from 2029 the site will produce three Peugeot models on the STLA One platform, including electric and hybrid variants.
- **BYD returns to slight sales growth in May** — First YoY sales increase in eight months (+0.26%), suggesting stagnation rather than recovery, despite strong export growth.
- **Daimler Truck introduces eActros Lowliner** — Following the MAN eTGX, the Mercedes-Benz eActros Lowliner is set for launch next year, matching a key MAN advantage in automotive logistics configurations.
- **Scania to build electric trucks in France** — €70 million investment in the Angers plant for electric truck production; also presented its first battery-electric double cab for emergency vehicles (Scania CP31L).

## Number of the Day
- Following the launch of the Model Y's six-seat 'L' version in India, Tesla cut the entry price by 900,000 rupees (~€8,100) to reposition the model competitively.

## Technology
- **Uber unveils new Munich robotaxi partner** — Uber plans a robotaxi pilot in Munich with Israeli autonomous driving specialist Autobrains and Nvidia, despite a previously announced pilot with China's Momenta.
- **BYD offers liability cover for driver assist system in China** — BYD's "God's Eye" system now includes a scheme covering full liability for its Urban NOA driver assistance feature.
- **Cylib and Vianode aim to close graphite loop** — German recycler Cylib and Norway's Vianode signed a letter of intent to develop and test recycled graphite from batteries for next-gen battery production.
- **Volvo CE and Hitachi partner on clean sites** — MoU to combine electric machinery with charging, power supply and energy management to support zero-emission construction sites.

## Feedback
- Yesterday's most-clicked link: Mini keeps electric car production out of Oxford for now.

## Quotes of the Day
- Vicky Edmonds (CEO, EVA England): "The priority must be turning that driver interest into real transactions, not creating more confusion about the future." She criticized calls to delay or weaken the UK's ZEV mandate review.
- Liam Byrne (British MP): "The transition to electric vehicles is essential. But transitions succeed when they are grounded in commercial reality and backed by a serious industrial strategy." He warned delayed ZEV mandate reforms could pose an "existential risk" to the UK car industry.

## Fleets + Infrastructure
- **First Bus deploys repowered e-buses in Bath** — Converting 27 former diesel double-deckers into battery-electric vehicles as part of fleet decarbonisation.
- **EZO and eir open Dublin HPC hub** — First of 50 planned urban charging hubs across Ireland over the next three years, opened in Walkinstown.
- **Amber to expand Australian vehicle-to-grid scheme** — Expanding V2G to 1,000 homes, Australia's largest residential V2G rollout, building on a 50-home pilot from October.
- **Kempower delivers Australia's first pantograph-down electric bus chargers** — Brookvale Bus Depot installed nearly a dozen Kempower pantograph chargers, enabling electrification of up to 229 diesel buses.
- **MCFT orders 20 Farizon SV electric vans** — Commercial kitchen specialist selected the Chinese-built eLCV after a month-long comparison with three rival electric vans.

## Service
- Reading tip: More Canadians considering electric cars again per J.D. Power; interest in Chinese EV brands growing due to lower prices, though range, charging access and cold-weather performance remain barriers.
- Klick tip: Spy shots show the Lucid Cosmos mid-size model during test drives in Arizona alongside a Tesla Model Y.

## Short Circuit
- Tesla robotaxi fleet in Texas reaches only 42 vehicles — well below Musk's earlier target of 1,000, mostly operating in Austin with smaller deployments in Dallas and Houston.
