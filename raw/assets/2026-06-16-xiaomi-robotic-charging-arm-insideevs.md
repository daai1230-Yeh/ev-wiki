# Tesla's Wild Robotic Charging Arm Never Happened. Xiaomi Just Made It Reality

**Author**: Iulian Dnistran | **Date**: 2026-06-15

---

Xiaomi unveiled a robotic EV charging arm — a concept Tesla teased a decade ago but never shipped. While Tesla has since pivoted to wireless charging for the Cybercab, Xiaomi's autonomous robot is expected to go on sale in Q4 2026.

The charging robot fits in a slim enclosure less than 6 inches (152mm) wide, making it usable in tight garages or public parking. It integrates into Xiaomi's smart home ecosystem and has three key capabilities: auto-initiates charging when the car is parked, auto-unplugs when the battery reaches the target state of charge, and allows remote initiation of charging if you forgot to plug in.

Xiaomi claims sub-millimeter precision using AI vision recognition. The arm communicates directly with the car to open/close the motorized charge port. Pricing and whether the enclosure includes the charger itself (vs. attaching to an existing home charger) have not yet been disclosed. Xiaomi already makes home chargers from 7–11 kW, so an all-in-one solution is expected.

Xiaomi isn't alone in this space — Hyundai and Star Charge have similar robotic charging arm solutions.
