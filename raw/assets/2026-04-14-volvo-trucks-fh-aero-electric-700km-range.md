# Volvo Trucks increases range of flagship e-truck to 700 km

**Author**: Cora Werwitzke | **Date**: April 14, 2026

---

Volvo Trucks will present its new long-haul electric truck in June. Ahead of the launch, the Swedish manufacturer has released further details on the FH Aero Electric—most notably revising the previously stated range from 600 to 'up to 700 kilometres.'

Volvo Trucks has announced an additional 100 kilometres of range compared to its most recent figures for the new flagship model. The company has not specified exactly how the increase to up to 700 kilometres has been achieved.

Sister brand Renault Trucks took the lead in presenting a largely identical model: the E-Tech T 780 with a range of 600 kilometres unveiled in November at Solutrans in Lyon. Volvo, which typically takes precedence in model launches, is following around six months later—but now appears determined to surpass all competitors in terms of range.

## How the Range Increase Was Achieved

A Volvo spokesperson explained: "Thanks to a combination of software optimisation, increased usable battery capacity and further improved aerodynamics, we will be able to offer the 'FH Aero Electric with extended range' in its highest specification with a range of up to 700 kilometres at the start of series production."

Note: Volvo uses 'up to'—an optimistic value that may not be achieved under less favourable conditions. Daimler Truck, by contrast, specifies 500 km for the eActros 600 as a conservatively calculated minimum.

## Key Specifications

- **Peak power output**: 460 kW (total)
- **Max gross combination weight**: 48 tonnes
- **Payload**: up to 28 tonnes (pre-trailer tare weight; roughly 21–22 tonnes of goods in practice)
- **Drivetrain**: New e-axle with two electric motors and 6-speed gearbox (replacing the I-Shift transmission)
- **Battery**: 780 kWh gross capacity across 8 battery packs (NCA chemistry from Samsung SDI, matching Renault sister model); Volvo extracts a higher usable share from gross capacity
- **MCS charging**: ~50 minutes (20–80% SoC) at 700 kW
- **CCS charging**: ~85 minutes (20–80% SoC) at 350 kW
- **Electric PTO**: Eliminates need for separate diesel generator for refrigerated units
- **Production start**: Summer 2026, world premiere in early June

## Competitive Context

The FH Aero Electric charges slightly more slowly than the Renault E-Tech T 780 (40 min MCS vs 50 min), but offers greater range. Competing models include the Mercedes-Benz eActros 600 (500 km conservative range) and the Nikola Tre BEV.

Roger Alm, President of Volvo Trucks: "This long-haul electric truck is the best in the industry. It offers an outstanding range in combination with high payload, fast charging and great riding comfort."
