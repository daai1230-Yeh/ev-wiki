# Renault acquires Flexis in full

**Author**: Sebastian Schaal | **Date**: 23.02.2026

---

Renault Group will fully acquire Flexis, the electric van joint venture it founded in 2024 with the Volvo Group and logistics specialist CMA CGM. Renault is taking full control while the Volvo Group continues as a strategic partner.

The three founding shareholders held shares as follows: Renault Group and Volvo Group each at 45%, and CMA CGM at 10%. Renault will acquire the 55% stake held by its two partners. The acquisition price was not disclosed.

Relations between shareholders had reportedly deteriorated after François Provost took over as Renault Group CEO in mid-2025, leading to a commercial court mediator being called in before parties agreed on a full takeover by Renault.

**Going forward**: Renault Group will push battery-electric light commercial vehicles to production maturity and build them at its Sandouville plant. From 2027, Renault Trucks (a Volvo Group brand, not Renault Group) will handle distribution — underlining continued operational ties.

New CEO Krishnan Sundararajan succeeds Philippe Divry, effective 23 February 2026.
