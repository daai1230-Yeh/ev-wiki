# Renault improves efficiency of R4 and R5 powertrains

**Author**: Daniel Bönnighausen | **Date**: 2026-06-29

---

According to a report by *Autocar*, Renault is developing a new generation of electric motors for the **Renault 4** and **Renault 5**, targeting improved efficiency and higher power output. Renault is focusing particularly on the **inverter** and **reduction gearbox**.

## Current Specs

Both models use the **AmpR Small platform** with two configurations:
- **Urban Range**: 90 kW motor + 40 kWh battery → up to 312 km WLTP (R5) / 308 km (R4)
- **Comfort Range**: 110 kW motor + 52 kWh battery → up to 410 km WLTP (R5) / 409 km (R4)

## Upcoming Upgrades

- New more powerful, more efficient Gen 2 Evo powertrains expected **end of 2026 or early 2027**
- Based on developments from the new **Twingo's 60 kW drive unit**
- Upgrades will NOT include the third-gen E7A motor (planned for next-gen Mégane/Scénic from 2028)
- No switch to **800-volt architecture** planned for these models

## LFP Battery Option

Renault UK MD Adam Wood told *Auto Express* that **LFP battery technology** is on the future roadmap, specifically for the Urban Range variants of the R4 and R5. No timeline disclosed; could enable lower-cost entry-level variants.

## Competitive Context

Upcoming rivals are applying pressure:
- **VW ID. Polo**: 155 kW / 52 kWh → 454 km WLTP; 99 kW / 37 kWh LFP → 329 km
- **Skoda Epiq**: 155 kW / 52 kWh → 440+ km; LFP entry: ~310 km

The efficiency gap is most pronounced in the Comfort Range variants, making the powertrain upgrades strategically critical.
