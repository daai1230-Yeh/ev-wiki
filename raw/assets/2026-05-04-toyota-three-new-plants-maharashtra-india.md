# Toyota plans 3 new assembly plants in Maharashtra, India to triple capacity

**Author**: Reuters / Nikkei | **Date**: 2026-05-01

---

Toyota Motor plans to build three vehicle assembly plants in the western Indian state of Maharashtra, as automakers shift focus from stagnating markets like the US and China to fast-growing emerging markets.

## Key Facts

- **New plants**: 3 assembly facilities in Maharashtra (western India)
- **Investment**: ~300 billion yen (~$1.91 billion)
- **Capacity target**: 1 million units/year by the 2030s (triple current India capacity)
- **Total India factories**: 6 (3 existing in southern India + 3 new in Maharashtra)
- **First plant operational**: ~2029
- **Global ranking**: India to become Toyota's **4th largest production base** (after Japan, China, USA)

## Products & Strategy

The new plants will produce a mix of vehicles including SUVs (Corolla-nameplate three-row SUV) and **plug-in hybrid vehicles** for environmentally conscious consumers. Alongside domestic demand, the factories will supply vehicles to Middle East and African export markets.

Toyota's India expansion is part of a broader strategic pivot away from stagnating markets (US, China) toward high-growth emerging markets. Suzuki (Maruti) is Toyota's strategic partner in India — Suzuki produces EVs and small cars for Toyota under their collaboration agreement.
