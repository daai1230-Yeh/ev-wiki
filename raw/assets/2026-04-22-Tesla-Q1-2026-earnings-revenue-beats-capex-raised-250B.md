# Tesla Q1 2026 Earnings: Revenue Beats Expectations, Capex Raised to $25B+

**Author**: Various (via Google Alerts) | **Date**: 2026-04-22

---

Tesla reported Q1 2026 financial results on April 22, 2026, beating analyst expectations on both revenue and earnings per share, but raising capital expenditure guidance significantly above prior forecasts.

**Key financials:**
- Total revenue: $22.387 billion (+16% YoY, vs. estimate of $22.35B)
- Automotive revenue: $16.2 billion (+16% YoY, from $14B)
- Energy segment revenue: $2.41 billion (-12% YoY, from $2.73B)
- Adjusted EPS: $0.41 (beat estimate of $0.37)
- Net income: $477 million (+17% YoY)

**Capital expenditure guidance:** Tesla raised its 2026 capex guidance to over $25 billion, up from a prior forecast of $20 billion — a $5 billion increase that surprised investors. The higher spending reflects Tesla's aggressive investment in infrastructure, manufacturing, and AI/autonomous vehicle development.

**Stock reaction:** Shares initially rose ~4% in after-hours trading on the earnings beat, but retreated after the higher capex guidance was revealed on the earnings call.

**Context:** Tesla entered Q1 2026 facing headwinds including declining registrations in key markets (including a plunge in California) and investor concerns about demand. The Q1 beat provided some relief, though the revenue miss on the top-line consensus and surging capex remain key concerns.
