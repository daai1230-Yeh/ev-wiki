# MAN unveils electric 16-tonne truck eTGM

**Author**: Cora Werwitzke | **Date**: May 13, 2026

---

The TGM is MAN's all-rounder in medium-duty distribution transport. Starting next year, the truck will also be available with an electric drive. The Traton subsidiary just unveiled the eTGM in Milan.

With the eTGM, MAN closes the final gap in its all-electric portfolio. According to the Munich-based manufacturer, its electric truck range now covers vehicles from 12 to 50 tonnes. For the new mid-sized model, MAN relies on its extensive shared-component strategy, using batteries and the electric drivetrain already deployed in other trucks in the brand's lineup.

MAN explained this strategy: "Like all MAN e-trucks, the eTGM is based on the standardised modular BEV platform of the heavy-duty series. The central drive unit, battery systems, high-voltage architecture, and thermal management are derived from the eTGX and eTGS development and have been adapted for the medium-duty segment."

The vehicle made its debut at the commercial vehicle trade fair Transpotec Logitec in Milan, with production set to begin next year at MAN's plant in Kraków.

## Positioning and Specs

At 16 tonnes, the eTGM sits between the 12-tonne eTGL and the full-sized eTGX/eTGS. MAN positions the new series for urban and regional distribution, municipal applications, and the construction sector. It offers a chassis payload of up to 10.6 tonnes and, when combined with a trailer, a gross combination weight of up to 33 tonnes.

## Battery and Range

The centrepiece of the eTGM is an NMC battery with two to four packs, each providing 80 kWh net capacity. In its maximum configuration, this amounts to a total net capacity of 320 kWh, which is expected to deliver up to **480 kilometres of range**. Achieving the maximum payload is only possible with the smallest battery configuration (reduced range). If range is the priority, this comes at the expense of payload.

The eTGM supports DC charging at up to **375 kW**, with a charging session from 10 to 80% state of charge (SoC) taking 43 minutes on paper (with four battery packs). AC charging will be introduced later, planned by end of 2027.

## Drivetrain

The manufacturer relies on a central electric drive unit (eCD210) with 210 kW output and a maximum torque of 800 Nm, combined with MAN's two-speed transmission, TipMatic 2. While this electric motor delivers 245 kW in the larger trucks, it is downgraded to 210 kW for the eTGM. The eTGM supports both one-pedal driving and the electric-typical coasting function.

## EU Regulatory Credits

MAN specifies the permissible gross vehicle weight as 16.01 tonnes (optional 16.5 tonnes also available). The reason: electric trucks only qualify for EU regulatory credits once they exceed 16 tonnes, granting significant toll advantages in many European markets.

## Executive View

Friedrich Baumann, MAN's Executive Board Member for Sales & Customer Solutions, stated: "It is the logical completion of our eTruck portfolio and makes MAN a true full-range provider of battery-electric commercial vehicles." He also emphasised that electric drivetrains are the most efficient among all propulsion types, with an efficiency of over 70%.
