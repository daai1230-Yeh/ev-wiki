# BMW integrates Neue Klasse technologies into the i7

**Author**: Sebastian Schaal | **Date**: 2026-04-22

---

Munich-based automaker BMW unveiled the updated version of the 7 Series sedan ahead of Auto China in Beijing. The electric model i7 benefits from advancements of the Neue Klasse—including a completely new battery.

With the iX3 and the i3 as a battery-electric saloon in the 3 Series format, BMW has already introduced two models based on its Neue Klasse platform. The drivetrain technology is well known, as it is more or less directly derived from the iX3, the first vehicle built on the new platform. However, BMW's emphasis on developing not isolated platforms, but rather technologies that can be integrated across different vehicles, means the updated i7 benefits from this approach.

**Battery and powertrain:** The i7 now gets a bigger battery with more range and faster charging speeds. The maximum DC charging rate has increased to 250 kW, and the i7 can go from 10% to 80% in approximately 28 minutes at compatible stations.

**Electrical architecture:** All 7 Series trims get an updated electrical architecture, newer software, and a zonal wiring harness derived from Neue Klasse development. This includes a new centralized computer with significantly more processing power, reducing the wiring harness weight and length considerably.

**Design:** The front end receives angular new light signatures, with main headlights now mounted vertically. Combined with sharper design elements including the BMW kidney grille, the front appears more cohesive and imposing. The side profile remains largely unchanged. The rear tail lights clearly reflect Neue Klasse influence and BMW's current brand design language.

**Interior:** The cabin features high-quality materials, a minimalist design, and an immersive lighting experience. The Panoramic iDrive — a slim, continuous display spanning the dashboard — incorporates technology from the Neue Klasse into the flagship sedan.
