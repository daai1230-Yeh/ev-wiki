# Alexandria's DASH launches first on-route electric bus charging project in Washington region

**Author**: Editorial staff | **Date**: 2026-06-29

---

The **Alexandria Transit Company (DASH)** has begun construction of an **on-route charging system** for its battery-electric bus fleet in Alexandria, Virginia — the **first on-route electric bus chargers in Northern Virginia and the Washington D.C. metropolitan region**.

The project was officially launched with a groundbreaking ceremony at the West End Alexandria redevelopment site (formerly Landmark Mall), attended by local officials including DASH CEO Josh Baker, Alexandria Mayor Alyia Gaskins, and U.S. Representative Don Beyer.

## Two 360 kW pantograph chargers for in-service charging

The project includes the **installation of two overhead pantograph chargers** capable of delivering up to **360 kW** of charging power. Unlike depot charging, the opportunity charging system recharges buses within minutes while they remain in passenger service.

The infrastructure is **funded through a DASH-City of Alexandria partnership**, supported by more than $1 million in federal funding through the FY2024 Consolidated Appropriations Act.

DASH selected **ABM** to deliver the charging infrastructure. Construction is scheduled for completion in **2027**.

## DASH electrification progress

DASH and the City of Alexandria adopted a target in 2019 to transition the fleet to 100% zero-emission vehicles:
- **16 battery-electric buses** currently in service
- **20 additional vehicles** funded and under procurement
- Depot expansion project started late 2025 (capacity for 30+ buses, 24 future pantograph chargers)

**Josh Baker**, General Manager and CEO: "This is a monumental step toward a cleaner, quieter and more sustainable transit system. As the first on-route electric bus chargers in Northern Virginia and the D.C. region, this reflects years of planning and collaboration."
