# France opens third round of EV social leasing programme

**Author**: Florian Treiss | **Date**: 2026-06-29

---

The French government will open the **third funding round** of its social leasing scheme for electric cars on **16 July**. The programme enables low-income employees to lease an electric vehicle at subsidised rates and forms part of France's efforts to accelerate the transition to electric mobility.

## Eligibility Criteria (Relaxed vs. Round 2)

To qualify, applicants must either:
- Travel more than 10 km each day by private car, OR
- Drive more than 8,000 km per year for work using their own vehicle

Income threshold: taxable reference income of **no more than €16,880** (relaxed from €16,300 in Round 2).

## Leasing Terms

- Monthly lease payments capped at **€200** (no upfront payment/deposit)
- Contracts run for **3 years** with an annual mileage allowance of 15,000 km
- Target: **50,000 battery-electric vehicles** under preferential conditions
- Round 2 (autumn 2025) lease prices ranged from €95–€195/month

## Available Models

- **Citroën ë-C3** from €94/month
- **Renault Twingo** from €130/month; **Renault 5** from €139/month
- **Peugeot E-208, E-2008, E-308** from €149/month
- **Volkswagen ID. Polo, ID.3 Neo, ID.3, ID.4** also available

## Government Subsidies

- Government covers **29% of vehicle purchase price**, up to €6,500/vehicle
- Up to **€9,000** if both vehicle and battery are manufactured within the EEA
- Extra **€500 bonus** if electric motor is also EEA-produced
- Chinese-built EVs effectively excluded via CO₂ footprint threshold
