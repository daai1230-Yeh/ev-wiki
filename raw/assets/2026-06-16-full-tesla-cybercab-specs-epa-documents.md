# Full Tesla Cybercab Specs: EPA Documents Confirm Battery Size, Weight, Power, And Preliminary Range

**Author**: Mack Hogan | **Date**: 2026-06-15

---

The Tesla Cybercab will have 219 horsepower and a 48 kWh battery, new EPA filings confirm. It'll weigh 3,113 lbs, making it the lightest EV on the U.S. market right now. It looks like it'll have around 293 miles of range, based on preliminary testing figures.

The Tesla Cybercab just got one step closer to mass production. The company has filed official documents with the EPA required to make it a full production vehicle. Thanks to the documents, first spotted by Car and Driver, we now know the official weight, power output, and battery size of the two-door robotaxi.

**Key specs confirmed by EPA filing:**

- **Weight**: 3,113 pounds — about 700 lbs less than the lightest Model 3, comparable to a Honda Civic Touring
- **GVWR**: 3,730 lbs (giving 617 lbs payload capacity)
- **Motor**: Single, front-mounted permanent magnet motor with 169 horsepower (163 kW) — making it Tesla's first front-wheel-drive vehicle
- **Battery**: 146 amp-hour at 326 volts = ~48 kWh
- **Range**: 418.2 miles in EPA lab testing; adjusted by 0.7 factor = ~293 miles of real-world EPA range (a Tesla executive claimed "close to 300 miles")

The Cybercab does not originally come with a steering wheel or pedals (true robotaxi design), though Tesla later fitted both to test units. Tesla says production has already begun.

The two biggest open questions remain: When will it actually go on sale, and what will it actually cost? The last known target was "before 2027" at under $30,000. True autonomous operation is still being tested in select cities, far from a nationwide rollout.
