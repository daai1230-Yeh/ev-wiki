# Ford's EV losses narrow to $777M in Q1 2026 amid Model e profitability efforts

**Author**: Florian Treiss | **Date**: 2026-04-30

---

Ford slightly reduced losses in its EV division in Q1 2026, but its EV business remains firmly in the red. The Model e unit reported a loss of $777 million, an improvement of $72 million compared to the same period last year.

## Key Figures (Q1 2026)

- **Model e loss**: $777M (vs. $849M in Q1 2025) — improvement of $72M
- **EBIT margin**: -63.1% (improved by 5.3 percentage points YoY)
- **Loss per vehicle**: ~$23,000 per EV sold
- **Revenue**: $1.2 billion (same as prior year)
- **EV unit sales**: ~34,000 units (up from ~31,000 in Q1 2025)

## Context

Ford's Model e division covers battery-electric passenger cars (Puma Gen-E, Explorer, Mustang Mach-E) — not electric vans like the E-Transit (Ford Pro). The segment faced major headwinds after the Trump administration withdrew the $7,500 EV tax credit (effective 30 September 2025). Ford discontinued the F-150 Lightning electric pickup last autumn, and announced $19.5 billion in write-downs in December 2025.

Despite selling 10% more EVs, Ford generated the same revenue, suggesting lower average selling prices — likely reflecting a higher share of cheaper European models.

## Outlook

- **Full-year 2026 EBIT loss guidance**: $4.0–4.5B (improvement over $4.8B loss in 2025)
- Ford is preparing more affordable, scalable EVs on the **Ford Universal EV platform (UEV)**, announced last August
- Also expanding **Ford Energy** battery storage business

Ford's total Q1 2026: revenue $43.3B, net profit $2.5B, adjusted EBIT $3.5B. Ford Blue (ICE) sold 584,000 vehicles; Ford Pro sold 316,000 commercial vehicles.
