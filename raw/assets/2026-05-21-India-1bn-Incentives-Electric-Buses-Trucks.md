# India weighs $1 bn-plus incentives to accelerate private adoption of electric buses, trucks

**Author**: ETAuto / Bloomberg | **Date**: 2026-05-20

---

India is considering an incentive package exceeding $1 billion to spur private-sector adoption of electric buses and trucks, aiming to cut fossil-fuel use in commercial transport amid a deepening energy crisis.

Key details reported:

- The program would run for about a decade and target India's largely privately owned commercial-vehicle fleet, with the biggest share likely earmarked for inter-city bus operators.
- Incentives under consideration include interest-subvention benefits of up to 1.5 million rupees (about $17,500) per vehicle over its lifetime, tapering over time.
- The government is also evaluating a partial credit-guarantee mechanism to encourage lenders to finance private companies' purchases of electric commercial vehicles.
- Meetings with the Prime Minister's Office and industry stakeholders were expected within the month to refine the plan; final budget allocation, eligible vehicles and subsidy structure remain to be worked out.
