# India approves nearly 4,900 EV chargers under PM E-Drive scheme

**Author**: Carla Westerheide | **Date**: 2026-05-13

---

India's government has approved 4,874 public EV chargers under the PM E-Drive scheme, backed by ₹503.86 crore (~€44 million) in funding. The rollout covers proposals from public-sector oil marketing companies (HPCL, IOCL, BPCL) and multiple state governments.

## Key Details

- **Chargers approved**: 4,874 public EV chargers
- **Funding**: ₹503.86 crore (~€44 million)
- **Participating states**: Rajasthan, Andhra Pradesh, Uttar Pradesh, Gujarat, Kerala, Telangana, Karnataka, Tamil Nadu
- **Total PM E-Drive charging budget**: ₹2,000 crore (~€180 million) — target of 72,000+ public chargers nationwide
- **Announced at**: EV charging infrastructure conference in Bengaluru

## Unified Bharat eCharge Platform

The government is developing a unified digital platform (Unified Bharat eCharge) to let EV users locate chargers, access services, and make payments across different charging networks via a single app — addressing fragmentation in India's charging ecosystem.

## Broader EV Policy Context

India's EV manufacturing incentives running parallel:
- ₹18,100 crore (~€1.6B) PLI scheme for advanced chemistry cell (battery) manufacturing
- ₹25,938 crore (~€2.3B) PLI-Auto scheme for EV and component production
- New rare earth permanent magnets programme

**India's existing infrastructure**: 27,737 EV chargers installed by March 2026 (not all operational); 8,932 were installed under the earlier FAME-II programme.
