# Daimler Buses to invest €200m in service network

**Author**: Cora Werwitzke | **Date**: 2026-04-17

---

Daimler Buses will invest up to €200 million in Europe over the coming years to establish new service locations, modernise existing facilities, and expand its range of services—with a particular focus on charging solutions. The eCoach technology demonstrator will also soon begin test drives.

Daimler Buses CEO Till Oberwörder emphasised: "Service makes the difference. To ensure this, our customers need a partner who supports them from vehicle selection and the right infrastructure through to fast and reliable maintenance." This is the largest service investment in Daimler Buses' history, and is focused exclusively on buses (compared to MAN's €300m investment which covers both buses and trucks).

Daimler Buses currently operates around 630 service locations across Europe (36 owned, rest partner-operated). Around 130 now handle electric buses, with certified high-voltage experts on site. The company is launching a dedicated charging hotline (available in 8 languages, aiming to resolve ~85% of cases remotely) and a specialised charging service team, rolling out first in Germany.

Daimler Buses Solutions, set up in 2023, has already completed around 70 e-infrastructure projects. The new investment will further expand this unit. Oberwörder stated: "Buses only earn money when they are in operation. The same applies to charging stations: if they fail, operations come to a standstill. Daimler Buses is therefore becoming a provider of service ecosystems."

**Roadmap:** In Europe, Daimler Buses aims to sell only locally CO₂-neutral new vehicles by 2039 (city buses by 2030). The Mercedes-Benz eCitaro has been on the market since 2018. The eIntouro intercity bus launches in H2 2026. A battery-electric coach is planned by end of the decade.

**eCoach update:** A technology demonstrator ('eCoach') based on a Setra 516 HD has been converted to electric under the federally funded ELCH project and will soon begin test drives in Neu-Ulm. Daimler expects initial range similar to the eIntouro (~500 km). The eCoach leverages synergies with Daimler Truck's electric platform and CATL batteries. Daimler is prioritising battery-electric over fuel-cell for coaches (with fuel-cell arriving "no earlier than the beginning of the next decade").

Daimler Buses is also entering the public charging market, planning charging stations at tourist locations and transport hubs (including Munich and Cologne)—a first for the manufacturer, which has previously focused only on private/semi-public facilities.
