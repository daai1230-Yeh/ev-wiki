# Milano Design Week hosts debut of Lazzerini's 100% recycled plastic seat (developed with Nextchem)

**Author**: Editorial Staff | **Date**: 20 April 2026

---

A seat made entirely from recycled plastic is being presented by public transport seat producer Lazzerini at Milano Design Week 2026 as part of an industrial collaboration with Nextchem, an Italian technology company within the Maire Group specializing in chemical processes for circular economy and low-carbon solutions.

The installation is open from April 20 to April 26, 2026, with scheduled public access throughout the Milano Design Week program.

Lazzerini is showcasing the Re-Compact seat within "The New State of Materials" exhibition curated by Materially, held at Stecca3 in Milan's Isola district. The product is manufactured using MyReplast polypropylene, a recycled polymer produced by Nextchem's subsidiary MyReplast Industries at its plant in Bedizzole, northern Italy.

The Re-Compact seat uses no virgin plastic. According to Lazzerini, it is the first transit seat to be made entirely from mechanically recycled polypropylene while meeting EN 45545-2 fire safety certification standards for railway rolling stock. The fire safety compliance was achieved without the use of halogenated flame retardants, which are commonly used in transport-grade plastics but are increasingly restricted under environmental regulations.

Nextchem's MyReplast technology sorts and reprocesses post-consumer and post-industrial plastic waste into polymer granules with controlled mechanical and aesthetic properties. The company states that its process can handle contaminated and mixed-origin feedstock that would otherwise be directed to incineration or landfill. MyReplast Industries currently operates at a capacity of approximately 40,000 tonnes of recycled polymer per year.

Lazzerini, based in Località Terrarossa (Tuscany), has been producing seating systems for buses, coaches, and rail vehicles since 1954. The company supplies seats to major European OEMs including Iveco Bus, Solaris, and CaetanoBus. It recently introduced lightweight composite structures and modular configurations aimed at reducing vehicle weight and enabling faster maintenance turnaround for fleet operators.

The collaboration with Nextchem represents a shift in materials sourcing for Lazzerini, which previously relied on virgin polypropylene for its injection-moulded seat shells. The Re-Compact model is intended as a serial production option rather than a prototype, with Lazzerini confirming availability for OEM integration from Q3 2026.
