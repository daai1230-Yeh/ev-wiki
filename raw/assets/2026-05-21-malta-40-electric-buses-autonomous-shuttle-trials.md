# Malta adds 40 electric buses and launches autonomous shuttle trials

**Author**: Carla Westerheide | **Date**: 2026-05-20

---

Malta Public Transport will add **40 electric buses** to its fleet under a **€14 million** investment programme. Simultaneously, the country will kick off autonomous electric shuttle trials next month as part of an EU co-financed pilot project.

## New Electric Buses

- Manufacturer: **King Long** (Chinese manufacturer, images confirm though not officially named)
- Likely 12-metre electric buses — same model added to fleet in 2023
- Supports Malta's goal of a **fully electric public transport fleet on Gozo**
- Fleet will eventually more than double in size

Transport Minister Chris Bonett: "This marks another significant step towards cleaner and more technologically advanced public transport."

Background: In 2023, Malta already put 30 electric buses and a charging depot with 3 MW total output into operation. This new batch continues the Malta in Motion strategy.

## Autonomous Electric Shuttle Trials (June 2026)

- **Capacity**: up to 15 passengers
- Routes: pre-approved routes in Malta and Gozo
- Initially under controlled testing conditions
- A trained safety operator on board throughout testing

Project partners: Malta Public Transport, Ministry for Transport, University of Malta — under the EU-funded **metaCCAZE programme** (Horizon Europe). A new regulatory and safety framework has been issued by Transport Malta with continuous monitoring and data collection.
