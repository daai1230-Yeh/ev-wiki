# Mahindra to expand passenger EV line-up, ramp up production capacity

**Author**: Sagar Parikh | **Date**: May 12, 2026

---

Indian automaker Mahindra has announced plans to launch six new passenger EVs over the next five years. The company will also increase its production capacity by 50 per cent to support the expanded line-up.

In May 2024, Mahindra announced it would launch six EVs by 2030. During the Q4 FY2026 earnings call on May 5, CEO Rajesh Jejurikar said it has introduced four so far, with three launches still pending. The company is now expanding the plan with three additional EVs, aiming to launch all six remaining models by March 2031.

## Key New Model: BO7

Jejurikar revealed plans for a new electric model codenamed 'BO7' in 2027, describing it as "a very big volume driver" on top of the current Electric Origin products. Mahindra has since begun testing the BO7 on public roads, inadvertently revealing it to be the production version of the BE.07 concept (August 2022). The production version competes with Tata Motors' Harrier.ev.

Key dimensions from the BE.07 concept: 4.565 m length, 1.9 m width, 1.66 m height, 2.775 m wheelbase.

Notably, Mahindra changed the platform strategy: "We're not doing more of the only-EV (platform), because we've found a way to now do a born EV or a grounds-up EV on the NU_IQ platform, while still doing ICE (vehicles) on the same platform."

## Future Pipeline

- **2027**: BO7 production SUV and Vision.T concept (electric off-road alternative to Thar Roxx)
- **2028**: Possible SUV coupé derived from BO7, hinting at the BE.09 concept

## Production Capacity

Mahindra currently can produce up to 96,000 EVs annually. The company plans to expand to **144,000 units** annually by March 2027, a 50% increase to prepare for the April 2027–March 2028 launch wave.

## Current EV Lineup and Sales

Mahindra currently offers five EVs in India: XUV 3XO EV, XUV400, BE 6, XEV 9e, and XEV 9S. Between March 20, 2025 and April 24, 2026, the company sold **55,000 EVs**, with most volume coming from the BE 6, XEV 9e, and XEV 9S (the INGLO-platform 'Electric Origin' series).
