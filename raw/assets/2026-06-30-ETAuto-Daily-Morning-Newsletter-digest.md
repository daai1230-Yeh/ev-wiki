## Top Stories

### 1. Delhi EV Policy 2.0: Govt doubles down on BEVs, hybrids left out

The Delhi Cabinet has greenlit **EV Policy 2.0** with a **₹15,000 crore investment** over four years (effective July 1, 2026 – March 31, 2030). Chief Minister Rekha Gupta announced the policy excludes strong hybrid vehicles despite the April 2026 draft having proposed incentives for them.

Key provisions:
- 100% exemption from road tax and registration charges for electric cars priced up to ₹30 lakh
- Benefit of up to ₹1 lakh on scrapping BS4 or older vehicles
- No ICE two-wheelers to be registered in Delhi from **April 1, 2028**
- Focus exclusively on BEVs (no incentives for hybrids)

### 2. Tata Motors-Iveco revenue to be $35-40 billion in 5 years: N Chandrasekaran

Tata Motors Chairman **N Chandrasekaran**, speaking at the company's AGM on June 29, outlined that the combined Tata Motors-Iveco business is expected to grow annual revenue from ~$25 billion currently to **$35-40 billion** over the next five years.

Tata Motors aims to emerge among the **world's top four commercial vehicle manufacturers** following its proposed acquisition of Italy's **Iveco Group**. The strategic decision is expected to finalize by September 2026, with acquisition completion targeted by Q2 FY27 (subject to regulatory approvals).

### 3. Carmakers pare discounts as GST cut revives small-car demand

Maruti Suzuki and Tata Motors have significantly lowered or removed incentives on popular models like the Wagon R as GST cuts revive small-car demand in India.
