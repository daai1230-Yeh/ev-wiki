# Ford to produce first LFP cells this year – thanks to CATL

**Author**: Cora Werwitzke | **Date**: 18.06.2026

---

Ford is on schedule to begin series production of LFP battery cells at its Marshall, Michigan plant this year. 'D-Sample' cells are already being produced using mass-manufacturing-ready materials, undergoing final tests and validations.

The cells are prismatic LFP batteries destined for Ford's new affordable electric pickup (Universal EV Platform, launching 2027). Ford expects market-ready cells before end of 2026.

Ford licensed the LFP technology from CATL, giving it more control than a joint venture. The plant ('BlueOval Battery Park, Michigan') is fully Ford-owned, but CATL employees have provided training and manufacturing expertise during ramp-up. Announced in 2023, the target was 2,500 workers and 35 GWh capacity at full expansion — Ford now plans 800 workers by end 2026, scaling to 1,700.

This reflects Ford's strategic shift from NCM to LFP/LMR chemistries. LFP cells are cheaper, materials more available, and more durable than NCM, though NCM offers higher energy density and better cold-weather performance. Ford dissolved its BlueOval SK joint venture with SK On in December — SK On focuses on NMC, while Ford pursues LFP/LMR diversification.
