# Tesla Is Now Testing A Virtual Waitlist For Superchargers

**Author**: Rob Stumpf | **Date**: May 14, 2026

---

Tesla is looking to solve charger congestion by adding a digital waitlist to Superchargers.

Tesla announced it has begun piloting a new "waitlist" feature that allows cars to line up for their position while navigating to a charger. Cars are added to a queue as they approach, so there's no confusion about who's next to charge. The feature starts at just five sites across the U.S. (four in California and one in New York), but could grow if it proves successful.

Based on a video shared by Tesla on X, the in-car navigation shows the estimated wait time as the driver approaches the station, along with the number of cars ahead in the queue.

Tesla's senior director of charging, Max de Zegher, first mentioned testing this feature almost a year ago. He recently noted that Tesla has had success reducing wait times through both hardware improvements (rapid charger deployment, more modern equipment) and software solutions like Tesla's Trip Planner. The virtual queue removes self-regulation of charging lines and adds guardrails.

The Tesla app also supports adding charging status to live notifications on a driver's phone.

One current limitation: in the beta phase, the app warns drivers if they're cutting ahead ("There is a waitlist to charge. Are you sure you want to start a charging session now?") but there's no logic yet to actually prevent them from beginning a session. It's also unclear how non-Tesla vehicles approaching a charger would be aware of the queue without relying on their phones.

Tesla plans to improve and iterate based on feedback, with wider rollout if the feature is successful.
