# The Xiaomi SU7 Made Me Understand How Xiaomi Conquered China

**Author**: Kevin Williams | **Date**: 2026-06-15

---

The updated Xiaomi SU7 Sedan is great, and the way it got here should strike fear in the hearts of every global auto manufacturer.

## The Factory Visit

Xiaomi's phone factory near Beijing is 83% automated — one phone churned out every 6 seconds with minimal human workers. The same philosophy applies to its car business: the vehicle body shop is 91% automated with only 100 of ~2,000 total employees working there. At full capacity, Xiaomi produces one car every 76 seconds. This self-developed, highly automated approach mirrors how it builds phones, and explains how Xiaomi has become such a threat.

## The Updated SU7 (2026)

The 2026 SU7 is technically a facelift but with meaningful upgrades: staggered tires (wider rear), stiffer structure, CDC adjustable air suspension on all but base trim, LIDAR standard on all trims, 800V+ architecture across the board. It charges faster and goes farther than the original.

**Top-spec SU7 Max specs**: 680 hp dual motor, 101.7 kWh NMC battery, 897V architecture, 530 kW max charging, 0-60 mph in 3.1 seconds, starting ~$50,000 in China.

## Driving Impressions

The SU7 is remarkably easy to drive hard — neutral handling, stiff chassis, grips well on Michelin Pilot Sport tires. On public roads, the ride is exceptionally comfortable with pedal calibration that won't upset passengers. Interior quality is decidedly premium with a screen-first interface that's sharper and faster than most competitors, plus wireless Apple CarPlay. The only criticism: steering is light and suspension slightly too soft in default modes — easily fixed via software.

## Why It Matters

Ford CEO Jim Farley famously said he "didn't want to give it back" after driving one for competitive analysis. Xiaomi brings its "is it cool enough?" product philosophy directly from phones to cars — cleverness-on-a-budget that appeals to younger, tech-savvy consumers. Xiaomi is already the third largest phone maker in Europe (behind Apple and Samsung). Its European R&D center is now open and poaching talent from BMW and Lamborghini. A Europe launch appears inevitable.
