# A first for South Korea: Hyundai trials battery subscription model

**Author**: Cora Werwitzke | **Date**: 2026-04-29

---

Hyundai is launching a pilot project in South Korea where EV batteries are used on a subscription basis — separating battery ownership from vehicle ownership for the first time in Korean history.

## Programme Details

- **Pilot scope**: 5 Ioniq 5 fleet taxis in the greater Seoul area
- **Operator model**: Monthly subscription fee to Hyundai Capital; expired batteries are exchanged for replacements owned by Hyundai Capital
- **Expansion plan**: Taxis first, then private consumers in H2 2026
- **Partner**: Hyundai Motor + Hyundai Capital (in-house auto finance arm)

## Why Taxis First?

Taxi fleets run high mileage, which accelerates battery wear — making them ideal for testing subscription economics and real-world battery replacement cycles.

## Regulatory Breakthrough

A special regulation approved in November 2025 by South Korea's Ministry of Land, Infrastructure and Transport permits — for the first time — registration of EV bodies and batteries under **separate ownership**. Previously impossible under Korean vehicle law, this barrier had suppressed EV adoption (battery degradation reduces resale value).

## Related Technology

Hyundai is also testing traditional **battery-swapping** (empty→fully-charged in 10 minutes) via startup **Pit In** (Hyundai Group spin-off), operating since September 2025 in Anyang, Gyeonggi Province. Currently ~30 taxis from 4 companies use this system.

## Strategic Goal

Hyundai ultimately aims to expand battery subscription and financing products to the broader consumer market — addressing high upfront EV costs and battery lifecycle concerns.
