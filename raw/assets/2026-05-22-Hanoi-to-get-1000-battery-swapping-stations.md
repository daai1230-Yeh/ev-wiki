# Hanoi to get 1,000 battery-swapping stations

**Author**: Carla Westerheide | **Date**: 2026-05-21

---

The city of Hanoi has partnered with LG Energy Solution and Honda to develop a large-scale battery-swapping network for electric motorcycles in the Vietnamese capital. The partners plan to launch 50 pilot swapping stations in 2026 before expanding to around 1,000 locations across Hanoi from 2027.

## Timeline

- **Q3 2026**: Pilot phase — ~50 battery-swapping stations + ~500 electric motorcycles in central Hanoi, particularly around Hoan Kiem ward (where low-emission regulations will first be introduced)
- **Q3 2027 onward**: Network expansion to 1,000 battery-swapping stations across all of Hanoi

## Partner Roles

| Partner | Role |
|---------|------|
| **LG Energy Solution** | Supplies 2170 cylindrical battery cells; oversees battery safety management, swapping station operations, and battery lifecycle systems |
| **Honda** | Provides electric motorcycles, battery packs, and swapping equipment |
| **Hanoi City** | Supports licensing procedures, local coordination, and policy implementation |

## Market Context

Vietnam remains one of the world's largest motorcycle markets. In 2025, the country had ~80 million motorcycles in circulation, including ~3.2 million electric models. Hanoi alone has ~8 million vehicles on its roads. Hanoi also plans to introduce a pilot low-emission zone within Ring Road 1 from 1 July 2026.
