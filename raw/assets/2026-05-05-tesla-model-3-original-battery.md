# This 380,000-Mile Tesla Model 3 Has Its Original Battery. Range Loss Is Huge

**Author**: Andrei Nedelea | **Date**: May 3, 2026

---

A high-mileage 2019 Model 3 Standard Range Plus drives just fine, but it has lost around a third of its original battery capacity.

Key findings:
- This 380,000-mile Tesla Model 3 is still running on its original battery pack
- Displayed range has dropped by 34.2%, from 240 miles to 158 miles
- Despite major degradation, it still managed 138.3 highway miles at 68 mph

Battery degradation is an inevitable reality of EV ownership, but how much capacity it loses over time can vary wildly depending on how it was used. A 2019 Tesla Model 3 Standard Range with 380,000 miles (610,000 km) and its original battery is still usable.

Drive Protected from Canada bought this high-mileage Model 3, which is reportedly still sporting its original battery pack, to document its performance. They took the car in to charge up to 100%, and it showed a range of 158 miles (254 km)—down from the original 240 miles (386 km) when new, meaning it's 82 miles (132 km) down, or 34.2% less.

This is a battery that has less than 70% of its original capacity, so it is a prime candidate to be swapped out for a fresh unit. However, over 150 miles is still a decent number for someone with a short commute.

## Highway Range Test

They performed a highway range test, setting off with a fully charged battery, driving at 110 km/h (68 mph), with ambient temperature rising from 51.8°F (11°C) to 73.4°F (23°C) by the end of the test. It returned to the charger showing 0% left in the battery after covering 138.3 miles (222.6 km), with an average consumption of 4.27 miles/kWh (14.55 kWh/100 km).

The car used 32.4 kilowatt-hours for the trip, considerably less than the 49 kWh it should have had originally. But the number is consistent with the initial range prediction, and it's still a good result given that it was almost all highway driving.

This is a useful reminder that the problem of battery degradation is not a simple one. This Model 3 has clearly lost significant capacity, but after mileage that would send many gas cars into retirement, it still behaves like a usable car rather than a rolling advertisement about range loss.
