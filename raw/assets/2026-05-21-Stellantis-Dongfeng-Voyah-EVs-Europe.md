# Jeep And Ram's Owner Partners With Another Chinese Brand To Build EVs In Europe

**Author**: Andrei Nedelea | **Date**: 2026-05-20

---

Stellantis — owner of Jeep, Ram and a dozen other brands — has announced a joint-venture deal with China's Dongfeng to assemble Voyah EVs at a European plant, extending the same localization playbook it already uses with Leapmotor in Spain.

Key points from the article:

- The Dongfeng deal centers on Stellantis' plant in Rennes, France, which can hold up to three production lines and once peaked at 400,000 vehicles a year, but now runs at about a third of capacity building only the Citroën C5 Aircross. Under the agreement, Dongfeng will also build Peugeot and Jeep vehicles in China.
- The driver is tariff avoidance: Chinese-made EVs face EU duties of up to 35% on top of the standard 10% import tariff, so local assembly is more economical than exporting finished cars.
- Stellantis already knows the model through Leapmotor (in which it holds a controlling stake). Leapmotor initially built the T03 in Poland (production stopped last March) and now makes the B10 crossover at Stellantis' Zaragoza plant in Spain. Leapmotor is reportedly seeking to expand use of Stellantis' idle European capacity and will supply a platform and key parts for a new Opel electric crossover.
- Other Chinese automakers are localizing too: BYD's large plant in Hungary, Chery partnering with Ebro at a former Nissan plant in Barcelona, and Xpeng and GAC working with Magna Steyr in Austria.
- The article's takeaway: EU tariffs intended to protect domestic makers may instead accelerate Chinese expansion via European factories — with the next wave of affordable EVs designed by Chinese engineers but built locally, sometimes under familiar Western badges.
