# SVOLT to start semi-solid-state battery production in September

**Author**: Cora Werwitzke | **Date**: 2026-05-20

---

SVOLT plans to start series production of semi-solid-state batteries in September 2026. CEO Yang Hongxin said fully solid-state batteries remain far from commercialisation, positioning the semi-solid-state path as the dominant technology for the near term.

SVOLT Chairman Yang Hongxin announced the production timeline via Weibo. Series production of semi-solid-state batteries will start in September, followed shortly by integration into multiple vehicle models. The 100 kWh battery version will also achieve large-scale mass production at launch. Production costs are expected to match those of conventional lithium-ion batteries with liquid electrolytes.

**What are semi-solid-state batteries?** They use a combination of liquid and solid materials in the electrolyte (solid-liquid hybrid), positioned between traditional lithium-ion batteries and fully solid-state batteries. They promise improved safety, higher energy density, and longer lifespan.

SVOLT, currently China's tenth-largest battery manufacturer, has accelerated its timeline: in mid-2025 the plan was trial production in Q4 2025 and series production in 2027, but the launch has now been brought forward to Q3 2026. The first generation targets 300 Wh/kg energy density; a second generation in development targets 360 Wh/kg.

SVOLT is reportedly set to supply semi-solid-state batteries for the next generation of Mini brand models (via Great Wall Motor's joint venture with BMW). However, Mini has not been officially confirmed as a recipient.

On fully solid-state batteries, Yang Hongxin emphasised that commercialisation will require "a very long-term effort" and expects semi-solid-state batteries to be the dominant path until solid-state achieves large-scale production.
