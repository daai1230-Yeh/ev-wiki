# EIB loans billion-euro sum for Hamburg's distribution grid and electric buses

**Author**: Cora Werwitzke | **Date**: 2026-05-20

---

The European Investment Bank (EIB) is providing Hamburger Energienetze (HNE) with up to €1 billion for the modernisation of Hamburg's electricity distribution grid. Verkehrsbetriebe Hamburg-Holstein (VHH) will also receive approximately €125 million for 188 new battery-electric buses and depot expansion.

**Electric bus fleet expansion:** Currently around one-third of VHH's ~800-vehicle fleet operates electrically. Since 2020, the company has exclusively purchased zero-emission buses. The 188 new BEVs break down as: 120 standard buses, 52 articulated buses, and 16 minibuses.

**Grid modernisation (HNE):** The EIB loan of ~€1 billion covers approximately 28% of HNE's total €2.9 billion grid investment plan through 2029. Programme includes: ~1,500 km of new/renewed cables (low, medium, high voltage); new and renewed substations; 120 new switchgear bays; and grid digitalisation for renewable integration. By 2040, Hamburg's grid peak load is projected to at least double.

**VHH investment:** Total planned electromobility investment of up to €337 million, with EIB contributing €125 million (~37%). The Schenefeld depot will be redeveloped and expanded to support the growing e-bus fleet.

**Integrated approach:** Both HNE and VHH are subsidiaries of HGV (Hamburg's municipal holding company), which coordinates the dual infrastructure investment — recognising that e-bus fleet expansion is only possible if the power grid can handle growing charging demand. Loan agreements were signed on 19 May 2026; first tranche was already disbursed in October 2025.
