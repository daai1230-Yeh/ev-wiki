# Stellantis rules out alternative powertrains for small-car platform

**Author**: Florian Treiss | **Date**: 2026-06-19

---

Stellantis is departing from its multi-energy strategy for its upcoming range of small vehicles inspired by the revival of Citroën's iconic 2CV. Within this new model family, known as the E-Car project, Stellantis plans to offer exclusively battery-electric vehicles.

While Stellantis CEO Antonio Filosa has emphasised the company's commitment to "freedom of choice" with hybrid and ICE options, an exception is being made for small cars targeted at the European market. Emanuele Cappellano, Head of Stellantis Enlarged Europe, confirmed the plans at the Automotive News Europe Congress in Brussels: the upcoming small-car family will be offered solely with electric drivetrains.

Stellantis officially confirmed its plans for a new electric 2CV in May. "Just like the original once democratized mobility, the new 2 CV will re-enchant electric mobility for a new generation," said Citroën CEO Xavier Chardon.

The decision is likely driven by several factors. The technology required to make a new small ICE car compliant with future emission regulations is simply too expensive—the same reason VW is phasing out the ICE Polo and replacing it with the fully electric ID. Polo.

Additionally, the European Commission plans to introduce a new vehicle class called M1E for small electric cars under 4.20 metres in length. This class will be weighted with a factor of 1.3 as a 'super credit' when calculating manufacturers' CO₂ fleet emissions, providing strong incentive to bring small EVs to market.
