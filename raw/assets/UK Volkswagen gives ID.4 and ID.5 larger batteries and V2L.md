# UK: Volkswagen gives ID.4 and ID.5 larger batteries and V2L

**Author**: Carla Westerheide | **Date**: 11.04.2026 - 11:00

---

Volkswagen has introduced technical updates for the ID.4 and ID.5 in the UK, including higher battery capacities, improved efficiency and vehicle-to-load capability. The changes also bring longer ranges and enhanced equipment across multiple trims.

Selected variants of both models are now equipped with batteries offering higher net capacity, improved performance and extended range. For example, the ID.4 Pro 4MOTION variants now deliver up to 220 kW and a WLTP range of up to 341 miles.

For the ID.4 and ID.5 Pure variants, battery capacity has been increased from 52 kWh to 58 kWh. Other trims now offer 79 kWh instead of the previous 77 kWh to 79 kWh. In the case of the former, the upgrade resulted in an increased range of 40 kilometres.

In the GTX variants, Volkswagen has reduced net battery capacity from 79 kWh to 77 kWh but increased system output from 250 kW to 286 kW. DC charging speeds remain unchanged at 175 kW for the ID.4 GTX and 185 kW for the ID.5 GTX.

Vehicle-to-load (V2L) has been introduced across the ID.4 and ID.5 lineups, enabling up to 3.6 kW of power output through an external socket.

Further changes across the ID.4 and ID.5 include revised steering wheel controls with physical buttons, enhancements to driver assistance systems and updates to infotainment. Traffic Assist has been extended to include Front Cross functionality, while driver attention monitoring and the One Infotainment system have been improved, VW states. Additional efficiency measures include an updated optional heat pump.

For ID.4 Pro 4MOTION variants, the maximum braked towing capacity has been raised from 1,200 kg to 1,800 kg.

Equipment levels have also been adjusted. Entry-level trims now feature larger 19-inch steel wheels, while mid-range versions gain an electric tailgate as standard. An optional 230-volt socket is available across both model lines.
