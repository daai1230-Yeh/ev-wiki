# BYD Surpasses Tesla as World's Top Energy Storage Deployer in 2025

**Author**: 綜合報導 | **Date**: May 13, 2026

---

BYD has overtaken Tesla to capture 13% of the global energy storage market in 2025, compared to Tesla's 10%, according to new data from Benchmark Mineral Intelligence. This marks the end of Tesla's reign as the top energy storage deployer—a position it held in 2023 and 2024.

## Key Performance Metrics

BYD shipped over **60 GWh** of energy storage systems (BESS) globally in 2025, ranking first among all system integrators. Tesla deployed **46.7 GWh** during the same period—a 49% year-over-year increase that was still not enough to keep pace with BYD's aggressive expansion.

Global BESS installations jumped **51% in 2025** to approximately 315 GWh, while cell shipments for stationary storage nearly doubled to over 600 GWh, according to Benchmark and InfoLink data. Eight of the top 10 BESS integrators are Chinese.

## Market Context

China's domestic deployment alone dwarfs the rest of the world combined. BYD's rise in energy storage mirrors its trajectory in electric vehicles—vertical integration and scale giving it a decisive cost and volume advantage.

The shift is significant: Tesla's Megapack product line had dominated the utility-scale storage market, but BYD's FinDreams Energy unit has aggressively expanded globally, including into Europe and the Americas.

## Chinese Dominance in BESS

The broader market data reinforces Chinese manufacturers' dominance: 8 of the top 10 BESS integrators globally are now Chinese companies, led by BYD, CATL, and other domestic players. This parallels similar consolidation seen in EV manufacturing.
