# electrive TODAY — 29 May 2026

**Source**: electrive (newsletter@electrive.com)

---

## Exclusive
- **Cupra Raval VZ** — first test drive around Barcelona. Positioned as the sporty counterpart to the more pragmatic VW ID. Polo, the compact EV impressed with sharp handling and composure through corners, though the sporty setup has drawbacks.

## News
- **Netherlands to introduce malus on combustion company cars** — from 1 January 2027, a surcharge on new lease contracts for combustion and hybrid company cars to accelerate the shift to BEVs.
- **France backs UK inclusion in 'Made in EU' rules** — Paris signals support for including the UK under the Industrial Accelerator Act, a shift from its previous backing of strict rules of origin.
- **Mercedes-AMG opens orders for electric GT 4-Door Coupé** — in Germany: GT 55 from €154,700, GT 63 from €196,350.
- **Nio launches flagship ES9 SUV in China** — Executive SUV priced from ~€49,500 without battery, ~€63,000 with the battery pack.
- **BMW recalls new iX3 over electric shock risk** — a fault could expose users to electric shock when touching the vehicle during charging; currently affects only a few vehicles.

## Number of the Day
- 223,980 electric passenger cars newly registered in Germany Jan–Apr 2026 — a 23.6% share of total registrations, up 41.3% year-on-year (kba.de).

## Technology
- **CATL launches battery swap network for light trucks** — with logistics provider DST; 31 stations in the Greater Bay Area, growing to 140 sites by end of 2026.
- **ProLogium eyes SPAC deal for Dunkirk plant** — Taiwanese battery maker to merge with shell company TDAC to fund its solid-state battery factory in Dunkirk, France.
- **LGES sells JV assets in Ohio** — LG Energy Solution sold real estate tied to its US battery JV with Honda for 3.74 trillion won ($2.48 billion); excludes land and production equipment.
- **Kessels to head R&D at Seat and Cupra** — Joost Kessels from 1 August 2026; Dr Werner Tietz to focus on his VW Group role.

## Quotes of the Day
- BYD senior executive Stella Li: rising global oil prices are a "wake-up call" bringing hesitant buyers into EV stores.
- BVRLA CEO Toby Poston: if fuel taxes are eased during high petrol prices, the government shouldn't add new charges on EVs.

## Fleets + Infrastructure
- **Scania demos 750 kW bidirectional truck charging** — calls it one of the world's first bidirectional MCS power transfers for heavy-duty trucks.
- **Manchester orders 125 more electric buses** — TfGM places another Wrightbus order for the Bee Network, targeting a fully zero-emission bus fleet by 2030.
- **UTA Edenred adds Ionity charging tariffs** — reduced fleet pricing at 6,500 fast-charging points across Europe; Ionity becomes its third major network partner after Tesla and EnBW.
- **PositivEnergy expands charging in Philadelphia** — installing ~435 public charging points (DC fast + Level 2) with the City of Philadelphia.

## Service / Short Circuit
- Reading tip: Latin America and the Caribbean now have over 10,000 electric buses, largely from Chinese manufacturers, with strong growth in megacities.
- Internet mocks Ferrari's electric Luce over its Jony Ive–shaped styling.
