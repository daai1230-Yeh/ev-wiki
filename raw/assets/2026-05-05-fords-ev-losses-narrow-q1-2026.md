# Ford's EV losses narrow to $777M in Q1 2026 amid Model e profitability efforts

**Author**: Florian Treiss | **Date**: April 30, 2026

---

Ford slightly reduced losses in its EV division in Q1 2026, but its EV business remains firmly in the red. The company's Model e unit reported a loss of $777 million — an improvement of $72 million compared to the same period last year.

Ford's Model e division covers battery-electric passenger cars such as the Puma Gen-E, the Explorer produced in Cologne, and the Mustang Mach-E. It does not include electric vans like the E-Transit (Ford Pro division).

The passenger EV segment has faced significant challenges in the US, particularly after the Trump administration withdrew the $7,500 tax credit for EV purchases as of 30 September 2025. In response, Ford discontinued its electric pickup F-150 Lightning last autumn. Additionally, Ford announced write-downs totalling $19.5 billion in December, primarily in its EV business.

## Q1 2026 Results

- **Revenue**: $1.2 billion (roughly same as prior year)
- **EV sales**: ~34,000 units (up from ~31,000 in Q1 2025)
- **EBIT loss**: -$777 million (improved from -$849 million in Q1 2025)
- **EBIT margin**: -63.1% (improved by 5.3 percentage points)
- **Loss per vehicle**: approximately -$23,000 per EV sold

Ford generated the same revenue with 10% more BEVs than a year earlier, suggesting average selling prices have declined, potentially due to a higher proportion of lower-priced European models.

## Outlook

For the full year, Ford expects an EBIT loss of $4.0 to $4.5 billion for Model e — an improvement over its $4.8 billion loss in 2025. Ford aims to improve margins on its first-generation EVs while preparing for more affordable, scalable electric vehicles on the Ford Universal EV Platform (UEV), announced last August.

Ford's overall Q1 results: Total revenue of $43.3 billion, net profit of $2.5 billion, and adjusted EBIT of $3.5 billion. Ford Blue (ICE) sold 584,000 vehicles; Ford Pro reached 316,000 vehicles.
