行密集遊說之際，此外，白宮尚未正式發布相關關稅文

件。

(三)共和黨參議員Bernie Moreno(R-OH)表示，政府正考慮調

整現有「生產抵減額度制度」（production offset 

credit），以協助汽車製造商減輕關稅衝擊；該制度允

許製造商依據其在美國生產車輛的價值，獲得抵減額，

以抵銷因232條款所衍生的進口零配件關稅負擔。

(四)目前制度設計為：抵減額度以車輛建議售價的3.75%為基

準，於2026年4月30日後降至2.5%，並於一年後取消。

Moreno參議員及業界人士透露，白宮正考慮將3.75%的比

率維持5年，並擴大適用範圍至引擎製造。

(五)白宮官員受訪時表示，川普政府致力於採取「多層面且

策略性」的方式，確保美國國內汽車與零配件產業的生

產力，並指出，在總統正式簽署官方文件前，相關政策

方向的討論仍屬推測階段。

Trump punts Section 232 truck tariffs to Nov. 1

By Margaret Spiegelman (/authors/Margaret-Spiegelman)  / October 6, 2025 at 4:16 PM

Post

Share

New U.S. national security duties on medium and heavy-duty trucks will go into effect in early
November, President Trump said on Monday, pushing back a previously announced start date amid
reporting of a last-minute lobbying push by automakers.

The administration also is considering expanding an existing production offset credit to provide
greater tariff relief for automakers, according to a report.

“Beginning November 1st, 2025, all Medium and Heavy Duty Trucks coming into the United States
from other Countries will be Tariffed at the Rate of 25%,” the president said in a social media post
(https://truthsocial.com/@realDonaldTrump/posts/115328463321928109).

Medium and heavy-duty trucks, along with parts for such trucks, are the subject of a Section 232 (of
the Trade Expansion Act of 1962) probe launched by the Commerce Department in April, one of a
slew of such investigations that the administration has indicated could lead to tariffs. In late
September, the president said he would impose (/node/184357) 25 percent tariffs on heavy-duty
trucks beginning Oct. 1, but the White House has yet to formally issue any such duties.

The plan has spurred some jostling among the big U.S. automakers, including over how the tariffs
could apply to vehicles assembled in Mexico, according to an Oct. 3 report
(https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-10-03/detroit-carmakers-feud-over-trump-
s-threatened-truck-tariffs?embedded-checkout=true) by Bloomberg.

The administration has not indicated whether it will provide any special treatment for trucks or
parts imported from Mexico and Canada, as it has for autos and auto parts (/node/182703)
subject to separate Section 232 duties imposed by Trump earlier this year.

Sen. Bernie Moreno (R-OH), meanwhile, told Reuters (https://www.reuters.com/business/autos-
transportation/trump-considering-significant-tariff-relief-us-vehicle-production-senator-
says-2025-10-03/) late last week that the administration was considering revising a system that

allows manufacturers to earn offsets against the Section 232 tariffs on auto parts based on the value

of vehicles they build in the U.S.

As it stands, the offset value begins at 3.75 percent of the manufacturer’s suggested retail price,
drops to 2.5 percent after April 30, 2026, and expires entirely a year after that.

Moreno and industry officials told Reuters the president is considering keeping the offset value at
3.75 for five years and expanding it to include engine production.

A White House official told the news service that the president and his administration were
“committed to a nuanced and multi-faceted approach to securing domestic auto and auto parts
production. Until any official action is signed by the president, however, any discussion about
administration policymaking is speculative.” -- Margaret Spiegelman
