# Stellantis to support Voyah expansion in Europe

**Author**: Florian Treiss | **Date**: 2026-05-20

---

Stellantis and Dongfeng have unveiled plans for an intensive partnership in Europe, centred on Dongfeng's Voyah brand. Dongfeng vehicles will also be manufactured at Stellantis' plant in Rennes, France in the future.

Building on their 34-year partnership in China, the two automakers plan to establish a joint venture for the European market to manage sales, distribution, production, and engineering of Dongfeng's new energy vehicles in Europe — covering BEVs, PHEVs, and range-extender vehicles.

**Deal structure:** Stellantis holds a 51% stake in the joint venture, Dongfeng 49% — mirroring the Leapmotor arrangement. Initially the JV will focus on distributing Voyah brand NEVs in select European markets using Stellantis's dealership network and after-sales expertise.

**Manufacturing:** Like Leapmotor in Spain, Dongfeng plans to have some vehicles built at Stellantis' Rennes plant in France (currently home to the Citroën C5 Aircross and Peugeot 5008). A "Made in Europe" label would help avoid EU import tariffs and qualify for EV incentive programmes.

**Background:** Dongfeng had long planned a 2024 European launch in Germany, France, Italy, Portugal, and Spain, but these plans were derailed by EU tariffs on Chinese EVs. Currently Voyah is only officially available in EU countries via select CEE markets (Poland, Czechia, Slovakia, Hungary, Slovenia, Bulgaria) and non-EU Norway and Switzerland. The Stellantis partnership is expected to significantly accelerate pan-European expansion.
