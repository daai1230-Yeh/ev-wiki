# CATL renews its battery line-up

**Author**: Cora Werwitzke | **Date**: April 22, 2026

---

CATL unveiled battery innovations and further improved existing batteries at its Super Technology Day in Beijing. These include the third generations of the Shenxing Superfast Charging Battery and the Qilin Battery, as well as new hybrid and sodium batteries and a Condensed Battery.

At its Super Technology Day, CATL announced the following innovations:

- Third-generation Shenxing superfast-charging battery
- Third-generation Qilin battery
- Second-generation Freevoy super hybrid battery
- Qilin Condensed Battery
- Naxtra sodium-ion battery
- Integrated solution for fast charging and battery swapping

**Third-Generation Shenxing Battery**: Focused on improving lifespan while maintaining ultra-fast charging. Charging rate of 10C (peak 15C). Charges from 10% to 80% in 3 minutes 44 seconds, and from 10% to 98% in 6 minutes 27 seconds. Even at −30°C, charges from 20% to 98% in about 9 minutes. After 1,000 complete cycles, capacity retention remains above 90%.

**Third-Generation Qilin Battery**: Cell-to-Pack technology achieving an energy density of 280 Wh/kg, designed for premium EVs with long ranges. Enables 1,000 km range while supporting 10C fast charging and delivering a peak power output of 3 MW. The full battery pack weighs just 625 kg — 255 kg lighter and 112 litres smaller than equivalent LFP systems.

**Qilin Condensed Battery**: Transfers aviation technology to passenger cars, achieving a cell energy density of 350 Wh/kg and volumetric energy density of 760 Wh/L — "a new record for mass-produced batteries." Expected to enable ranges of 1,500 km for saloons and over 1,000 km for large SUVs. Uses an aerospace-grade titanium alloy casing and eliminates liquid electrolyte, removing fire and leakage risks.

**Second-Generation Freevoy Super Hybrid Battery**: Designed for PHEVs/EREVs, enabling 600 km of pure electric range with 10C superfast charging. Achieves energy density of 230 Wh/kg using an LFP/NCM mixed chemistry.

**Naxtra Sodium-Ion Battery**: Announced entry into full-scale mass production by the end of 2026, with energy density up to 175 Wh/kg.

**Network expansion**: CATL plans to build 4,000 integrated charging and battery-swapping stations by end of 2026, covering nearly 190 cities. It also announced a "charge-swap sharing network" with partners including Changan, Chery, GAC, Seres, SAIC-GM-Wuling, and BAIC, aiming for over 100,000 shared energy supply facilities by end of 2028.

CATL Chairman Robin Zeng emphasized: "For Chinese technology to go global, it relies not just on speed and scale, but on the quality of innovation, the ability to validate, and the credibility of the brand."
