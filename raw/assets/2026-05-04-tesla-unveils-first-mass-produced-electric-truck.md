# Tesla unveils its first mass-produced electric truck

**Author**: Cora Werwitzke | **Date**: 2026-04-30

---

Tesla's first electric truck has rolled off the new high-volume production line at Gigafactory Nevada. The automaker released an image of the truck alongside the workforce in a new section of the plant — confirming that Semi series production has begun.

Tesla shared the image on its official Tesla Semi account on X, with the caption: 'First Semi off high-volume line'. This milestone marks the end of one of the longest development cycles in Tesla's history. First unveiled in 2017 and initially promised for the roads by 2019, the Semi is now expected to go on sale in 2026.

## Key Facts

- **Production location**: Purpose-built facility adjacent to Gigafactory Nevada
- **Planned capacity**: Up to 50,000 trucks/year (gradual ramp-up)
- **Two variants**: Standard Range (325 miles / ~523 km, $260,000) and Long Range (500 miles / ~800 km, $290,000)
- **Powertrain**: Three integrated electric motors, up to 800 kW
- **Charging**: Supports megawatt charging at 1.2 MW; first Megacharger operational in Ontario, California, with 66 more sites planned across 15 states
- **GVW**: 82,000 lbs (~37.2 tonnes)

The Semi would be the most affordable Class 8 battery-electric semi-trailer tractor on the US market at these prices. Pre-production units include ~36 vehicles in PepsiCo's California fleet.

## Market Demand

The 'California Clean Truck & Bus Voucher' programme shows strong demand: between January 2025 and February 2026, 965 of 1,067 total applications were for the Tesla Semi, versus fewer than 100 combined for Daimler, Paccar, and Volvo.
