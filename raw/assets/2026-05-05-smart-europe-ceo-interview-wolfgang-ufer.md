# smart returns to its roots — and scales up in Europe: Interview with CEO smart Europe Wolfgang Ufer

**Author**: Peter Schwierz | **Date**: May 1, 2026

---

Smart is expanding its electric portfolio — but the return of the two-seater could redefine the brand. In his first major interview as CEO of smart in Europe, Wolfgang Ufer (appointed March 1, 2026) outlines growth plans, market dynamics and why the upcoming #2 marks a turning point for smart.

## Key Points

**Brand identity**: Smart stands fully for electric vehicles — no plug-in hybrids planned for Europe. The portfolio spans from the compact #1 and #3, through to the larger #5 SUV introduced in 2025. The brand targets lifestyle premium vehicles with strong design in compact segments.

**The upcoming smart #2**: Described as a "volume model," the #2 (successor to the fortwo) will be a fully electric two-seater under approximately 3 metres in length. It reconnects the brand with its urban two-seater DNA while being reinterpreted for a new era. The vehicle is being tested on roads in China, using a body from the "453 series" previous generation for testing purposes. Launch expected from 2027 with strong sales potential. Ufer's mother (age 86) still drives a smart EQ fortwo and is already interested in the new #2.

**European growth**: Germany is showing the strongest momentum, driven by subsidies, rising fuel costs, and increasing EV visibility. Spain, France, and Norway (especially for the #5) are also growing strongly. Smart operates in 17 European markets. Q1 2026 results are ahead of target.

**Target customers**: Current portfolio (1, #3, #5) targets high-income customers aged 40-60 making a conscious EV choice. The #2 broadens this to 18-86 year olds, with applications ranging from car sharing and social care services to personal premium urban use.

**Market outlook**: Ufer is optimistic that 2026 marks the start of a new positive phase for EV adoption, driven by technology improvements (faster charging, longer range), macroeconomic factors (higher fuel prices), and improving public perception of EVs.

**Smart #5 performance**: The #5 achieved its highest order intake ever the month before this interview — still growing despite being on the market for months.

**Portfolio strategy**: Mercedes-Benz and Geely as shareholders shape the portfolio. All models require shareholder approval. Smart is closely aligned with Mercedes-Benz in dealerships and aftersales throughout Europe.
