# LGES secures major order for cylindrical cells – possibly from BMW

**Author**: Sebastian Schaal | **Date**: 2026-04-30

---

LG Energy Solution (LGES) has secured new orders exceeding 100 GWh for its large 46-series cylindrical cells in Q1 2026. According to Korean media reports, the orders come from a single customer: BMW.

## Key Details

- **New orders**: 100 GWh of 46-series cylindrical cells (46mm diameter)
- **Total order backlog**: Now exceeds 440 GWh
- **Likely customer**: BMW Group (per South Korean media Chosun and MK)
- **Contract value**: ~10 trillion Won (~€5.8 billion), 10-year term
- **Cell formats**: 4695 (for iX3, i3, i7) and 46120 (for larger vehicles like upcoming iX5)
- **Production**: Already producing 4695 cells at Ochang plant; Arizona (US) plant to produce diverse 46-Series cells by end of 2026

## Why BMW?

The 46-millimetre format with 95mm height (4695) matches BMW's Neue Klasse platform exactly. For larger vehicles with higher floors, BMW plans 46120-format cells (possibly iX5). The BMW X5 is made in Spartanburg, South Carolina — making LGES's Arizona plant a logical nearby supply source.

This would be the first time LGES supplies battery cells for BMW's battery-electric vehicles (BMW has previously used LG cells in hybrids).

## LGES Other 46mm Customers

LGES holds 46mm cylindrical cell contracts with: Rivian, Chery (China), and likely Mercedes-Benz (rumoured 50.5 GWh contract signed 2024, delivery from 2028).

## Q1 2026 Financials

- Revenue: 6.555 trillion Won (+1.2% YoY)
- Operating loss: 207.8 billion Won (down due to reduced pouch EV battery shipments from inventory adjustments by a major North American customer)
- Growth offset by stable cylindrical EV battery shipments and ESS demand
