# India moves past E20, notifies E30 petrol norms to cut oil dependence

**Author**: ETAuto | **Date**: 2026-05-20

---

India has notified new petrol standards for higher ethanol blends, signalling preparation for a future beyond E20. The Bureau of Indian Standards issued a Gazette notification on 18 May 2026 covering E22, E25, E27 and E30 grades — petrol blended with 22%, 25%, 27% and 30% ethanol respectively.

Key details:

- The norms take effect from 15 May 2026 and provide specifications to enable future fuel introduction.
- The aim is to reduce dependence on crude-oil imports, lower vehicular emissions and support the domestic sugar and biofuel industries.
- The notification does not mandate immediate nationwide sale of E30; it functions as a technical framework for higher-ethanol-compatible and flex-fuel vehicles.
- Industry bodies such as the All India Distillers Association welcomed it as a milestone for India's clean-fuel and ethanol-blending roadmap.
- The ETAuto newsletter notes the notified grades and an ARAI study for E25 may give the government room on the policy path.
