# Kia EV5 review: Not the fastest, but one of the most impressive

**Author**: Daniel Bönnighausen | **Date**: 2026-06-18

**Model tested**: Kia EV5 GT-Line | **Battery**: 81.4 kWh | **Range**: 503 km (WLTP) | **DC charging**: 150 kW | **Base price**: €51,990

---

With the EV5, Kia has launched an electric compact SUV that does not seek to stand out through headline-grabbing specifications. Instead, the 4.61-metre-long model focuses on the qualities that matter most in one of Europe's largest vehicle segments. Our test drive shows where the EV5 excels – and where Kia still has room for improvement.

The EV5 fills a key gap in Kia's line-up. While the carmaker has steadily expanded its electric vehicle portfolio, it has lacked a conventionally styled battery-electric SUV in the same segment as the Sportage. The EV5 now takes on that role, serving as the electric counterpart to one of Kia's most important models.

However, success is far from guaranteed. With models like the VW ID.4, Skoda Enyaq, Hyundai Ioniq 5, and Tesla Model Y, there are already well-established benchmarks. Kia is entering a market where many customers already have clear points of comparison.

## Driving Dynamics

The front-wheel-drive version produces 160 kW and accelerates from 0–100 km/h in 8.4 seconds, with a top speed of 165 km/h. The AWD variant accelerates in 7.3 seconds to 180 km/h, while the flagship EV5 GT produces 225 kW with a 0–100 km/h time of 6.2 seconds.

The suspension setup prioritises ride comfort over sporty handling. On rough surfaces, the rear axle transmits short, sharp impacts more directly to rear passengers. However, in normal driving conditions, these limitations are rarely noticeable. The EV5 is not designed for aggressive cornering but for smooth, steady progress.

## Regenerative Braking

Kia has implemented the regeneration system particularly well. Drivers can choose between several recuperation levels using the steering-wheel paddles, and the EV5 retains the last selected setting after the vehicle is switched off. The i-Pedal function enables true one-pedal driving and reliably brings the car to a complete standstill.

## Charging

The EV5 supports DC fast charging at up to 150 kW. Battery preconditioning activates automatically when a charging destination is entered in the Kia navigation system — the instrument cluster provides confirmation. However, when using Google Maps via Apple CarPlay, the Kia navigation disengages and automatic preconditioning is disabled, requiring manual activation.

Battery temperature is displayed only in three broad stages; a more detailed readout would offer greater transparency compared to some competitors.

## Interior & Cargo

The cockpit features a 12.3-inch driver display, a 12.3-inch central touchscreen, and a 5.3-inch climate control display with physical controls below. Boot volume is 566 litres, with a flat loading floor when rear seats are folded. The frunk can accommodate a Type 2 charging cable and V2L adapter.

## Verdict

The EV5 is a well-rounded electric compact SUV that prioritises real-world usability over headline specs. Its comfortable ride, effective one-pedal driving, and practical cargo space make it a strong everyday vehicle. The preconditioning/CarPlay conflict and limited charging transparency are areas for improvement, but these do not overshadow the overall package.
