# The Rivian R2 Is Just As Efficient As A Tesla Model Y, Despite Weighing More

**Date**: 2026-06-19

---

The 2027 Rivian R2 Performance has matched the Tesla Model Y Performance on EPA-rated energy efficiency, despite being roughly 500 pounds heavier, taller, and wider.

## EPA Numbers

| | Rivian R2 Performance | Tesla Model Y Performance |
|---|---|---|
| Range | 330 miles | 306 miles |
| Efficiency | 32 kWh/100 mi (3.12 mi/kWh) | 32 kWh/100 mi |
| Wheels | 21-inch | 21-inch |
| Weight | ~5,000 lbs | ~4,500 lbs |
| Horsepower | 656 hp | ~510 hp |
| 0–60 mph | 3.6 sec | 3.3 sec |

## Why This Is Significant

Matching Tesla's efficiency from a heavier, boxier crossover with more power is a notable engineering achievement. Tesla has long been the benchmark for EV efficiency thanks to its aerodynamic focus and powertrain optimization. Rivian achieving parity while delivering more range (330 vs 306 miles) and more power is a strong signal of maturing EV engineering at the company.

## Context

The R2 Performance launches at $57,990 and represents Rivian's attempt to compete in the mainstream mid-size EV SUV segment — the same space dominated by the Model Y. Efficiency parity removes one of Tesla's traditional advantages in this comparison.
