# TruckTower seeks EIT Urban Mobility funding for VerTIBUS vertical bus depot project

**Author**: Editorial staff | **Date**: 2026-06-29

---

German startup **TruckTower** has submitted the **VerTIBUS** funding proposal under the **EIT Urban Mobility Strategic Innovation Open Call** to evaluate the feasibility of **vertical bus depot infrastructure** across Europe.

## Project Overview

- **VerTIBUS** = vertical bus depot concept (BusTower) addressing land-constrained urban depots
- **Duration**: 24 months (if approved)
- **Consortium**: TruckTower (lead), Fraunhofer IVI, emutec GRID SYSTEMS, Luetkens Architektur, IPROCONSULT, SiKo, TAIVR/Nicole Noack, **Arriva** (operator partner)
- **Scope**: 5 transport operators + 9 depot sites/application cases

## What VerTIBUS Will Assess

Technical configurations, safety/fire-protection, regulatory frameworks, EV charging/grid integration, and economic models. Output: a **transferable decision framework** to determine when a BusTower solution is viable vs. conventional depot expansion, new construction, or relocation.

## Why It Matters

Fleet electrification is creating acute depot capacity pressure — more space needed for charging infrastructure, larger vehicle footprints, and grid connections. TruckTower's vertical stacking concept addresses the land-availability bottleneck that many European operators face.

MD Felix Hackbarth: "BusTower is not merely a research concept. TruckTower has the system competence, IP basis and partner network to plan and implement such projects already today."
