說明：

一、謹查近年中國境內產能過剩，龐大廉價產品尋求國際市場

作為出海口，而作為歐盟主要進口來源國之一，中方業者

出口大量產品至歐盟，對歐方形成貿易逆差，部分產品更

構成傾銷，造成歐盟產業損害，此外，中國電動車受惠中

國政府不公平補貼，出口至歐盟對當地電動車製造商構成

經濟損害之威脅，導致歐中貿易摩擦逐漸升溫。

二、此外，中方業者更挾不公平優勢，參與歐盟會員國之政府

採購案，造成歐盟內部市場扭曲，而中國醫療器材之政府

採購市場，對外國企業構成不公平待遇，影響歐洲企業與

產品參與相關採購案，更進一步加劇近期歐中貿易緊張情

勢，並增加經濟安全風險。

## 附件 2

China-based EU business calls
for robust protection

Representatives of European business invested in China visiting Brussels this week come with a plea to the

European Union to get tough on securing reciprocity in trade relationships.

European businesses in China are facing a new era of low growth in the country. The business and policy

climate is changing fast.

“It is a very different situation than last year or two years ago,” said Jens Ekelund, president of the
European Union Chamber of Commerce in China in a meeting with journalists.

Ekelund welcomed the EU’s new competitiveness compass’ approach to Chinese trade and investment.
“There is a higher focus on the European interest.”

More trade defence action

China’s industry churns out cheap products resulting from price deflation and overcapacities, explains

Ekelund. Chinese authorities appear reluctant to address the situation.

Unprofitable firms continue operating, seeking international markets and displacing competitive firms

worldwide.

The US administration’s new 10% tariffs on Chinese exports and small shipment restrictions will intensify
pressures. But surging exports to markets like the EU will continue “irrespective of the Trump tariffs,”

Ekelund said.

The European Commission launched record numbers of trade defence investigations in 2024, including
anti-dumping, anti-subsidy and foreign subsidies cases.

“But this is only reactive,” Ekelund said. He called for a more proactive, larger-scale conversation with

China about Europeans controlling their economic destiny.

Surging Chinese security state

European companies face growing pressure from an assertive state favouring domestic firms amid

heightened security focus.

Otherwise globally competitive European 5G technology providers have quickly lost market share in the

country.

“A blackbox cybersecurity process,” as European Chamber board member Markus Borchert described it,
excludes the likes of Nokia, Ericsson and SAP from operating licences.

The telecommunications sector is now required to localize every single part of its activities, including
patents and semiconductors. This makes it nearly impossible to gain state or state-owned company

contracts and has forced companies into costly silo-ing of their operations.

European Union 5G service provider market share dropped from 6.8% to 3.2% since 2022, Borchert said. He
contrasted this with Chinese 5G operators gaining EU market share despite EU 5G Toolbox security
standards.

This makes it practically impossible to compete on larger contracts with the sprawling state and state-

owned enterprises.

End free trade in certain industries?

There is alarm at China’s growing innovation lead in key areas such as ICT, electric vehicles, autonomous

driving, pharmaceuticals and medical devices.

“From a free trade perspective, this raises many questions”, Ekelund said.

He suggested excluding certain strategic sectors from WTO non-discrimination rules, within a clearly
defined EU framework maintaining dialogue with China.

Trade tensions escalate

China retaliated against EU electric vehicle duties through multiple channels, investigating EU dairy, pork
and brandy exports for dumping.

Export difficulties emerged even as investigations continue. French cognac faced customs delays ahead of

2025 Chinese new year celebrations.

When members of his organisation complained, a member of the Chinese government responded: “Let

them drink whisky,” Ekelund said.
