# JLR expands EMA platform to support full hybrids

**Author**: Florian Treiss | **Date**: 17.06.2026

---

For years, JLR—the group behind Jaguar and Land Rover—has been working on its new electric vehicle platform called Electrified Modular Architecture (EMA). Now it has become clear the platform is also intended to be used for vehicles with full hybrid (HEV) powertrains.

JLR has announced it is 'increasing propulsion flexibility' to offer more choice in global markets. While JLR remains committed to positioning Jaguar as a purely electric brand (with the upcoming Type 01 all-electric four-door GT), Land Rover will offer a broader range of powertrain options. Its sub-brands—Range Rover, Defender, and Discovery—will feature a spectrum ranging from mild hybrids and full hybrids to plug-in hybrids and battery-electric vehicles. No more pure ICE vehicles.

The EMA, first announced by JLR in 2021, was initially designed to utilise 800-volt technology for high charging performance. However, the EMA will no longer be a pure battery-electric platform; it will now also accommodate full hybrids.

JLR plans to introduce its first electric model under the Range Rover brand later this year, based on the EMA platform—also available as a full hybrid. A battery-electric Defender based on EMA is also expected to follow, likely complemented by a full hybrid version.

The first all-electric models from Land Rover—the Range Rover Electric and Range Rover Sport Electric—are both set to launch in 2026 on the Modular Longitudinal Architecture (MLA) platform.
