# electrive COMMERCIAL — 4 June 2026

**Source**: electrive global (newsletter@electrive.com)

---

## Editorial
Carla Westerheide notes the busy run-up to summer break among truck manufacturers: Daimler Truck announced an electric Lowliner, Scania is preparing electric truck production in France, and Volvo Trucks is set to unveil its new flagship electric truck in Sweden. Meanwhile, Sany has quietly begun e263 deliveries in Germany, with the issue covering Sany's European rollout in detail alongside Daimler's eActros Lowliner entry.

## Top
- **Sany launches e-truck deliveries in Europe** — First e263 electric truck units handed to customers in Germany; details on payload, net battery capacity and battery warranty included. Sany teased a "big splash with new models and a preview for 2027" for IAA Transportation.

## Bus
- **Palmer Energy Technology acquires Kleandrive** — Acquired UK bus repower specialist Kleandrive out of administration, securing electrification/retrofit expertise for heavy-duty vehicles.
- **First Bus deploys repowered e-buses in Bath** — Converting 27 former diesel double-deckers to battery-electric as part of fleet decarbonisation.
- **West Yorkshire orders 193 electric buses from Wrightbus** — £100 million contract for the new Weaver Network, including single- and double-decker models.
- **Zenobē and Vectalia agree €120m e-bus deal** — Framework agreement to accelerate electric bus deployment and depot electrification in Spain.
- **Kempower delivers Australia's first pantograph-down electric bus chargers** — Brookvale Bus Depot installation enables electrification of up to 229 diesel buses.
- **Zum deploys major electric school bus fleet** — San Francisco Unified School District introduces 238 electric school buses with bidirectional/V2G charging capability.

## Truck
- **Scania to build electric trucks in France** — €70 million investment in Angers plant; also presented its first battery-electric double cab for emergency vehicles (Scania CP31L).
- **MAN joins Green Brenner Initiative** — With Dettendorfer Energy and other partners, advancing zero-emission logistics along the Brenner Corridor.
- **Mercedes F1 team deploys eActros 600** — Mercedes-AMG PETRONAS will use the electric truck across all nine European races, covering ~15,000 km transporting infrastructure.
- **Orange EV secures record order for 600 terminal trucks** — Full rollout scheduled for 2026.
- **UK study backs autonomous HGV routes** — Hub-to-hub trunking and intermodal shuttle services identified as most viable first applications; UK freight trials expected from 2027.

## Final
- **Daimler Truck introduces eActros Lowliner** — Mercedes-Benz Trucks will launch the battery-electric eActros Lowliner in 2027, entering a segment led by MAN and Designwerk, with up to 621 kWh LFP battery capacity, up to 500 km range, and later megawatt charging support.
