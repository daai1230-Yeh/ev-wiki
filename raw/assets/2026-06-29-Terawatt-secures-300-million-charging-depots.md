# Terawatt secures up to $300 million for charging depots

**Author**: Carla Westerheide | **Date**: 2026-06-29

---

US charging infrastructure provider **Terawatt Infrastructure** has secured a **debt financing facility of up to $300 million** to accelerate the rollout of charging depots for autonomous and electric fleets.

## Financing Structure

- **$150 million** committed funding + option for additional **$150 million**
- Syndicate led by **RBC Capital Markets**, with **SMBC** and **UBS Investment Bank** participating

## Business Model

Terawatt owns and operates the full charging stack: real estate, grid connections, hardware, power management software, and operations. Fleet operators access infrastructure via **operating expenditure** (no large upfront capex).

Terawatt describes itself as the **largest operator of dedicated AV and commercial EV charging infrastructure in the United States**.

## Use of Proceeds

Funds will be used to acquire and develop charging depots for:
- Autonomous vehicle (AV) operators
- Commercial electric fleets
- Heavy-duty logistics providers

## Market Context

- Global robotaxi market estimated to grow from ~7,000 vehicles in 2025 to **6 million vehicles by 2035**, reaching ~**$415 billion** in market value
- CEO Neha Palmer: "Electrification of fleets is here to stay and the infrastructure powering it has to be bulletproof"
