# 2027 Mercedes C-Class EV vs. 2027 BMW i3: Which Is Better?

**Author**: Andrei Nedelea | **Date**: 2026-04-21

---

Mercedes has just revealed the electric C-Class, a direct rival to the BMW i3. These two long-range, charging-monster EVs are about as direct as rivals get, but they have diverging approaches to the electric compact executive sedan problem. Both share 800-volt platforms, but one leans toward luxury while the other goes all-out on EV tech.

## Size

The Mercedes C-Class EV is larger: 4.88 m long on a 2.96 m wheelbase vs. the BMW i3's 4.76 m / 2.89 m. The C-Class is actually closer in size to the gas-powered E-Class sedan. Despite its size, the C-Class doesn't offer notably more rear legroom.

## Power & Performance

| | Mercedes C400 4Matic | BMW i3 xDrive 50 |
|---|---|---|
| Power | **482 hp** | 469 hp |
| Torque | **590 lb-ft (800 Nm)** | 476 lb-ft (645 Nm) |
| 0-100 km/h | **3.9 sec** | ~4.5 sec (est.) |
| Transmission | 2-speed rear + front motor decouple | Single-speed |

The Mercedes has more power and torque at launch. Both brands will add single-motor, performance (M/AMG), and wagon variants later.

## Charging & Range — BMW Wins

| | BMW i3 | Mercedes C-Class EV |
|---|---|---|
| Battery | **108.7 kWh** | 94.3 kWh |
| WLTP Range | **559 miles (900 km)** | 473 miles (762 km) |
| Peak Charging | **400 kW** | 330 kW |
| 10-80% time | **21 min** | 22 min |
| 10 min range add | **249 miles (400 km)** | 202 miles (325 km) |

The BMW i3 clearly wins on range and charging speed. Both support V2L; the i3 also supports V2H and V2G.

## Technology Approach

**Mercedes** flaunts its tech with a massive optional 39.1-inch pillar-to-pillar dashboard display — the largest ever seen in a car. It also offers adaptive air suspension and 4.5° rear-wheel steering as options, giving a broader range of ride personalities.

**BMW** takes a "shy tech" approach — restrained but effective. The 17.9-inch parallelogram screen is bold in shape but clean in execution. The 43-inch panoramic windshield display is effectively a HUD. The "Heart of Joy" supercomputer enables exceptionally smooth driving dynamics and a standout "limo stop" function.

## Verdict

On specs, the BMW clearly wins — more range, faster charging, more advanced EV tech. The Mercedes counters with more power, a larger interior, and more comfort/adaptability options.

Mercedes seems more interested in translating its existing brand values into the EV age. BMW is using electrification as justification to break with tradition. Both approaches make for a compelling revival of an old rivalry under very different terms.
