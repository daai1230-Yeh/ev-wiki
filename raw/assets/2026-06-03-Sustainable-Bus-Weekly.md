# Sustainable Bus Weekly — June 3, 2026

**Source**: Sustainable Bus Weekly (info@sustainable-bus.com)

---

This week's edition spotlights Karsan's plans for a new 18-metre hydrogen bus alongside fresh battery developments, Latin America's electric bus market approaching 10,000 vehicles, Greater Manchester's order for 125 additional Wrightbus e-buses, New York State's decision to postpone its electric school bus mandate, and Solaris' strategic push into North America under the CAF Group. Also covered: new charging infrastructure in Trieste, trolleybus and e-bus investments in Limoges, Volvo Buses' intercity electrification contract in Sweden, Solaris securing Environmental Product Declarations for three Urbino electric models, insights from Busworld Southeast Asia 2026, and the largest electric school bus deployment in the United States.

## Top News
- **Karsan announces 18-metre hydrogen bus and new battery developments** — with 424 e-buses delivered in Italy.

## Main News
- Santiago, São Paulo and Bogotá make up nearly 70% of Latin America's e-bus fleet, now approaching 10,000 vehicles.
- New York State delays electric school bus mandate by five years amid cost and infrastructure concerns.
- Greater Manchester adds 125 Wrightbus e-buses to Bee Network fleet plan.

## Highlights
- Solaris is positioning North America as a strategic growth market within CAF Group's urban mobility activities, combining CAF's established US rail presence with Solaris' zero-emission bus portfolio — interview with Félix Fernández, CEO of CAF USA and President of Solaris North America.
- Europe's public transport operators face overlapping pressures of staffing shortages and fleet electrification at the bus depot — around 105,000 bus and coach driver positions remain unfilled across Europe (IRU figures).

## There's More
- Solaris obtains EPD (Environmental Product Declaration) certification for three Urbino electric bus models.
- Volvo Buses to supply 47 electric intercity buses to Bergkvara by end-2027.
- Conduent to divest public transit business to Modaxo for $164 million.
- Busworld Southeast Asia: a recap of how the 2026 edition went.
- Karsan autonomous bus involved in Gothenburg tram collision — "Incident appears not related to the autonomous driving system," says Karsan.
- Trieste Trasporti opens e-bus charging station with 14 charging points.
- A new operating model for Limoges: trolleybuses and e-buses take centre stage in the "Moovéo" programme.

## Photo Story
- San Francisco Unified School District and Zum have launched what is described as the largest electric school bus deployment in the United States.
