# electrive TODAY — 2 June 2026

**Source**: electrive global (newsletter@electrive.com)

---

## News
- **Mini keeps electric car production out of Oxford for now** — BMW reportedly reconsidering plans to electrify Mini's Oxford plant amid trade barriers and rising costs, casting doubt over medium-term modernisation.
- **Nio slows overseas expansion plans** — Nio scaling back international expansion, especially in Europe, to focus on China; no new model updates or battery swap stations planned abroad before end of 2027.
- **Mercedes expands electric GLC line-up** — Two new variants joining the electric GLC range this month, offering different powertrains paired with a smaller battery than the current 94 kWh AWD version.
- **Norway keeps EV share near 98%** — 15,210 new BEVs registered in May (97.8% EV share); BEVs have accounted for ~98% of new passenger car registrations all year.
- **MG to launch IM5 and IM6 in Germany** — Electric IM5 saloon and IM6 SUV launching in Germany in July, following debuts in UK, Norway and Switzerland; will also arrive in France, Belgium and Netherlands.
- **Lucid appoints Silvio Napoli as CEO** — Leadership transition finalised; Marc Winterhoff returns to COO role. Napoli will focus on customer engagement, cost competitiveness and operational efficiency.

## Number of the Day
- UK charge point operator char.gy installed its 5,000th public charge point, part of Brighton & Hove City Council's plan for ~6,000 kerbside charge points.

## Technology
- **Europe launches Moore4Power semiconductor project** — Consortium led by Infineon and 62 partners launches major semiconductor research initiative for next-gen power electronics, including e-mobility applications.
- **Estonia clears Tesla FSD for public roads** — Estonia approved Tesla's FSD (Supervised) for public roads, recognising the Dutch RDW type approval; third EU member state after Netherlands and Lithuania.
- **Syrah says Tesla graphite dispute partially resolved** — Tesla withdrew its termination notice in the graphite supply dispute with Syrah Resources, though the contract situation is not fully resolved.
- **Rosenberger unveils HVR280 HV connector** — New high-voltage connector series for EVs supports up to 1200V/310A; applications include battery packs, inverters, PDUs and drive units.

## Feedback
- Yesterday's most-clicked link: BMW opens configurator for rear-wheel-drive iX3.

## Quotes of the Day
- Stef Cornelis (Transport & Environment): "It's perplexing that in almost half of EU countries, governments are still giving a subsidy for companies to drive a petrol car." Two-thirds of EU member states lack a clear tax signal pushing companies toward electric cars.
- André Thierig (Tesla Germany factory head), on whether Berlin could build the Semi or Cybercab: "No concrete decision has yet been made on an additional product... Which product comes to Berlin and when depends on the market."

## Fleets + Infrastructure
- **Gridserve opens fast charging hub at Moto Lymm** — New 'Electric Super Hub' on the M6 with 24 ultra-rapid bays (400 kW-capable), linking Liverpool and Manchester.
- **GreenWay raises €138m for CEE charging expansion** — Polish charging operator secures €138 million in debt financing to expand its network across Central and Eastern Europe.
- **Polestar and Clever test bidirectional charging** — Selected Danish households will trial V2G, V2H and backup power supply with Polestar EVs and Clever infrastructure.
- **West Yorkshire orders 193 electric buses from Wrightbus** — Contract worth up to £100 million for single- and double-decker electric buses for the new Weaver Network.
- **XCharge names Iljasov Co-CEO** — Albina Iljasov appointed Co-CEO alongside Yifei Hou, overseeing European business operations since June 1.

## Service
- Reading tip: Alpine (Renault Group) introduces electric crossover A390; Germany CEO Amaury Marzloff discusses the brand's electric transformation and priorities for an electric sports car.

## Short Circuit
- VW blocks API access for charging control — Volkswagen restricted external access to an API used by EV owners to monitor and control charging via third-party services, affecting some home photovoltaic charging integrations.
