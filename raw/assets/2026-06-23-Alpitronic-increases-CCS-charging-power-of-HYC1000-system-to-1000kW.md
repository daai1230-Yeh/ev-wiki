# Alpitronic increases CCS charging power of HYC1000 system to 1,000kW

**Author**: Daniel Bönnighausen | **Date**: 22.06.2026

---

Alpitronic is advancing its decentralised megawatt charging system HYC1000 with a new high-performance dispenser. Thanks to a single, liquid-cooled CCS connector, the new variant is designed to supply electric vehicles with over 1,000A and up to 1,000kW. Previously, the HYC1000 supported a maximum of 600kW via CCS.

With the launch of the HYC1000 last year, Alpitronic introduced its first decentralised charging system with a total output of up to one megawatt. The system features eight 125 kW power stacks with a granularity of 62.5 kW, which are not located in the charging station itself but in a central power cabinet. From there, power is dynamically distributed to the connected charging points.

The new High Performance Dispenser (HP Dispenser) delivers currents exceeding 1,000A and charging powers of up to one megawatt via a single CCS connection. The dispenser operates within a voltage range of 150 to 1,000V.

The fundamental concept of the HYC1000 remains unchanged. One HP Dispenser occupies two of the eight power cabinet outputs, allowing up to four such dispensers to be operated from a single power cabinet. Mixed configurations are also possible — for example, three EV Dispensers with up to 600kW each and one HP Dispenser with up to 1,000kW, providing seven charging points at a single location.

Alpitronic had already tested a preliminary version of this technology last year during the record-breaking drive of two Mercedes Concept AMG GT XX vehicles in Nardò, achieving a peak charging power of 1,041kW at a peak current of 1,176A. The insights gained from this test were incorporated into the series development of the new HP Dispenser.

"With the new HP Dispenser, we are expanding our HYC1000 with a solution that combines high charging performance with the flexibility of a distributed charging system," says Philipp Senoner, CEO of Alpitronic. "Short charging times are a key requirement for many people when switching to electric mobility."

The HP Dispenser will initially be available in Europe, with a market launch in the USA and Canada announced for 2027.
