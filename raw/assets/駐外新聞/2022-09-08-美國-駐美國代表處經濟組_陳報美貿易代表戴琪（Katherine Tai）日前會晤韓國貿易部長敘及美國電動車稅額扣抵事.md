Readout of Ambassador Katherine Tai's Meeting
with Korea’s Minister for Trade Ahn Dukgeun

September 07, 2022

WASHINGTON – United States Trade Representative Katherine Tai met today with Korea’s Minister for Trade Ahn

Dukgeun.  Ambassador Tai welcomed Korea’s close cooperation on the Indo-Pacific Economic Framework and

discussed the upcoming Indo-Pacific Economic Framework Ministerial in Los Angeles.  

The Ministers also discussed a broad range of trade issues with the goal of forging a stronger partnership in areas of

common interest, including with respect to challenges related to supply chains, efforts to support environmental

protection, and ways to strengthen our cooperation in regional and multilateral fora including APEC and the G-20. 

Ambassador Tai also noted her desire for close cooperation with Korea on supporting internationally-recognized worker

rights. 

Ambassador Tai listened closely to the ROK’s concerns about the electric vehicle (EV) provisions of the Inflation

Reduction Act of 2022 and the two committed to open an engagement channel on these issues.  They also highlighted

the importance of meaningful action on clean energy technologies to combat the climate crisis while addressing supply

chain and security vulnerabilities.

South Korea and U.S. agree to ‘ministerial-level’ talks on EV tax credit | InsideTrade.com

South Korea and U.S. agree to ‘ministerial-level’ talks on EV tax
credit

September 7, 2022 at 6:42 PM

Tweet

The U.S. and South Korea have agreed to undergo “ministerial-level” discussions on the U.S. Inflation Reduction
Act’s electric vehicle tax credit, which Korea has said is discriminatory against its auto manufacturers, according to
a report from the Yonhap News Agency.

South Korean Trade Minister Ahn Duk-geun voiced his concerns about the Inflation Reduction Act’s electric
vehicle tax credit to U.S. Trade Representative Katherine Tai during a meeting in Washington, DC, on Wednesday.

“We will discuss specific dates and other details (of the launch) as quickly as possible, but I plan to meet USTR Tai
every week, including next week and week after that,” Ahn told reporters following the meeting, according to the
report.

If necessary, South Korea will ask for a revision of the legislation, the minister said, according Yonhap News
Agency.

“The U.S. government too fully understands the seriousness of this issue, and so we plan to continue our
consultations on how it can actually be resolved with practical measures,” Ahn said. “Since we have formed a
consultation channel with the USTR as I had hoped and agreed to start detailed discussions on this issue, we will
continue our discussions as many alternatives as possible.”

President Biden signed the Inflation Reduction Act on Aug. 16.

South Korea believes the EV tax credit could violate the Korea-U.S. Free Trade Agreement and World Trade
Organization rules. It alleges that the law discriminates against foreign manufacturers.

South Korea is actively reviewing whether to bring the case to the WTO. The European Union has expressed
similar concerns about the EV tax credit.

“Ambassador Tai listened closely to the ROK’s concerns about the electric vehicle (EV) provisions of the Inflation
Reduction Act of 2022 and the two committed to open an engagement channel on these issues,” USTR said in a
readout after the meeting.

Korean Trade, Industry and Energy Minister Lee Chang-yang, also in Washington this week, met with U.S.
lawmakers on Monday to discuss the Inflation Reduction Act and other issues, according to a readout from the
Korean government.

“Minister Lee expressed his agreement on the IRA’s overall aim of responding to climate change and expanding
clean energy, but relayed that its discriminatory elements favoring U.S.-made electric vehicles (EVs) over imported
EVs and granting tax benefits to the former are a source of serious concern to the Korean government and related
industries, stressing that the two countries must promptly settle the matter,” the readout states.

Lee also said “that communication should continue via a new bilateral negotiation channel for in-depth talks, and
emphasized the need for more involved concern and action from the U.S. Congress,” it continues.

During their Wednesday meeting, Tai and Ahn also discussed this week’s Indo-Pacific Economic Framework for
Prosperity ministerial meeting in Los Angeles, USTR said. South Korea is one of the 14 countries participating in
the U.S.-led framework.
