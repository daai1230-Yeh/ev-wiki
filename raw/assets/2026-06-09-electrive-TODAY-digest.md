# electrive TODAY — 9 June 2026

**Source**: electrive global (newsletter@electrive.com)

---

## News
- **Skoda launches Epiq production in Navarra** — Series production of the all-electric Epiq has started in Spain; unlike the VW ID. Polo and Cupra Raval, the entry-level EV rolls off the assembly line at Volkswagen's Navarra plant near Pamplona rather than Martorell.
- **Seven EU countries reject weaker CO₂ rules** — France, Spain and five other EU member states want the EU to stay committed to its electrification strategy, opposing additional concessions in the upcoming Automotive Package.
- **ACEA and SMMT seek delay to UK-EU EV tariffs** — European and British automotive associations push to postpone planned Brexit tariffs on EVs, arguing battery sourcing rules tied to duty-free trade remain too restrictive.
- **Mercedes opens pre-orders for electric GLC in China** — Pre-orders open starting at 349,000 yuan; the SUV is based on the new MB.EA platform with up to 703 km of CLTC range.
- **Cupra confirms production version of Tindaya concept** — The Tindaya concept shown at IAA Munich will go into series production; final powertrain not yet confirmed.
- **Leapmotor prices B05 from £28,995 in UK** — UK pricing confirmed ahead of sales starting in July; 67.1 kWh battery, up to 300 miles WLTP range, DC charging up to 174 kW.

## Number of the Day
- Battery-electric van registrations in the UK rose 35.5% YoY in May to 2,345 units (SMMT); BEVs reached a 9.8% market share, up from 7.6% in May 2025.

## Technology
- **China tightens EREV rules** — New technical regulations for range-extender EVs take effect November 2026, responding to the rapidly growing EREV market.
- **MAN and Rosenbauer unveil electric aerial ladder truck** — Fully electric fire service vehicle based on the 16-tonne MAN eTGM, expanding the range of battery-electric emergency vehicles.
- **Schaltbau starts production of MCS contactor** — New C330 megawatt charging contactor for MCS applications; bi-directional, supports up to 3,000 A and 1,500 V.
- **Freudenberg develops next-gen battery cell caps** — 'Cell Caps 2' for prismatic and cylindrical cells, designed to cut cap height by up to 30% and increase energy density.

## Quotes of the Day
- Xavier Chardon (CEO, Citroën): "We are planning a fully electric vehicle that will be built in Europe and is expected to cost less than €15,000 without government subsidies." — confirming the electric comeback of the 2CV, with a concept close to the production model planned for the Paris Motor Show in October 2026.
- Michael Dunne (CEO, Dunne Insights): "By 2030, we will see some form of Chinese cars on American roads. One way or another, they'll find their way in." — arguing China is pursuing a long-term strategy to dominate the global EV market across cars, trucks and batteries.

## Fleets + Infrastructure
- **Katoen Natie orders 1,000 BMW EVs** — Belgian logistics group will add over 1,000 electric BMW and Mini models by end of 2027, replacing combustion company cars in BMW's largest single EV fleet order in Belgium.
- **Hampshire selects Believ for major contract** — Believ will install 17,180 public EV charge points across Hampshire, combining £6.6m public funding with up to £90m private investment.
- **Ubitricity grows Bexley network to 500 chargers** — 400 additional on-street charge points installed in the London Borough of Bexley, integrated into existing lamp posts.
- **Montra Electric deploys 100 electric reefer trucks in India** — Deployed with Green Drive Mobility and TVS Vehicle Mobility Solutions for More Retail's quick commerce operations.
- **Blink sells Envoy business to Blade Ranger** — Blink Charging sells subsidiary Envoy Technologies to Israel-based Blade Ranger as part of a strategy refocus on EV charging infrastructure operations.

## Service
- Reading tip: A new T&E study warns weakening EU CO2 targets for cars could undermine renewable energy expansion; Europe could need up to 150 additional power plants and billions in extra grid investment if fewer EVs support vehicle-to-grid services.
- Click tip: BYD has released official images of its upcoming flagship sedan ("Great Han"/Han 9), to be offered as BEV and PHEV with Blade Battery and megawatt charging technology.

## Short Circuit
- Clean Motion unveils electric hearse — Swedish EV maker introduces an extended version of its EVIG Memorial vehicle, a battery-electric model for sustainable coffin transport.
