# Korean charging provider Chaevi goes public

**Author**: Florian Treiss | **Date**: 2026-04-21

---

South Korean Porsche partner Chaevi is set to go public on the KOSDAQ technology exchange in Seoul, aiming to raise approximately €64 million in capital through its initial public offering.

Chaevi was founded around ten years ago and has since grown into South Korea's largest charging provider. Based in Daegu, the company operates approximately 6,000 of its own DC fast chargers. Including locations managed for other companies and institutions, this number increases to around 10,000 DC fast chargers across South Korea.

Chaevi operates as a vertically integrated full-service provider of charging solutions for electric vehicles — covering hardware manufacturing, software development, installation, and network operation. The company has also expanded its footprint into the United States, operating across 13 states.

The IPO is scheduled for April 30, 2026, with an offering price range of 12,300 to 15,300 Korean won per share. The company is offering 10 million primary shares, managed by KB Securities and Samsung Securities. Chaevi is reportedly the first domestic EV charging infrastructure firm in South Korea to pursue an IPO.

Porsche is among Chaevi's notable partners, highlighting the company's position in the premium charging segment.
