# Small, electric, and quick to charge: Smart's bid to reinvent the two-seater

**Author**: Peter Schwierz | **Date**: June 22, 2026

---

Smart has revealed new details about the upcoming Smart #2 in Rome. The electric two-seater is set to build on the legacy of the former Fortwo with a more advanced technical package. Built on Smart's own ECA platform, it features a 35.7-kWh battery, a target WLTP range of around 300 kilometres, and fast charging in under 20 minutes. Smart Europe is targeting an entry price of under €20,000 for the base model.

## Electric Compact Architecture (ECA)

At the heart of the Smart #2 is a newly developed platform called the Electric Compact Architecture (ECA), designed specifically for an ultra-compact, fully electric vehicle — not a downsized standard platform but an architecture engineered from the ground up for short exterior dimensions, electric components, and maximum space utilisation.

A key selling point: the Smart #2 achieves a turning circle of just 6.95 metres kerb-to-kerb, matching the last Smart two-seater. For urban driving, delivery services, and car-sharing operators, this is highly practical.

## Battery and Charging

- **Battery**: 35.7 kWh (comparable to VW ID. Polo entry level)
- **WLTP range**: ~300 km (target, not yet certified)
- **DC fast charging**: 10–80% in under 20 minutes

The charging performance is notable for a car this size, indicating well-engineered thermal management. Smart suggests users could cover their weekly charging needs with a single fast-charging stop during a shopping trip.

## Safety

The Tridion cell — previously a hallmark of the Fortwo — returns in a new generation, protecting the battery and enabling effective crash structures within a very small vehicle body.

## Interior

The concept features a continuous bench seat, a quasi S-shaped cockpit design, and an open interior architecture. The bench seat enables passengers to slide across to the driver's seat when parked in tight spaces.

## Market Positioning

The Smart #2 will debut at the Paris Motor Show this autumn. If the sub-€20,000 entry price is achieved, it would fill a genuine gap in Europe's small EV market — combining nostalgic appeal, technical distinctiveness, and affordability.
