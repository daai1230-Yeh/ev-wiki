# CATL launches new battery module assembly line in Debrecen

**Author**: Florian Treiss | **Date**: 2026-05-13

---

CATL has started assembling battery modules in a newly constructed facility in Debrecen, Hungary, with an annual capacity of 5 GWh. Test production of battery cells is set to begin soon once final permits are secured.

## Background

CATL announced its Debrecen factory in August 2022 with an investment of €7.34 billion across multiple expansion phases. Named customers include Mercedes-Benz (confirmed) and BMW (reported), the latter having built a new plant in Debrecen to manufacture the all-electric BMW iX3. Debrecen will be CATL's second European cell factory after Arnstadt, Germany.

## Current Status

- **Module production**: New hall with 5 GWh annual capacity launched — enough for ~50,000 vehicles with 100 kWh batteries or ~125,000 EVs with 40 kWh batteries.
- **Cell production**: Construction of the 40 GWh cell factory is complete; production equipment installed. Waiting for final permits before starting test production.
- **Delays**: CATL is behind schedule — series production was targeted for March/April 2026; transition from test to series production may take additional months.
- **Existing production**: Since autumn 2024, CATL has produced 240,000 modules in a rented hall in Debrecen (capacity for ~60,000 EVs).

## Future Plans

CATL plans to scale the Debrecen cell factory to 100 GWh capacity in a later expansion phase.
