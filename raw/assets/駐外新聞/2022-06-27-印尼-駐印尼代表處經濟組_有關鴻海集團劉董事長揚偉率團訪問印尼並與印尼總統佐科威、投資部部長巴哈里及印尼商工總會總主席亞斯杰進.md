Menteri Investasi Siap Kawal Investasi Foxconn Di Indonesia 

Jakarta, 25 Juni 2022 – Menteri Investasi/Kepala Badan Koordinasi Penanaman Modal (BKPM) Bahlil 
Lahadalia mendampingi Chairman Hon Hai Precision Industry Co. Ltd (Foxconn) Young Liu bertemu 
dengan Presiden Joko Widodo di Istana Bogor, pagi ini (25/6). Pertemuan tersebut membahas tindak 
lanjut  Nota  Kesepahaman  yang  telah  disepakati  sebelumnya  terkait  dengan  rencana  investasi 
Foxconn, Gogoro, PT Industri Baterai Indonesia (IBC), dan PT Indika Energy Tbk di Indonesia. Adapun 
nilai rencana investasi tersebut sebesar USD8 miliar dan perkiraan penyerapan tenaga kerja lebih dari 
10.000 orang. 

Dalam  hasil  pertemuan  tersebut,  Bahlil  mengungkapkan  bahwa  Presiden  Joko  Widodo  sangat 
mengapresiasi rencana investasi Foxconn yang akan berkolaborasi dengan Badan Usaha Milik Negara 
(BUMN)  dan  swasta  nasional  dengan  melibatkan  pengusaha  lokal  dan  Usaha  Mikro,  Kecil,  dan 
Menengah  (UMKM).  Kementerian  Investasi/BKPM  diperintahkan  untuk  melakukan  pengawalan 
rencana investasi Foxconn sampai dengan terealisasi. 

“Bapak Presiden memerintahkan kepada saya untuk segera mengawal sampai pada tahap eksekusi. 
Presiden  berharap  ini  segera  terealisasi  dan  satu  konsep  yg  paling  disenangi  presiden  ini  adalah 
kolaborasi BUMN, PMA dan swasta nasional yg melibatkan pengusaha lokal dan UMKM,” jelas Bahlil.  

Bahlil  mendorong  agar  rencana  investasi  Foxconn  ini  berlokasi  di  Kawasan  Industri  Terpadu  (KIT) 
Batang,  Jawa  Tengah  dengan  berbagai  insentif  yang  ditawarkan  oleh  Pemerintah  Indonesia.  KIT 
Batang menjadi salah satu lokasi yang menjadi bagian dari pembangunan ekosistem industri baterai 
dan kendaraan listrik di Indonesia, di mana saat ini telah masuk investasi dari Hyundai dan  LG asal 
Korea Selatan.  

Selain itu, Bahlil juga menyatakan bahwa Foxconn juga berminat untuk investasi di Ibu Kota Negara 
(IKN).  

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
“Harapannya  bisa  betul-betul  terjadi  dan  berjalan  dengan  baik.  Mereka  juga  berkeinginan  masuk 
invetasi di IKN. Jadi tidak benar kalau ada orang mengatakan IKN tidak  ada investasi. Banyak yang 
masuk,” jelas Bahlil. 

Chairman  Foxconn  Young  Liu  menyampaikan  apresiasi  atas  dukungan  Presiden  Joko  Widodo  dan 
Menteri  Investasi/Kepala  BKPM  yang  akan  sepenuhnya  memfasilitasi  investasi  Foxconn.  Foxconn 
menawarkan model bisnis baru yaitu BOL (Build, Operate, Localize) untuk investasinya di Indonesia. 

“Adanya  model  bisnis  baru  BOL  ini  akan  memungkinkan  Foxconn  dan  perusahaan  Taiwan  lainnya 
untuk dapat bermitra lebih baik lagi dengan perusahaan Indonesia dalam membangun industri di sini,” 
ujar Young.  

Chairman Young menyatakan keyakinannya bahwa kerja sama antara Foxconn dengan Pemerintah 
Indonesia ini dapat mencapai kesuksesan besar.  

Mengenai  minatnya  untuk  berinvestasi  di  IKN,  Foxconn  juga  mengungkapkan  minatnya  pada 
pengembangan smart city melalui infrastruktur bus listrik (e-bus) dan jaringan IoT (Internet of Things).  

Adapun  rencana  investasi  Foxconn,  Gogoro,  IBC,  dan  Indika  meliputi  industri  kendaraan  listrik: 
kendaraan listrik roda empat, roda dua, dan bus listrik; industri baterai kendaraan listrik; dan industri 
pendukung: energy storage system, stasiun penukaran baterai, industri daur ulang baterai, Research 
& Development (R&D) dan pelatihan. 

Pertemuan  ini  merupakan  tindak  lanjut  dari  kunjungan  kerja  Menteri  Investasi/Kepala  BKPM  ke 
Taiwan tanggal 22 Oktober 2021 lalu yang dilanjutkan dengan Nota Kesepahaman antara Kementerian 
Investasi/BKPM, Foxconn, Gogoro, Industri Baterai Indonesia (IBC), dan Indika Energy pada tanggal 21 
Januari  2022.  Kerja  sama  yang  disepakati  meliputi  pengembangan  ekosistem  energi  baru 
berkelanjutan  melalui  investasi  pada  baterai  listrik,  kendaraan  listrik  dan  berbagai  industri 
pendukungnya melalui skema kerja sama BOL tersebut di Indonesia. 

Berdasarkan data Kementerian Investasi/BKPM, realisasi investasi asal Taiwan pada tahun 2021 lalu 
tercatat sebesar USD316,9 juta dengan total 458 proyek. Sementara itu, pada triwulan I 2022, realisasi 
investasi asal Taiwan di Indonesia tercatat sebesar USD37,5 juta dengan total 219 proyek. (*)

Press Release    

Jakarta, 25 June 2022 

No. 38/Sipers/B.8/A.3/2022 

Minister of Investment Ready to Guard Foxconn Investments in Indonesia 

Jakarta, June 25, 2022 – 

The  Minister  of  Investment/Head  of  the  Investment  Coordinating  Board  (BKPM)  Bahlil  Lahadalia 

accompanied  the  Chairman  of  Hon  Hai  Precision  Industry  Co.  Ltd.  (Foxconn) Young  Liu  met  with 

President Joko Widodo at the Bogor Palace, this morning (25/6). The meeting discussed the follow-

up  to  the  previously  agreed  Memorandum  of  Understanding  related  to  the  investment  plans  of 

Foxconn, Gogoro, PT Industri Battery Indonesia (IBC), and PT Indika Energy Tbk in Indonesia. The 

value of the investment plan is USD 8 billion and the estimated employment is more than 10,000 

people. 

In  the  results  of  the  meeting,  Bahlil  revealed  that  President  Joko  Widodo  highly  appreciated 

Foxconn's  investment  plan  which  will  collaborate  with  State-Owned  Enterprises  (BUMN)  and 

national  private  companies  by  involving  local  entrepreneurs  and  Micro,  Small  and  Medium 

Enterprises  (MSMEs).  The  Ministry  of  Investment/BKPM  was  ordered  to  supervise  Foxconn's 

investment plan until it is realized. 

“Mr. President ordered me to immediately escort him to the execution stage. The president hopes 

that this will be realized soon and one concept that the president likes the most is the collaboration 

of BUMN, PMA and the national private sector involving local entrepreneurs and MSMEs," said Bahlil. 

Bahlil encouraged Foxconn's investment plan to be located in the Batang Integrated Industrial Estate 

(KIT), Central Java, with various incentives offered by the Indonesian government. KIT Batang is one 

of  the  locations  that  is  part  of  the  development  of  the  battery  and  electric  vehicle  industrial 

ecosystem in Indonesia, where investment from Hyundai and LG from South Korea has now entered. 

In addition, Bahlil also stated that Foxconn is also interested in investing in the State Capital (IKN). 

"Hopefully it can really happen and go well. They also want to invest in IKN. So it is not true that 

people say IKN is not an investment. Many have entered," Bahlil explained. 

Foxconn Chairman Young Liu expressed his appreciation for the support of President Joko Widodo 

and  the  Minister  of  Investment/Head  of  BKPM  who  will  fully  facilitate  Foxconn's  investment. 

Foxconn offers a new business model, namely BOL (Build, Operate, Localize) for its investment in 

Indonesia. 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
"The new BOL business model will allow Foxconn and other Taiwanese companies to better partner 

with Indonesian companies in building the industry here," said Young. 

Chairman  Young  expressed  his  confidence  that  this  collaboration  between  Foxconn  and  the 

Government of Indonesia could achieve great success. 

Regarding his interest in investing in IKN, Foxconn also expressed his interest in developing smart 

cities through electric bus (e-bus) infrastructure and IoT (Internet of Things) networks. 

The investment plans of Foxconn, Gogoro, IBC, and Indika include the electric vehicle industry: four-

wheeled,  two-wheeled,  and  electric  buses;  electric  vehicle  battery  industry;  and  supporting 

industries: energy storage system, battery exchange station, battery recycling industry, Research & 

Development (R&D) and training. 

This  meeting  is  a  follow-up  to  the  working  visit  of  the  Minister  of  Investment/Head  of  BKPM  to 

Taiwan on October 22, 2021, which was followed by a Memorandum of Understanding between the 

Ministry of Investment/BKPM, Foxconn, Gogoro, the Indonesian Battery Industry (IBC), and Indika 

Energy on January 21, 2022. The agreed cooperation includes the development of a sustainable new 

energy ecosystem through investment in electric batteries, electric vehicles and various supporting 

industries through the BOL cooperation scheme in Indonesia. 

Based on data from the Ministry of Investment/BKPM, the realization of investment from Taiwan in 

2021 was recorded at USD316.9 million with a total of 458 projects. Meanwhile, in the first quarter 

of 2022, the realization of investment from Taiwan in Indonesia was recorded at USD37.5 million 

with a total of 219 projects. (*)
