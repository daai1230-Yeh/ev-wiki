# Uber Says It Has A 'Superpower' To Boost EV Charging Growth

**Author**: InsideEVs | **Date**: 2026-04-27

---

Uber has announced a $100 million investment in building public fast-charging stations, partnering with charging networks including EVgo in the U.S. and Ionity in Europe to build stations in high-traffic regions.

## The Data Advantage

According to Andrew Cornelia, Uber's global head of electrification and sustainability, "One of Uber's superpowers is the fact that we have just so much data." The company is using this data to determine where charging infrastructure needs to be built. Armed with vast amounts of real-world trip data, Uber can help solve one of the industry's most persistent problems: matching charging supply with real-time demand from drivers who can't afford to waste time waiting.

## Supporting the Autonomous Vehicle Revolution

Uber is amid a tectonic shift, brokering dozens of deals worldwide to bring autonomous vehicles to its platform. The vast majority of these partnerships involve modern EVs which need consistent, reliable charging. Uber is aiding the build-out of new fast-charging stations to support the incoming wave of robotaxis on its platform while also providing more plug-in options to its human drivers, who are increasingly going electric.

## Utilization Guarantees

One key differentiator: Uber is providing charging networks with utilization guarantees. Fast-charging stations require millions in upfront capital and only make financial sense if utilization rates are consistently high. By directing its driver fleet to specific stations, Uber can effectively guarantee the usage volumes that make these investments viable for charging operators.

This strategy positions Uber not just as a ride-hailing platform but as a key enabler of EV infrastructure — bridging the gap between charging network operators, autonomous vehicle companies, and everyday EV drivers.
