# Germany: VW ID.3 Neo starts at 33,995 euros

**Author**: Sebastian Schaal | **Date**: 2026-04-17

---

At the world premiere a few days ago, Volkswagen had indicated a base price of under €34,000 for the ID.3 Neo. Now, the revised compact model can be configured, with list prices starting at €33,995. With the German 'I.D. purchase bonus,' the car becomes even more affordable.

The list price of €33,995 for the comprehensively revised ID.3 Neo represents only a slight increase over the 'old' ID.3, which previously started at €33,330 in Germany. For customers, the actual entry price is likely to remain below €30,000 for some time: the 'ID. purchase bonus' allows €4,000 to be deducted from the list price, currently valid until 30 June (with possible extension).

With the transition from the ID.3 to the ID.3 Neo, VW has updated the technical foundation from MEB to the further-developed MEB+, refreshed the design, and overhauled the entire offering logic. The designations ID.e Pure, Pro, Pro S, and GTX have been discontinued, replaced by Trend, Life, and Style equipment lines—with an ID.3 GTI likely to follow later.

Previously, the Pure, Pro, and Pro S variants were tied to specific drivetrain-battery combinations. VW has now decoupled these, allowing customers to configure more individually.

**Pricing overview:**

| Trim | 50 kWh/125 kW | 58 kWh/140 kW | 79 kWh/210 kW |
|------|--------------|--------------|--------------|
| Trend | €33,995 | — | — |
| Life | €36,225 | €39,195 | €44,995 |
| Style | €38,895 | €41,875 | €47,595 |

The base Trend model includes LED headlights, rain sensor, automatic climate control, 12.9-inch touchscreen, ACC, lane-keeping assist, blind-spot warning, and front/rear parking sensors.

The Life trim adds 18-inch alloy wheels, two-zone climate control, heated seats and steering wheel, and a reversing camera. From Life upwards, an optional tow hitch is available (for bike racks, not trailer towing).

The Style version adds 19-inch wheels, keyless locking, dynamic high-beam assistant, standard navigation, and illuminated logos. Optional extras include adaptive dampers, a glass roof, or Harman Kardon audio. Notably, the heat pump is still not standard on any trim and must be added from €1,090.

Moonstone Grey remains the free base colour; other colours cost €860–€1,010 extra. Series production in Zwickau begins in May, with market launch planned for summer.
