# Foxconn and EMP to begin producing electric SUVs in Poland from 2029

**Author**: Florian Treiss | **Date**: 18.06.2026

---

Poland's Prime Minister Donald Tusk has confirmed reports about an electric car factory planned by ElectroMobility Poland (EMP) and Foxconn in southern Poland's Jaworzno. The plant is set to open in 2029, where up to 400,000 electric SUVs will be produced annually.

In early May, it was revealed that ElectroMobility Poland (EMP), a joint venture established ten years ago by several state-owned energy companies in Poland, was seeking a partnership with Foxconn. The collaboration now appears to be confirmed. Poland's Prime Minister Donald Tusk stated: "Together with the Taiwanese, we will establish a major centre via ElectroMobility Poland in Jaworzno, which will be integrated into the city's and region's entire industrial and technology ecosystem. The plans include vehicles, likely three mid-range models, including SUVs."

Poland also intends to build a semiconductor factory in collaboration with Foxconn: "So: cars and semiconductors."

EMP CEO Cyprian Gronkiewicz had previously stated that construction of the factory was planned to begin in spring 2027. The joint venture plans to further develop Foxtron's existing platforms, with over 70% involvement from Polish engineers.

Production of three electric SUV models is scheduled to begin at the plant in 2029 with an annual capacity of 400,000 vehicles. Poland plans to provide loans totalling 4.5 billion PLN (approximately €1.06 billion) from EU recovery funds.

EMP was founded in 2016 to develop a Polish electric car brand named Izera. Earlier plans collapsed in 2024, but the project has now revived with a Taiwanese partner instead of a Chinese one.
