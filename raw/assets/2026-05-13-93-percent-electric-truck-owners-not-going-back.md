# The Results Are In: 93% of Electric Truck Owners Say They're Not Going Back

**Author**: Iulian Dnistran | **Date**: May 12, 2026

---

A new study by Germany's Oko-Institut (Institute for Applied Ecology) shows that operators of battery-electric heavy trucks are overwhelmingly happy with the technology. The institute surveyed 57 transport companies that have run electric trucks for at least a year and collectively own more than 300 of them. 93% of respondents reported being satisfied or very satisfied with their EV trucks, citing reliability, driving comfort, and lower running costs as the headline benefits. The survey was fielded in winter 2025-2026, drawing from Daimler Truck customers running models such as the Mercedes-Benz eActros, eEconic, and eArocs.

Operators do flag persistent challenges. Sticker prices remain high, and connecting depots to the grid is a major undertaking — the surveyed sites averaged about 1,115 kW of grid capacity, with installed charging power averaging 629 kW across 40 of the firms. Charging is overwhelmingly done at the depot; less than 5% of sessions take place at public truck chargers, which often lack the physical space to accommodate large rigs. Operators argue that low electricity costs and continued exemption from road tolls are crucial to scaling fleets further.

On reliability, 32% of respondents said issues arose at the same rate as with diesel, 16% saw significantly fewer issues, while 18% reported more issues and 7% significantly more. Daily mileage averaged 268 miles (432 km) across vehicles ranging from 3.5-tonne vans to 18-tonne long-haul rigs, and roughly 40% of fleets have at least 40% of their trucks running over 310 miles per day. Looking ahead, 93% of these early adopters expect electric trucks to become the standard vehicle type in their companies by 2030.
