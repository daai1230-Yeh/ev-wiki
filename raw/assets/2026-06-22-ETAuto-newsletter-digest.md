## Top Stories

### Why electric two-wheelers remain a loss-making ride for OEMs
Despite surging sales and rising adoption, India's electric two-wheeler makers face margin pressures as imported components and competitive pricing squeeze profitability. The segment continues to grow but OEMs struggle to achieve sustainable margins.

### Maruti, Mahindra and Tata Motors eye UK market to sell made-in-India EVs under FTA
The India-UK Free Trade Agreement, set to come into force on July 15, permits duty-free exports of electric, hybrid and hydrogen-powered vehicles. Indian automakers are positioning to tap the UK EV market opportunity, with Maruti already exporting the eVITARA to Europe (~36,000 units in 9 months). India will get access to duty-free exports in price segments from under GBP 20,000 to 80,000, with the total quota reaching a peak of 88,000 units from the 15th year.

### India's auto sector faces talent crunch as EV shift strains supply chain
India's automotive sector is facing a growing shortage of skilled workers in its supply chain as the shift to electric vehicles accelerates, requiring new competencies in battery technology, software, and electronics.
