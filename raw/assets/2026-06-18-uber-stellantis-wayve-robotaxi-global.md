# Uber, Stellantis, And Wayve Want To Blanket The World In Robotaxis

**Author**: Andrei Nedelea | **Date**: Jun 17, 2026

---

Uber, Stellantis, and Wayve want to scale Level 4 robotaxis globally, but the rollout timeline remains unclear. Stellantis would supply the vehicles, Wayve the AI driving tech, and Uber the ride-hailing network.

The three companies have entered into a non-binding memorandum of understanding in which each contributes: Stellantis will provide the vehicles, Wayve will handle the autonomous driving software, and Uber will integrate it into its vast ride-hailing network. They plan to bring autonomous mobility to cities "across Europe, North America and beyond."

Stellantis says it already has platforms that can support fully autonomous Level 4 driving, with embedded sensor suites and the required safety and redundancy. Stellantis and Uber struck a similar deal last year, with Nvidia as the self-driving technology provider.

Wayve's autonomous driving system relies on an array of six cameras, one radar, and machine learning, promising to replicate human-like driving in complex, heavily congested cities. Wayve employs a "mapless" AI system that learns to drive by observing how the humans around it drive and react in various situations.

Wayve says it has had vehicles on London's streets since 2018, but not as part of any commercial ride-hailing service and exclusively with a safety driver behind the wheel. This will still be the case for the initial rollout. The two companies have previously announced plans to deploy (Nissan-based) robotaxis in Tokyo by late 2026, as well as in ten other major cities later on.

Stellantis is one of a series of carmakers to team up with Uber to make robotaxis, including Lucid, Rivian, Volkswagen, and Nissan, as well as other robotaxi companies like Zoox.
