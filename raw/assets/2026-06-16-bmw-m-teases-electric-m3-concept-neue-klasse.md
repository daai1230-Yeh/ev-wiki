# BMW M teases electric M3 with Concept Neue Klasse

**Author**: Daniel Bönnighausen | **Date**: 2026-06-15

---

BMW M unveiled the Concept Neue Klasse at the 24 Hours of Le Mans, showcasing the future design language for all-electric M models based on the Neue Klasse platform — a clear preview of the upcoming electric M3.

Technically, the concept features four electric motors (one per wheel), controlled via BMW M Dynamic Performance Control within the 'Heart of Joy' high-performance computer. This enables wheel-specific regulation of drivetrain and braking. The battery is an M-specific optimised version of sixth-generation cylindrical cells on an 800-volt architecture with over 100 kWh capacity.

Design highlights include prominently flared wheel arches, a muscular shoulder line, M Aero mirrors, front splitter, V-shaped bonnet air vent, floating diffuser, and ducktail spoiler. A new signature feature for future BMW M models is the 'M Yellow Lights' inspired by GT racing cars. Natural fibre materials are used on the exterior and interior.

The battery-electric M models of the Neue Klasse are set to launch starting in 2027.
