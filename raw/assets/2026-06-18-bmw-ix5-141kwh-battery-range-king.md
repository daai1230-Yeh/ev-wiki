# The 2027 BMW iX5's Monster Battery Pack Dwarfs The Competition

**Author**: Suvrat Kothari | **Date**: Jun 15, 2026

---

BMW is going big with the new iX5 due out soon. The electric SUV will pack 144 kWh of usable battery capacity for the U.S. market and 141 kWh for Europe — the largest battery ever installed in an electric BMW.

BMW hasn't officially confirmed range figures, but is clearly swinging for the fences. The automaker's iX3 crossover and i3 sedan both promise well over 400 miles of EPA range from a 112 kWh battery, so the iX5 should comfortably clear that bar.

For context: the Porsche Cayenne Electric has about 113 kWh gross capacity; the Lucid Gravity Grand Touring packs 123 kWh and delivers over 400 miles. The iX5 out-batteries both by a significant margin. The closest rival is the Rivian R1S Max pack at 140 kWh.

The tradeoff: the battery contributes to a curb weight of roughly 3,000 kg (~6,600 lbs), making it one of the heaviest EVs in its segment. BMW addressed this with decoupled air suspension springs and dampers for larger internal volume, repositioned adaptive dampers and anti-roll bars, and cell-to-pack technology eliminating module layers.

The iX5 pushes BMW's Cluster Architecture to its limits. Unlike the iX3 (on the new Neue Klasse platform), the iX5 sticks with legacy underpinnings but borrows key Neue Klasse technologies: sixth-generation cylindrical cells and 800-volt architecture.

BMW is now completing final calibration drives at its Spartanburg plant. Four additional powertrain options will be offered: mild hybrid gas and diesel versions, a plug-in hybrid, and the first-ever hydrogen fuel-cell X5.
