# ABB Introduces Decentralised Charging System M-Series

**Author**: Cora Werwitzke | **Date**: 2026-04-24

---

ABB E-mobility is expanding its charging infrastructure offerings with the new M-Series — a decentralised system featuring a power unit and associated satellite charging stations. The Swiss company's solution provides charging capacities ranging from 200 kW to 1.2 MW, thanks to its modular design.

ABB E-Mobility has launched the new M-Series, a decentralised, air-cooled split system designed for public fast-charging locations, fleet depots, retail sites, and destination charging. The system is able to grow "in line with actual demand," allowing operators to start with a small ecosystem and add further dispensers and power units as needed.

## Architecture

The M-Series connects central technology cabinets — referred to as "Power Cabinets" — with a portfolio of specialised dispensers named Solo, Duo, Dock, and Ultra. The system supports the charging formats CCS1, CCS2, NACS, and MCS (Megawatt Charging System for trucks), covering 36 different site configurations.

## Key Advantages

The system dynamically distributes available capacity across charging points and adjusts in real time to match actual demand. This decentralised architecture allows operators to manage with lower installed power while ensuring high output when demand is high.

ABB's positioning frames the M-Series around what it calls "delivered-energy economics" rather than peak output. The company argues that charge point operators are transitioning from capacity scaling to operating high-performance infrastructure businesses, where the relevant metric is the cost of energy delivered across a site's lifetime rather than rated power per stall.

Both series are built on the same air-cooled, in-house-developed silicon carbide IP54 power electronics platform and a common reference architecture, commercially deployed since 2024.
