# Mercedes-Benz's Electric Super Sedan Has A New Kind Of EV Motor And Unprecedented Charging Speed

**Author**: Suvrat Kothari | **Date**: 2026-05-19

---

Mercedes-AMG has revealed the new GT 4-Door Coupe, an all-electric performance sedan that the author argues may be the most technologically advanced EV a Western automaker has yet put into production. It launches AMG's new AMG.EA performance platform and is positioned against the Porsche Taycan and Lucid Air Sapphire.

Key technical points captured from the article:

- **Axial flux motors**: Claimed to be the first production EV using axial-flux ("pancake"-shaped) motors from YASA, the British specialist Mercedes acquired in 2021. Mercedes says the power unit is roughly 67% lighter and 67% shorter than conventional radial motors.
- **Performance**: The GT 63 trim uses three motors (two rear, one front) for 1,153 hp and 1,475 lb-ft, hitting 60 mph in 2.3 seconds, with a 186 mph limited top speed. A GT 55 trim offers 805 hp.
- **Battery**: A 106 kWh pack with 2,660 cylindrical cells across 18 modules, using an NCMA cathode with a silicon-containing anode and ~298 Wh/kg cell energy density. Range is roughly 700 km WLTP (~315 miles EPA). Cells are immersed in coolant with on-demand module-level cooling, drawing on AMG One and F1 lessons.
- **Charging**: 600 kW peak charging — the highest from a non-Chinese automaker — enabling 10–80% in about 11 minutes. By comparison the Tesla Cybertruck does 500 kW; many luxury EVs are around 400 kW. Chinese makers like BYD still lead globally with near-megawatt speeds.
- **Design & aero**: A polarizing, heavily styled exterior with an "Aerokinetics" active aerodynamics system (underbody panels deploying at 75 and 87 mph) plus optional active rear diffuser and cooling flaps. It also offers a simulated manual gearbox with synthesized V8 sounds.
- **Availability**: GT 55 reaches U.S. dealers later in 2026, GT 63 in early 2027; pricing to be announced.
