主旨：陳報美國聯邦眾議院歲計委員會通過「在美國建設法案」

擬修改「通膨削減法」之潔淨能源車輛稅額扣抵，敬請查

參。

說明：

一、相關文號：本組本(112)年5月31日經美字第1120000513號

函。

二、謹查，美國「通貨膨脹削減法」(IRA)就潔淨能源車輛之 

稅額扣抵訂有相關條件，包括：關鍵礦物(critical 

minerals)之3,750美元稅額扣抵，規定一定比例電池之關

鍵礦物需來自美國提煉或加工，或來自與美國訂有自由貿

易協定之國家，或在北美地區回收者，該比例將從40%逐步

提高至2027年80%；電池零件之3,750美元稅額扣抵，規定

一定比例之電池零件需在北美地區製造或組裝，該比例將

從50%逐步提高至2029年100%。

淨能源車輛稅額扣抵之修改部分，重點略以：

(一)取消前述關鍵礦物及電池零件之稅額扣抵，修改為針對

新合格插電式電動車(new qualified plug-in 

electric drive motor)提供2,500美元稅額扣抵，並依

據電池容量額外提供稅額扣抵，至多不超過7,500元。各

製造商最多可獲得20萬輛車之稅額扣抵，包括之前符合

扣抵條件之車輛。

(二)關鍵礦物條件：要求至少80%比例之電池關鍵礦物需在美

國提煉或加工，或來自與美國訂有自由貿易協定之國

家，始可適用稅額扣抵；並規定自由貿易協定為獲國會

批准之國際協定。

(三)電池零件條件：要求全部在北美組裝或加工，始可適用

稅額扣抵。

(四)電動車電池含有來自「有疑慮之外國實體」所開採或加

工之關鍵礦物，將不適用稅額扣抵。

四、謹查，上述法案倘擬順利完成立法，仍需完成以下程序：

1

眾議院委員會通過法案後，將提交眾議院全院委員會辯論

與表決，倘獲全院委員會表決通過後，將提交參議院審

議，經參議院表決通過後，法案須送請總統簽署成為法

律；倘總統不同意簽署，可行使否決權，將法案退回參眾

議院，參眾議院皆須三分之二多數始可推翻總統的否決。

五、依據Inside U.S.Trade本(6)月13日報導：

(一)歲計委員會主席Jason Smith(R-MO)表示，拜登政府制定

過於寬鬆之關鍵礦物規定使中國獲益，透過此法案修改

電動車補貼方式以保護美國供應鏈。

(二)該委員會少數黨首席議員Richard Neal(D-MA)表示，此

法案是不適當的(egregious)，例如該法案為大型企業及

特殊利益團體提供稅額抵減，廢除IRA對中產家庭之能源

稅額扣抵，將阻礙實現氣候目標。

(三)聯邦眾議員Dan Kildee(D-MI)表示，20萬輛車稅額扣抵

的上限將有利於外國汽車製造商，由於許多美國國內製

造商已接近或超過上限，而其他外國公司如起亞、奧

迪、保時捷及賓士等距離上限仍有相當空間。

(四)雖然該法案獲歲計委員會通過，惟本案最終獲眾議院及

參議院通過而順利立法之可能性甚微，因參議院現由民

主黨主導；2022年IRA法案，由兼任參議院議長之副總統

賀錦麗投下關鍵一票，參議院以51票贊成對50票反對，

表決通過。

Ways & Means approves overhaul of IRA EV credits; Democrats disavow | InsideTrade.com

Ways & Means approves overhaul of IRA EV credits; Democrats
disavow

June 14, 2023 at 1:00 PM

Tweet

The House Ways & Means Committee on Tuesday approved the “Build it in America Act,” which would overhaul
the Inflation Reduction Act’s electric vehicle tax incentives, roll back a tax provision that requires companies to
amortize research and development expenses and impose a tax on nationals from a foreign country of concern
when buying U.S. farmland.

Ways & Means Committee Chair Jason Smith (R-MO), who introduced the legislation last week, said during a
markup session on Tuesday that it would protect supply chains from China by eliminating “subsidies for electric
vehicles that have become a windfall for China under the loose critical minerals regulations the Biden
Administration has pushed.”

Committee ranking member Richard Neal (D-MA), though, called the legislation “egregious,” in part because it
would fund tax breaks “to big corporations and special interests by repealing the Inflation Reduction Act’s energy
credits for middle-class families and stunting our climate goals.”

The Inflation Reduction Act created two $3,750 tax credits for electric vehicles, each with assembly and battery
sourcing requirements. The Build it in America Act would instead restore the qualified plug-in electric drive motor
vehicle credit that the Inflation Reduction Act tax credits replaced and cap the access to that credit at 200,000
vehicles per manufacturer.

Reinstating the cap would primarily benefit foreign automakers, Rep. Dan Kildee (D-MI) said during the markup.
Many domestic automakers are close or have already exceeded the 200,000-vehicle cap, Kildee said, while
foreign companies such as Kia, Subaru, Audi, Volvo, Porsche and Mercedes-Benz are the farthest from the cap.

“This bill to me shows that despite the rhetoric, Republicans don’t want to get rid of the EV credit entirely,” Kildee
said. “They just want to maintain the main beneficiaries to be foreign-owned automakers and they’re fine taking the
credit as long as the benefits go mainly to those foreign automakers.”

Kildee also noted that the Inflation Reduction Act created battery and critical mineral requirements to prevent
batteries with Chinese components from qualifying under the tax incentive rules. The Build it in America Act would
require that at least 80 percent of a battery’s critical minerals be extracted or processed in the U.S. or in a country
with which the U.S. has a free trade agreement. It also stipulates that only pacts that Congress approves would be
considered free trade agreements, which would exclude the critical minerals deal the Biden administration recently
struck with Japan and similar agreements. Such deals are intended to ensure cars from those partners can benefit
from the IRA tax credits for EVs.

The Build it in America Act also would roll back a provision of the 2017 Tax Cuts and Jobs Act that went into effect
in 2022, requiring companies to amortize research and development expenses over five years instead of taking
deductions in the years in which those expenses were incurred.

argued that repealing the provision would make the U.S. more competitive with China, which he said has

“Right now, the United States is lagging behind in R&D -- a trend that's been happening for a while,” he said.
