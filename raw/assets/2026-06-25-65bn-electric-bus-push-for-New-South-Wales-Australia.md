# $6.5bn electric bus push for New South Wales

**Author**: Ciaran Daly | **Date**: 24.06.2026

---

The state government of New South Wales, Australia, has announced a major spending programme for electric buses and associated infrastructure. The latest state budget allocates $6.5bn AUD over ten years to purchase 1,700 new electric buses and build 17 charging depots.

The funding bolsters NSW's existing Zero Emission Buses scheme, which aims to transition more than 8,000 ICE-powered public transport buses to zero-emission technology. As of August 2025, approximately 220 electric buses were operating across Sydney, with the state government having purchased a further 921 since March 2023.

The $6.5bn will fund new electric bus purchases as well as depot renovations for charging infrastructure. Sydney's Brookvale depot became the first of 11 depots to receive EV charging infrastructure in September 2025, most recently installing Kempower pantograph chargers — the first of their kind in Australia.

NSW Government statement: "The NSW Government will invest $6.5 billion over 10 years for thousands of new electric buses and electric supported bus depots to reduce our reliance on foreign fuels, meet the needs of the growing community and underpin a revival of domestic bus manufacturing."

The electric bus measure sits alongside the state's new 12-month, $561.4m 'Transport Affordability Package', which also cuts private vehicle registration prices and lowers the weekly toll cap.
