# 2027 BMW i7 Gets More Range And Faster Charging With Neue Klasse Tech

**Author**: Suvrat Kothari | **Date**: 2026-04-22

---

BMW just pulled the wraps off the refreshed 2027 7 Series, and the update goes far deeper than a facelift. The electric version of the flagship now gets a new high-voltage battery, faster charging, and a revised powertrain. The broader 7 Series lineup also gets new software and electrical architecture across all trims.

**Battery and range:** The i7 gets a new battery pack developed with Rimac Technologies, housing sixth-generation cylindrical cells instead of fifth-generation prismatic units. This delivers a 20% improvement in energy density and pushes usable capacity up more than 10% to 112.5 kWh. The i7 60 xDrive can now exceed 350 miles of EPA-estimated range.

**Charging:** The maximum DC charging rate has increased from 195 kW to 250 kW, and at compatible stations, the i7 can go from 10% to 80% in 28 minutes. Its maximum current flow now stands at 630 amps. Every i7 also now ships with a NACS (North American Charging Standard) port as standard, opening up access to the Tesla Supercharger network.

**Drivetrain improvements:** BMW claims the rotors no longer need rare earth magnets and have gained silicon carbide inverters. Optimized wheel bearings together improve overall vehicle efficiency by up to 7%.

**Electrical architecture:** Despite riding on the same Cluster Architecture (CLAR), all 7 Series trims now get the new electrical architecture developed for the Neue Klasse platform. This includes a new centralized computer with 20 times more processing power than the current i7, and a zonal architecture that reduces the wiring harness weight by 30% and length by 2,000 feet.

**Pricing:** The refreshed i7 starts at $106,200 for the 50 xDrive and $124,700 for the 60 xDrive, plus a $1,550 destination charge.
