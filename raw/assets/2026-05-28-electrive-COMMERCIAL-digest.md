# electrive COMMERCIAL — 28 May 2026

**Source**: electrive (newsletter@electrive.com)

> Editorial: While Europe debates charging targets, Latin America has quietly put 10,000+ electric buses on the road (Santiago de Chile alone runs 4,000+). Scania is pushing electric trucking with longer ranges, MCS charging and 750 kW bidirectional transfer — trucks becoming mobile energy assets.

---

## Top
- **Latin America reaches 10,000 electric buses in operation** — Chile, Colombia and Brazil dominate; Chinese makers (BYD, Foton, Yutong) supply most vehicles.

## Bus
- **Manchester orders 125 more electric buses from Wrightbus** for the Bee Network (zero-emission fleet target 2030).
- **Citybus (Hong Kong) orders 20 Wrightbus StreetDeck Electroliner double-deckers** — built in Malaysia, delivered 2027.
- **Volvo Buses secures order for 47 battery-electric buses** — Bergkvara orders Volvo 8900 Electric for Skåne regional and Helsingborg urban transport.
- **BYD delivers 23 electric buses to Mallorca** — EMT Palma, part of a 113-unit programme.
- **Autonomous Karsan e-bus collides with tram in Gothenburg** — self-driving e-ATAK hit a tram an hour after launch; no injuries.
- **India plans major EV incentives for trucks and buses** — programme worth over $1 billion (€862m).

## Truck
- **Scania demonstrates 750 kW bidirectional truck charging** — among the first bidirectional MCS demos for heavy-duty vehicles.
- **China opens first truck battery swap station using Bosch BaaS** — in Chizhou, first customer deployment of Bosch's Battery-as-a-Service.
- **Dongfeng unveils 400 kW hydrogen fuel cell system** — "Hydrogen Core" for 49-tonne trucks, ranges up to 1,700 km.
- **Zeron secures further funding for e-truck expansion** — completed Series B phase 2; ~$400m raised since March.
- **Study finds high satisfaction with electric trucks** — Öko-Institut: 93% of operators highly satisfied.
- **Sany hands over 15 electric trucks to Saptaindra** (Indonesian mining contractor).

## Final
- **Scania opens orders for longer-range e-trucks with MCS** — a seventh battery pack below the cabin enables ranges "far exceeding 800 km"; MCS port now available to order.
