# Jeep And Ram's Owner Partners With Another Chinese Brand To Build EVs In Europe

**Author**: Andrei Nedelea | **Date**: 2026-05-20 | **Source**: InsideEVs

---

Stellantis will build **Dongfeng's Voyah EVs** in France through a new European joint venture. The deal mirrors Stellantis' existing Leapmotor tie-up in Spain, as Chinese automakers increasingly localise production to blunt EU tariffs.

## The Deal

- **Factory**: Stellantis plant in **Rennes, France** — previously produced up to 400,000 vehicles/year, now only making the Citroën C5 Aircross at ~33% capacity
- Dongfeng will build **Voyah** brand EVs there
- In return, Dongfeng will build **Peugeot and Jeep** vehicles in China

## Context: EU Tariff Bypass Strategy

Chinese EVs face an additional EU import duty of up to **35%** (on top of a 10% base tariff). Local assembly eliminates this disadvantage.

Stellantis' Leapmotor precedent:
- Leapmotor T03 was built in Poland (halted March 2025)
- Now builds the **B10 electric crossover** at Stellantis' Zaragoza, Spain factory
- Leapmotor platform and components will underpin a new **Opel electric crossover**

## Broader Pattern

Other Chinese automakers localising in Europe:
- **BYD**: Building a huge factory in Hungary
- **Chery**: Partnered with Spain's Ebro to use the former Nissan plant in Barcelona
- **Xpeng & GAC**: Using Austria's Magna Steyr for European assembly

Europe's tariffs were designed to protect local industry — but may instead be accelerating Chinese EV brands gaining a European manufacturing foothold.
