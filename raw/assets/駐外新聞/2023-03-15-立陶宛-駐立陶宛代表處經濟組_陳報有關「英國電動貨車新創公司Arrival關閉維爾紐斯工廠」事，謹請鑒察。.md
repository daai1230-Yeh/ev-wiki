In order to reduce costs, British commercial electric vehicle production start-up Arrival, which has ﬁnancial problems,

is closing Vilnius experimental electric vehicle drive manufacturing company Arrival LT. The technology unicorn is

changing its strategy and will try to establish itself in the US.

"Not only the company in Vilnius is being closed, but also in other countries, employees are being laid oﬀ," conﬁrms

Mantas Žukauskas, manager of UAB "Arrival LT".

The Employment Service was notiﬁed that Arrival LT is laying oﬀ all 42 employees: technology, production and

mechanical engineers, operators of metallurgy technological process control equipment, purchasing specialists.

Currently, there are only 2 people left in the company.

It is not yet clear how the shareholder will deal with the property in Lithuania.

Mantas Žukauskas, manager of UAB "Arrival LT" in liquidation.

Mr. Žukauskas will be involved in the liquidation of the company for the time being, and later, he does not rule out

that he will use the work to design electric motors.

Moving to the USA

The Russian-based electric car maker is laying oﬀ about 50% of its 1,600 employees in various countries. Until 2023

at the end of March, the company will have less than 800 employees.

As a result of job cuts and other cost reduction measures, the company's operating costs should be halved - up to no

more than 35 million. USD per quarter, which signiﬁcantly reduces the amount of investment needed to ﬁnance the

business this year.

"Arrival has changed its strategy and will now focus on the US to take advantage of green energy subsidies," writes

theguardian.com . 

It plans to start production in Charlotte, North Carolina, "subject to raising additional capital." The start of production

in Charlotte is scheduled for 2024. at the end

The latest job cuts are the ﬁrst move by Igor Torgov, who joined the company last February and has now assumed

the role of CEO.

I. Torgov replaced the interim director when Arrival's founder, Russian billionaire Denis Sverdlov, resigned.

in 2022 in April, Arrival predicted that 600 electric vans would be built for European customers. in 2022 in September

this number was reduced to 20. Only a few vehicles are now being produced in the UK, which are dedicated to

vehicle and production testing.

Tax measures play the most important role in moving to the US. Subsidies for electric vehicles range from $7,500 to

$40,000. Arrival plans to produce electric buses and vans in the US.

Stuck in losses

On Monday, Arrival announced a $300 million USD equity ﬁnancing facility with Westwood Capital, subject to certain

conditions being met. It also announced the convening of an extraordinary general meeting of shareholders, which

will vote on several resolutions, including a reverse stock split and capital reduction.

According to preliminary data, it is likely that in 2022 the loss can be 0.998-1.008 billion USD, when last year it

reached 1.304 billion. USD.

The expected adjusted EBITDA loss for this year is 379-380 million. USD, compared to an adjusted USD 203 million.

The company does not hide that, despite the actions taken so far, there are still signiﬁcant doubts about the ability to

continue operations, as it is necessary to continue to increase capital in order for the company to achieve

proﬁtability.

At best, Arrival won't start generating revenue until 2024 if it successfully starts selling electric vans in the US. But

ﬁnancing the operation will be diﬃcult, as competition in the electric van segment in the US has increased

dramatically in the past few years. The tone is set by companies such as General Motors and Rivian, which managed

to start mass production of electric vans in the US last summer, designed for IT giant Amazon's delivery service.
