# VW Group starts production of new MEBeco electric drive

**Author**: Florian Treiss | **Date**: 2026-06-19

---

Audi's plant in Győr, Hungary, has started series production of the new MEBeco electric drive system (APP290). This will first power the new Cupra Raval, followed by VW Group's new electric small car family.

The plant has been preparing for MEBeco production since 2022, supported by a total investment of €350 million (with €22.5 million from the Hungarian government). The conversion secures 260 jobs, with operations expected to ramp from one to three shifts per day.

The MEBeco powertrain is part of the MEB+ platform, an evolution of VW Group's MEB platform. A key advancement is increased vertical integration: for the first time in Győr, the plant manufactures not only complete powertrain units but also key components—laminated core, rotor, power electronics—including software and final testing.

## APP290 Specifications

The APP290 takes its name from its maximum torque output of 290 Nm. In the Cupra Raval it will be offered in four power levels: 85 kW, 99 kW, 155 kW and 166 kW (top VZ variant). The Raval VZ accelerates 0–100 km/h in 6.8 seconds and has a top speed of 175 km/h.

## Vehicle Rollout

The first vehicle to use the MEBeco/APP290 is the all-electric Cupra Raval (built in Martorell, Spain). Upcoming models using this powertrain include: VW ID. Polo and its GTI variant, VW ID. Cross (SUV), and Skoda Epiq.
