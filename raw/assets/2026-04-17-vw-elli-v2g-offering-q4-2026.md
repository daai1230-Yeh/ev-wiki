# VW and Elli to launch V2G offering in Q4 2026

**Author**: Florian Treiss | **Date**: 2026-04-17

---

Volkswagen has announced that it will launch a V2G offering in Germany before the end of this year. In collaboration with its in-house energy brand Elli, it will soon be possible to feed electricity from the batteries of current VW electric vehicles back into the public grid, thereby reducing costs.

Bidirectional charging, and particularly vehicle-to-grid (V2G), is considered a key element of the energy transition. It allows excess electricity from the grid to be stored in EV traction batteries during periods of oversupply and fed back into the grid when demand is especially high. Similar offerings have recently been launched by BMW with E.ON and Ford with Octopus Energy; The Mobility House, together with Mercedes-Benz and Renault, has also announced similar plans.

Until the end of last year, regulatory barriers in Germany stood in the way of bidirectional charging—these have now been resolved. VW's ID. EV family has been V2G-capable since 2023, making it ready for commercial rollout.

Elli and VW are targeting a market launch in Q4 2026, with pre-registration beginning as early as June. The rollout will initially focus on selected markets, with further European countries to follow.

According to Elli, customers could save between €700 and €900 annually under optimal conditions, assuming they regularly connect their VW EV to a home wallbox for several hours per day. In the long term, the initiative aims to enable near cost-neutral charging.

Martin Sander, VW Board Member for Sales, stated: "Electric mobility can only realize its full potential if it also makes economic sense for our customers. With Vehicle-to-Grid, we are delivering exactly that: significantly lower home charging costs – and, in the long term, nearly cost-free charging."

Giovanni Palazzo, CEO of Elli, added: "Vehicle-to-Grid delivers its full value only through the connection of vehicles, infrastructure and the energy market. Today, we have the technological and operational capabilities to scale an integrated solution across millions of vehicles and brands. We are making our vision of a 'Managed Battery Network' a tangible reality for the first time."

To use the offering, customers will need a dynamic electricity tariff, a DC bidirectional wallbox, a smart meter, and the Elli app. Elli acts as the link between the vehicle, customers, and the energy market, with its own energy trading team operating 24/7 on the EPEX Spot exchange. In practice, this could translate into free electricity for around 10,000 kilometres of driving per year, or direct financial credits.

The concept is based on aggregating large numbers of EVs into a virtual power plant, monetising stored energy through arbitrage on the electricity market and grid-stabilising services.
