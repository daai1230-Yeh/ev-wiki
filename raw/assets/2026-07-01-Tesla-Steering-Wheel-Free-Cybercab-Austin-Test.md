# Tesla Starts Testing Steering-Wheel-Free Cybercab On Austin Streets. Can It Match Waymo?

**Author**: Mack Hogan | **Date**: Jun 30, 2026

---

Tesla has begun testing a steering-wheel-free version of its Cybercab robotaxi in Austin, Texas, the company announced Monday on X, via a 27-second video showing a safety monitor sitting in the right seat of the Cybercab — effectively a passenger seat in this version, since there's no driving position.

The Cybercab was designed to have a cabin free of traditional driving controls; CEO Elon Musk confirmed that intention at its launch event in October 2024. But until now, the prototypes seen testing in public have featured traditional steering wheels and pedals, which isn't an ideal setup for a two-seat robotaxi.

The no-wheel design poses its own challenges. The robotaxis that both Tesla and Waymo have deployed commercially feature steering wheels and pedals as backup options, so first responders or company employees can maneuver the cars if necessary, and they can typically be remotely piloted from a control center — which is how Amazon-owned Zoox launched autonomous robotaxis without a steering wheel or pedals.

That approach won't be as easy for the Cybercab if Tesla delivers on its promise to sell Cybercabs to private customers — a departure from rivals who operate their own robotaxi fleets and can remotely control or retrieve them. If a privately owned Cybercab has damaged cameras or a software issue, it's unclear whether the owner would be able to move the car.

Tesla typically drip-feeds details, and while there's a safety driver in the released video, it's unknown whether or how they can control the vehicle. The company has said the Cybercab will be available before 2027, so its timeline is tight if it wants Full Self-Driving good enough to eliminate the need for a steering wheel or pedals.
