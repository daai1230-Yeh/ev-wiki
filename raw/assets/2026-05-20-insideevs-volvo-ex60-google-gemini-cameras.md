# The Volvo EX60 Can 'See' The World And Chat With You About It

**Author**: Iulian Dnistran | **Date**: 2026-05-20

---

Volvo and Google have teamed up to demonstrate how Gemini AI can integrate with a vehicle's cameras. The new EX60 electric crossover is the first car where Google's Gemini AI assistant can chat with occupants about their surroundings — though the feature has not been publicly released yet.

Demonstrated at Google I/O, the Gemini assistant in the EX60 can grab information directly from the car's cameras, interpret it in real time, and answer driver or passenger questions. Examples shown:
- Driver asks about a foreign-language road sign → Gemini scans, translates, and explains it
- Driver asks about a tall building visible ahead → Gemini identifies it as the Transamerica Pyramid in San Francisco, explains it's mainly offices (not a tourist spot), notes there's a park at its base, and offers to plan a route nearby

The system is similar to Google Lens on a smartphone, but supercharged with Gemini and natural voice commands. It relies on the EX60's neural processing engine and software-defined architecture, plus Gemini's multi-modal understanding.

**Privacy:** The feature is opt-in (requires driver's permission), and faces are blurred when images are analysed.

**Coming soon to EX60 and other Google Built-In cars:**
- Car-optimised YouTube Music
- 3D Immersive Navigation in Google Maps (debuted on Android smartphones, rolling to Android Auto, then to cars)
- Deeper Gemini integration to control vehicle features via voice

The first Volvo models to receive Immersive Navigation in Google Maps will be the EX60, EX90, and ES90.
