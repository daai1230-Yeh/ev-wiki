## This week's overview

This edition opens with Germany's launch of a new programme supporting at least 1,500 electric buses, Hamburg securing over €1.1 billion in EIB financing, and EMT Palma adding new BYD–Castrosua vehicles. At the Next Mobility Exhibition 2026 (Milan, 13–16 May, Fiera Milano Rho), Yutong showcased long-life EV technologies and integrated electric architecture, while Menarini outlined plans for a full 6-to-18m electric range and proprietary batteries. The issue also covers charging infrastructure projects in Seattle, major electric bus contracts in China and Hong Kong, India's proposed $1 billion incentive plan, Malta's investment in electric buses and autonomous shuttles, new onboard video technologies from Luminator, Bologna's Solaris hydrogen buses entering service, and Optibus expanding demand-responsive planning tools.

## Top news

- Germany's transport ministry has launched a new funding programme aimed at supporting the deployment of at least 1,500 electric buses.

## Main news

- EMT Palma receives 23 BYD–Castrosua e-buses as part of a 113-vehicle zero-emission fleet renewal plan.
- Schunk supplies 123 depot charging pantographs to King County Metro in Seattle.
- Hamburg secures over €1.1 billion EIB financing for power grid expansion and 188 electric buses.

## Highlights

- Yutong presented its latest battery-electric buses and EV technologies at Next Mobility Exhibition 2026 in Milan, focusing on long-life components, integrated electric architecture and fleet digitalization (four battery-electric models showcased).
- Menarini outlined plans for a full 6-to-18m electric range with proprietary batteries.
- Optibus expanding demand-responsive planning tools for public transport operators amid European staffing pressures.
- Additional coverage: India's proposed $1 billion e-bus incentive plan, electric bus contracts in China and Hong Kong, Malta's electric buses and autonomous shuttles, Luminator onboard video technologies, and Bologna's Solaris hydrogen buses entering service.

Companies covered this issue: Germany e-bus funding round, BYD, Castrosua, Schunk, Hamburg, Yutong, Menarini, Irizar, India, Wrightbus, Skywell, VinFast, Optibus, Luminator.
