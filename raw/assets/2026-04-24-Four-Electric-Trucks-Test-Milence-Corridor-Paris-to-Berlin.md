# Four electric trucks test Milence corridor from Paris to Berlin

**Author**: Cora Werwitzke | **Date**: April 23, 2026

---

The truck charging joint venture Milence has opened a new truck charging park on the motorway near Kassel-Lohfelden in Germany – and its chargers were immediately put to the test this week. A demo tour with electric trucks from Daimler Truck, MAN, Volvo Trucks, and Renault Trucks stopped at the new location on their 1,000-kilometre route from Paris to Berlin.

The charging park in Kassel-Lohfelden in central Germany is Milence's ninth location in the country. Situated along the A7 and A49 motorways, it is integrated into the established HGV site SVG Autohof Lohfeldener Rüssel. Four charging bays, each equipped with 400 kW CCS connectors, are available on-site. Notably, the Kassel-Lohfelden charging park is the first within the Milence network to integrate a stationary battery storage system into the charging ecosystem — demonstrating how storage technology enables expansion even at locations where sufficient grid connectivity has previously been lacking.

Milence is a joint venture established in 2022 by Daimler Truck, Traton, and the Volvo Group to create a Europe-wide public charging network for HGVs.

As part of the roadshow "Power to Go Further," four battery-electric trucks from Daimler Truck, MAN, Volvo Trucks, and Renault Trucks covered a 1,000-kilometre route from Paris to Berlin in several stages since 15 April, charging exclusively at Milence charging parks along the way. The tour followed public Milence charging points across four countries — France, Belgium, Netherlands, and Germany — and concluded in Berlin on Thursday. The German manufacturers participated with the Mercedes-Benz eActros 600 and the MAN eTGS ultra-trailer tractor. Volvo Trucks and Renault Trucks used the current-generation Renault Trucks E-Tech T and Volvo FH Electric.

Daimler Truck test engineer Dirk Stranz confirmed that the longest stage was around 320 to 350 kilometres, which the eActros 600 handled effortlessly with a partial load of 32 tonnes. He also noted that battery-electric trucks are even easier to control than diesel trucks, especially during precise manoeuvres.

Milence CEO Anja van Niersen stated: "The 'Power to Go Further Tour' marks a milestone for Milence. It demonstrates the impressive progress of electric road transport towards sustainable operations."

Currently, the Milence network comprises 34 operational charging hubs with 221 charging points across eight countries. The company aims to have 50 operational charging parks across 10 European markets by the end of this year. By the end of 2028, the goal is 90 charging parks and 284 MCS charging points at 71 locations.

The CPO has published newly calculated operating costs: €0.995 per kilometre for electric models compared to around €1.003 per kilometre for diesel — meaning electric trucks are now cheaper to operate than diesel on key European corridors. When using renewable energy, the joint venture estimates a CO₂ saving of up to 1,470 kg per truck per run.
