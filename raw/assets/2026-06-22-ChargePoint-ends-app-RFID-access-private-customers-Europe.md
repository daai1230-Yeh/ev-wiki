# ChargePoint ends app and RFID access for private customers in Europe

**Author**: Daniel Bönnighausen | **Date**: 2026-06-19

---

ChargePoint is restricting use of its app and RFID card in Europe. As of June 30, private customers will no longer be able to start and pay for charging sessions through these methods. Access will be reserved for users registered via employers, fleet managers, charging point operators, station owners, or leasing providers.

Affected users have been notified by email. According to the message, the ChargePoint app and RFID card will soon be available in Europe only to drivers whose charging access is provided through an employer, fleet operator or leasing company. Private EV drivers will no longer be able to use the ChargePoint app or RFID card to locate charging stations, initiate charging sessions or process payments.

Customer accounts will be permanently closed on 30 June 2026. Users with remaining funds in their ChargePoint Wallet will be able to request a refund to their registered payment method, though the reimbursement process may take up to 90 days.

ChargePoint emphasises that the change does not affect access to charging infrastructure itself. Drivers impacted by the discontinuation will still be able to charge at ChargePoint-operated stations using contactless bank cards, roaming services or third-party charging apps.

From ChargePoint's perspective, this change reflects a strategic shift. The company aims to concentrate its European app on users 'who are set up through their employer, fleet manager, or car leasing provider.' This aligns with its pivot towards commercial applications, fleets, and charge point operators.

ChargePoint CEO Rick Wilmer stated Europe is set to become increasingly important for the company, with a long-term goal of increasing the European revenue share from ~25% (Q4 2025) to 50% or more—focused on the fleet and CPO segments.

For private users, those who have used the ChargePoint app as a standalone charging solution or the RFID card as an access method must find alternatives by end of June 2026.
