# India to slash tariffs on UK-made cars starting in July

**Author**: Sagar Parikh | **Date**: 2026-06-20

---

Nearly a year after signing their Free Trade Agreement (FTA), India and the UK are set to bring it into effect on 15 July. The FTA will make UK-built cars imported to India cheaper, starting with ICE models and eventually including hybrid, electric, and hydrogen vehicles.

## India's concessions for UK-made ICE cars

India will lower customs duty on ICE cars from as high as 110% to as low as 10%, based on engine capacity and fuel type, implemented in phases starting at 30% in year one, reaching 10% by year five. India has set a quota of 378,000 ICE cars for the first 15 years (15,000–37,000 units/year).

## India's concessions for UK-made electric/hybrid cars

For hybrid, electric, and hydrogen vehicles, India adopts a protectionist measure—tariff reductions only begin in year six (approximately 2031). Over 10 years, up to 137,500 units may be imported at reduced tariffs (4,400–22,000 units/year). India requires a minimum CIF value of £40,000 for reduced tariffs, protecting the domestic lower-priced segment.

Key tariff milestones for EVs priced £40,000–80,000:
- Year 6: 50% → Year 10: 10%

For models above £80,000 (e.g., Range Rover Electric, Rolls-Royce Spectre):
- Year 6: 40% → Year 10: 10%

## UK's concessions for Indian-made EVs

The UK will eliminate customs duty on India-made hybrid, electric, and hydrogen vehicles from year six, subject to an annual quota (17,600–88,000 units/year over 10 years). Total quota over 10 years: up to 550,000 units (zero tariff). Models priced above £80,000 are ineligible for the concession.

This opens significant opportunities for Indian automakers like Maruti Suzuki (which has already exported ~36,000 eVITARA units to Europe), Mahindra, and Tata Motors to access the UK EV market.
