Trump gives EU until July 4 to cut tariffs
before auto duty hike kicks in

By Brett Fortnam (/authors/Brett-Fortnam)  / May 7, 2026 at 3:42 PM

Post

Share

President Trump on Thursday gave the European Union until July 4 to cut tariffs in line with the trade deal it
struck with U.S. last summer before he raises tariffs on European autos and auto parts.

Last week, Trump said he would raise tariffs on European autos (/node/186525) and auto parts to 25
percent because the EU has not yet implemented the agreement he and Commission President Ursula von der
Leyen agreed to in Turnberry, Scotland. The two leaders spoke on the phone on Thursday to discuss a range of
issues, including tariffs and Iran, he said in a social media post
(https://truthsocial.com/@realDonaldTrump/posts/116500111621281950).

“We discussed many topics, including that we are completely united that Iran can never have a Nuclear Weapon,”
he said. “We agreed that a regime that kills its own people cannot control a bomb that can kill millions. I’ve been
waiting patiently for the EU to fulfill their side of the Historic Trade Deal we agreed in Turnberry, Scotland, the
largest Trade Deal, ever! A promise was made that the EU would deliver their side of the Deal and, as per
Agreement, cut their Tariffs to ZERO! I agreed to give her until our Country’s 250th Birthday or, unfortunately,
their Tariffs would immediately jump to much higher levels.”

As part of the deal, the EU agreed to eliminate tariffs on U.S. industrial goods. But the bloc has yet to enact those
tariff cuts as EU institutions negotiate legislation on how to implement the trade pact. The European Parliament
and EU member states have called for the deal to include additional provisions, including a safeguard clause to
protect EU industries from surges in U.S. imports.

European negotiators met for six hours (/node/186580) on Wednesday and are slated to convene later this
month for another round of talks. European Trade Commissioner Maroš Šefčovič earlier this week called for the
EU to implement its end of the deal by the summer (/node/186555).

European Parliament International Trade Chair Bernd Lange on Monday claimed Trump’s threat to raise tariffs
was made because he was “really angry with Germany.” German Chancellor Friedrich Merz last week said the U.S.
was being humiliated by Iran in the war between the two.

“I’m convinced that in this context he also is targeting specifically German car manufacturers,” Lange said in an

 
interview with Euronews (https://x.com/euronews/status/2051202355933126700).

The U.S. has imposed 25 percent tariffs on foreign autos and auto parts under Section 232 of the Trade Expansion
Act of 1962. It agreed to cap its tariffs on European autos and auto parts at 15 percent as part of the EU trade deal.
The U.S. last September lowered those tariffs to 15 percent retroactive to Aug. 1, 2025, per the terms of the
agreement.

“Of course there are no legal or economic reasons” for the tariffs, Lange said. “This is really politically against
Germany.” -- Brett Fortnam (bfortnam@iwpnews.com (mailto:bfortnam@iwpnews.com))
