# Honda withdraws e:Ny1 from multiple markets

**Author**: Sebastian Schaal | **Date**: 2026-04-17

---

Only in 2023 did Honda introduce the e:Ny1 to the market, and now the electric SUV is already facing discontinuation. In the UK, Germany, Italy, Spain, and Switzerland, the e:Ny1 has disappeared from the manufacturer's website. In France and Austria, customers can still configure the model.

Honda Motor Company developed the compact electric SUV specifically for Europe to meet growing demand for fully electric B-segment SUVs. However, the model has fallen short of expectations and failed to generate significant sales volumes in most markets.

**Technically,** the e:Ny1 is based on Honda's e:N Architecture F (front-motor-drive platform). The electric motor delivers 150 kW and 310 Nm of torque, paired with a 68.8 kWh battery, providing a WLTP range of 412 km. A standard 10–80% charge takes a substantial 45 minutes.

The motor is the EMR3 from Vitesco Technologies—notably, not the latest at launch (the successor EMR4 was introduced in 2021). Despite relatively modest specs and ranges by 2023 standards, the e:Ny1 launched at €47,590 in Germany, considered expensive. The price was later reduced to €38,990, but this still failed to attract customers.

**Sales data in Germany:** 2023 (limited availability): 266 units → 2024: 795 units → 2025: just 105 units—underscoring its niche status.

Honda has not issued an official statement on the discontinuation, but the removal from manufacturer websites in key markets sends a clear signal. The days of the e:Ny1 appear to be numbered.
