# InsideEVs Critical Materials: EV Week in Review – June 20, 2026

**Newsletter**: InsideEVs Critical Materials (weekly) | **Date**: June 20, 2026

---

## EV News

**Ferrari Luce allocation strategy**: Buying the new (and controversially-styled) Ferrari Luce could help move buyers up in line for coveted Ferrari models. Ferrari is reportedly linking EV purchases to customer allocation priority for other models.

**Mercedes CLA EV**: Mack Hogan reviewed the new Mercedes CLA EV — some things he loved, but some parts of the experience were "maddening."

**Porsche Taycan 2027**: Porsche originally said fake shifting would make EVs "worse." Now it's adding the feature to the new 2027 Taycan, along with a bigger standard battery.

**BMW i3 sedan**: BMW is opening orders ahead of schedule due to high demand. The electric 3-Series will offer 440 miles of EPA range and 400 kW charging.

**Rivian R2 Performance**: More efficient than a Tesla Model Y Performance — despite being boxier and 500 pounds heavier. (EPA: 330 miles, 32 kWh/100 mi vs Model Y's 306 miles at same efficiency.)

**Rivian layoffs**: Rivian laid off "hundreds" of employees (less than 2% of staff). Also faced a class action lawsuit claiming it didn't keep its promise to bring Level 3 driving tech to first-gen R1 vehicles.

**Lucid Cosmos**: Lucid's roughly $50,000 Cosmos will be its first EV with potential for mass appeal. Design revealed through European patent filings.

## Battery & Charging News

**Electrify America battery backups**: Betting on battery storage at DC fast charging sites — charge during off-peak hours, discharge during peak demand, reducing grid stress and potentially costs.

**250,000 US charging points**: America now has over 250,000 charging points across Level 1, Level 2, and DC fast charging.

**GM silicon anodes**: Silicon-anode tech will help EVs drive farther and charge faster as a bridge until solid-state is ready. (See separate article.)

**Telo MT1 truck**: Uses 800-volt architecture, charging its 106-kWh pack at 400 kW.

**Honda + QuantumScape**: Honda has a new partner for solid-state batteries. (See separate article.)

## Autonomy News

**Waymo second recall**: Addressing robotaxis that could drive into closed highway construction lanes. (First recall was for cars entering flooded roads.) Waymo also launched a premium membership tier with shorter wait times and priority access to new cities.

**Tesla Cybercab**: Packs an "extremely tiny battery," making it the lightest, most efficient Tesla ever — 30% more efficient than the Lucid Air.

**Uber robotaxis**: Uber's deal with Stellantis, Wayve brings new partners to its robotaxi ambitions. Its premium robotaxi service with Lucid and Nuro will launch in Houston in 2027 (after SF Bay Area this year).

## Feature: Xiaomi SU7 Ultra Review (Kevin Williams)

Kevin Williams drove the Xiaomi SU7 Ultra — a tri-motor AWD sedan with 1,500 horsepower — at a track in China. Price: ~$75,000.

Key impressions:
- Extraordinary power that never feels out of its depth
- "Incredibly forgiving" — corrects errors from even unskilled drivers
- Set Nurburgring records
- Unlikely to reach the US; will make waves when it arrives in Europe
