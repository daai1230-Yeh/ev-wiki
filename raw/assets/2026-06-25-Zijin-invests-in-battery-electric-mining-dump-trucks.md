# Zijin invests in proprietary battery-electric mining dump trucks

**Author**: guest author | **Date**: 24.06.2026

---

Chinese mining giant Zijin Mining is advancing electrification of its vehicle fleets. The company now deploys battery-electric dump trucks at multiple sites and even develops some of the vehicles in-house.

Zijin Mining is one of China's largest producers of gold, copper and zinc. After finding no suitable vehicles on the market, the company established its own production line for battery-electric mining dump trucks. By end of 2025, the first model — the LK220E — had rolled off the line. Zijin now operates nearly 300 battery-electric dump trucks weighing up to 140 tonnes, equipped with swappable 776 kWh batteries.

The first LK220E vehicles were delivered to the Julong copper mine in May, located at ~5,300m altitude. Results: over 90% availability rate and 17%+ reduction in energy consumption vs. comparable diesel models. Zijin is also developing the larger LK350E and autonomous LK110EI trucks.

At the Xinjiang Zijin zinc mine, more than 290 electric trucks from multiple manufacturers (including Lingong Heavy Machinery) now handle over 80% of transport volume. The mine operates a 'Zero Carbon Transport Loop' powered by local wind, solar, and storage systems, plus an AI-powered battery-swapping system capable of 180+ swaps/day — each taking ~4 minutes, operable in -40°C to +40°C.

A digital fleet management system monitors battery status and safety in real time, reducing speeding violations by 75%. These learnings will be applied to other open-pit mining sites globally.
