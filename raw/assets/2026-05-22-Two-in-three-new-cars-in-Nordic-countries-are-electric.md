# Two in three new cars in Nordic countries are electric

**Author**: Daniel Bönnighausen | **Date**: 2026-05-21

---

As Nordic countries hit the overall mark of two out of three newly registered passenger cars in April being purely electric, Norway and Denmark in particular are driving up the electric car share with 98.6% and 81.9%, respectively. In Finland and Sweden, the shares were significantly lower at 48.8% and 42%.

## April 2026 BEV Registration Shares

| Country | BEV Share (April 2026) | Notes |
|---------|----------------------|-------|
| Norway | 98.6% | 11,103 total registrations |
| Denmark | 81.9% | Up from 63.3% a year ago; private sales at 96.3% |
| Finland | 48.8% | |
| Sweden | 42.0% | |

## Top-Selling Models by Country

- **Norway**: Volkswagen ID.4 topped the model rankings
- **Denmark**: Škoda Elroq achieved the highest new registrations
- **Sweden**: Volvo EX40 led the list
- **Finland**: Toyota Yaris Cross first; Škoda Enyaq (BEV) in second

In Norway, all models in the top-ten list are fully electric, whereas in Denmark, Sweden, and Finland, internal combustion engine and hybrid models still feature among the ten most popular passenger cars.

While Norway has practically become a fully electric new car market, and Denmark is not far behind, Sweden and Finland continue to present a more mixed picture. Even so, electric vehicles are inexorably gaining ground in new car markets.
