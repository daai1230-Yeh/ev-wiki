# BEVs over Hydrogen: South Korea considers reallocating subsidies

**Author**: Florian Treiss | **Date**: 24.06.2026

---

The South Korean government is apparently considering reallocating household funds earmarked for hydrogen cars and hydrogen filling stations to EV subsidies instead. This would benefit the current EV incentive program, the budget of which would otherwise likely be exhausted by August.

South Korea's subsidy budget for battery-electric cars – totalling 1.7454 trillion Won (~€1 billion) – is at risk of premature exhaustion. This is partly due to rising fuel prices caused by the conflict in Iran. Significantly more South Koreans are purchasing BEVs this year: the Ministry of Trade, Industry and Energy reports a 125.3% increase, with 162,026 BEVs sold in the first five months of the year.

Climate Minister Kim Seong-hwan recently stated: "From a people-centered perspective, regardless of how much budget is allocated, it is right to allow consumers to buy as many vehicles as they want."

Potential candidates for budget reallocation include:
- Hydrogen car subsidies: 576.2 billion Won (~€330 million)
- EV charging station expansion: 451.75 billion Won (~€260 million)
- Hydrogen refuelling station development: 174.3 billion Won (~€100 million)

South Korea currently offers subsidies of 5.3–5.8 million Won (~€3,000–€3,300) per BEV purchase. Eligible vehicles include the Hyundai Ioniq 6, Kia EV3, Tesla Model 3 and Y, and BYD Dolphin and Seal. Restricting subsidies for foreign models has been under discussion.
