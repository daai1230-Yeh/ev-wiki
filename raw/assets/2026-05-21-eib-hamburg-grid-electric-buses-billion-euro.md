# EIB loans billion-euro sum for Hamburg's distribution grid and electric buses

**Author**: Cora Werwitzke | **Date**: 2026-05-20

---

The European Investment Bank (EIB) is providing Hamburger Energienetze (HNE) with up to **€1 billion** for modernising Hamburg's electricity distribution grid. Verkehrsbetriebe Hamburg-Holstein (VHH) will also receive approximately **€125 million** for electric buses.

## Electric Bus Procurement (VHH)

VHH plans to procure **188 new battery-electric buses**:
- 120 standard buses
- 52 articulated buses
- 16 minibuses

Currently around one-third of VHH's ~800-vehicle fleet operates electrically. Since 2020, the company has exclusively purchased zero-emission buses. The new vehicles will significantly increase the electric share. Funding also covers redevelopment and expansion of the Schenefeld depot.

Total VHH electromobility investment over coming years: up to **€337 million**, with EIB covering ~37% (€125 million).

## Grid Modernisation (HNE)

HNE's investment programme includes:
- ~1,500 km of new and renewed cables (low, medium, high voltage)
- New and renewed substations plus expanded digital control technology
- 120 new switchgear bays
- Digitalisation of grid infrastructure to integrate renewables

HNE plans to invest ~**€2.9 billion** in grid expansion and modernisation by 2029. The EIB €1 billion loan covers ~28% of total financing. Hamburg's grid peak load is expected to **at least double by 2040**.

## Strategic Alignment

Both companies are subsidiaries of HGV (Hamburg's municipal holding company). The EIB loan agreements were officially signed on 19 May 2026. The first tranche was already disbursed in October 2025.

EIB Vice-President Nicola Beer: "Hamburg demonstrates how Europe's cities can holistically transform their infrastructure: with robust power grids and electrified public transport."
