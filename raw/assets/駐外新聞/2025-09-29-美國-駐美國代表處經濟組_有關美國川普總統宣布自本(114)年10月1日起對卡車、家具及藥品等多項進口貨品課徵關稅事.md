主旨：有關美國川普總統宣布自本(114)年10月1日起對卡車、家

具及藥品等多項進口貨品課徵關稅事，謹請鑒察。

說明：

一、本組本年9月26日經美字第1140000977號函諒達。

二、美國川普總統於本年9月25日於社群媒體宣布，基於國家安

全考量，自本年10月1日起對以下產品課徵關稅：

(一)重型卡車(heavy truck)：25%。

(二)廚房櫥櫃(kitchen cabinets)、浴室盥洗台(bathroom 

vanities)及相關產品：50%。

(三)軟墊家具(upholstered furniture)：30%。

(四)品牌、專利藥品(branded or patented 

pharmaceutical product)：100%，但已於美國動工或建

設中之製藥廠可豁免關稅。

三、針對藥品關稅，美國白宮官員澄清，將不適用對已與美國

達成貿易協議之國家，如歐盟將適用15%上限之稅率、日本

之稅率將不高於對歐盟等其他任何貿易夥伴所適用之稅

By Margaret Spiegelman  / September 25, 2025 at 8:45 PM

President Trump on Thursday evening announced plans to impose duties next month on several kinds of
goods subject to national security probes, including trucks, kitchen cabinets, pharmaceuticals and more.

The goods are among a slew of products covered by a growing list of investigations under Section 232 of
the Trade Expansion Act of 1962, which the administration has said could lead to new tariffs.

Trump unveiled the plan in a series of social media posts. Truck imports, he said, will soon face 25
percent duties.

“In order to protect our Great Heavy Truck Manufacturers from unfair outside competition, I will be
imposing, as of October 1st, 2025, a 25% Tariff on all ‘Heavy (Big!) Trucks’ made in other parts of the
World,” he wrote. “Therefore, our Great Large Truck Company Manufacturers, such as Peterbilt,
Kenworth, Freightliner, Mack Trucks, and others, will be protected from the onslaught of outside
interruptions. We need our Truckers to be financially healthy and strong, for many reasons, but above all
else, for National Security purposes!”

In a second post, he said the U.S. will impose 50 percent tariffs on “all Kitchen Cabinets, Bathroom
Vanities, and associated products, starting October 1st, 2025.”

“Additionally, we will be charging a 30% Tariff on Upholstered Furniture,” that post continues, adding
that such tariffs are needed to address “the large scale ‘FLOODING’ of these products into the United
States by other outside Countries. It is a very unfair practice, but we must protect, for National Security
and other reasons, our Manufacturing process.”

Trump last month announced that the administration was investigating furniture imports, and a White
House official at the time said it was part of a broader investigation into timber, lumber and derivative
products. The president's posts on Thursday about new duties do not mention timber or lumber.

A third post, meanwhile, promises a “100% Tariff on any branded or patented Pharmaceutical Product,
unless a Company IS BUILDING their Pharmaceutical Manufacturing Plant in America,” also set to
begin on Oct. 1. “IS BUILDING,” Trump adds, “will be defined as, ’breaking ground’ and/or ‘under
construction.’”

The president in August said tariffs on pharmaceuticals would be phased in, starting “small” and rising
to 250 percent.

He has promised a similar exemption from duties on chips to companies that are investing in the U.S.

President Donald Trump’s new tariffs on pharmaceutical imports will not apply to

countries with negotiated agreements with the US that contain provisions on drugs,

according to a White House official, delivering promised relief to economies including
the European Union and Japan.

Duties on pharmaceuticals from the EU will be capped at 15% per the terms of its
framework deal, the official said Friday. Japanese drugs will also be charged the rate

spelled out in its pact, the official added. The US-Japan joint statement says American
tariff rates on Japanese drugs and semiconductors should not exceed those applied to
others, including the EU.

The UK, another country that exports pharmaceuticals to the US, would face the 100%

tariff, according to a White House official. London also negotiated a trade agreement
with Washington that included provisions for similar exemptions, but the two countries
have not yet agreed on a rate for pharmaceuticals.
