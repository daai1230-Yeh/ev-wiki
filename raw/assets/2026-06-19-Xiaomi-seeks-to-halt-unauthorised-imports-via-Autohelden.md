# Autohelden launches direct imports for Xiaomi and other brands (Xiaomi takes legal action)

**Author**: Cora Werwitzke | **Date**: 17.06.2026 (updated 18.06.2026)

---

The automotive parts platform Autohelden plans to bring Xiaomi's all-electric cars to Germany and Europe — along with models from Zeekr, Jetour, and Avatr — targeting ~50,000 vehicles in the first full year.

Autohelden MD Christoph Wicke confirmed the process chain is complete. The importer will handle customs clearance and TÜV approval, with distribution via 80–100 locations across Europe. Models planned: Xiaomi SU7 and YU7, Zeekr 8X/9X, Avatr 06/07/11/12, Jetour X70/T2.

Xiaomi officially plans to sell globally only from 2027.

**Caveats for buyers**: Imported vehicles use the Chinese GB/T charging standard (not CCS). Adapters allow CCS charging but may affect performance. Chinese prices do not transfer directly — transport, EU homologation, and customs duties raise the final price.

**Update 18 June 2026**: Xiaomi is taking legal action to stop the parallel import. "Xiaomi has no business relationship with Autohelden/Fleemo," a spokesperson stated, adding that an EU-wide border seizure application will be made to halt all unauthorised imports.
