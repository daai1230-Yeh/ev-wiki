# The Volvo EX60 Can 'See' The World And Chat With You About It

**Author**: Iulian Dnistran | **Date**: 2026-05-20

---

At Google I/O this year, Volvo and Google demonstrated how the Gemini AI assistant built into the new EX60's Google-based infotainment can pull live information from the car's cameras, interpret it in real time and answer driver or passenger questions. The feature is still a work in progress with no announced release date, but the demo was striking.

Highlights captured from the article:

- A driver can ask about a foreign-language road sign and Gemini will scan, translate and explain it.
- In the demo, the system identified the Transamerica Pyramid in San Francisco, noted it is mainly an office building rather than a tourist attraction, mentioned a park at its base, and offered to plan a route there.
- The experience is described as similar to Google Lens but supercharged with Gemini and natural voice commands, enabled by Gemini's multimodal understanding plus the EX60's neural processing engine and software-defined architecture.
- Google says privacy is central: the feature works only with driver permission and faces are blurred during image analysis.
- Related upcoming Google features for cars with Google Built-In include a car-optimized YouTube Music app, 3D Immersive Navigation in Google Maps, and deeper Gemini integration for voice control of vehicle functions. The EX60, EX90 and ES90 are the first Volvos slated to get Immersive Navigation.
