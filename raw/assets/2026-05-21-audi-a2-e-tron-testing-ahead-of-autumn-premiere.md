# Audi tests A2 E-Tron ahead of autumn premiere

**Author**: Daniel Bönnighausen | **Date**: 2026-05-20

---

Audi confirmed the A2 e-tron as the brand's new entry-level electric model in March, and has just unveiled a camouflaged prototype. Before its official premiere in autumn 2026, the compact EV is undergoing various testing phases — including trials in the Nordics, wind tunnel testing, and public road driving.

## Key Details

- The A2 E-Tron is Audi's fully electric entry into the **premium compact class**, designed to make premium electric mobility more accessible.
- Production will be at Audi's home plant in **Ingolstadt**.
- World premiere scheduled for **autumn 2026**.

## Testing Phases

**Cold-weather testing (Lapland):** Evaluating the prototype on snow-covered roads, icy handling courses, and a frozen lake, with focus on driving dynamics, thermal management, battery performance, and the interaction between drivetrain, brake control, and chassis.

**Wind tunnel testing:** Aerodynamics optimised in Audi's Technical Development wind tunnel with wind speeds up to 300 km/h, high-precision aeroacoustic measurements, and a 235 km/h rolling road. The A2's roofline is a particular focus.

**Public road testing (Altmühltal region):** Routes featuring inclines, varying surfaces, and tight corners to evaluate chassis and driver assistance systems under everyday conditions.

## Platform Speculation

Audi has not yet revealed drivetrain details. The SSP platform is not yet ready, and the PPE (used for the Q6 E-Tron) is designed for larger models. The MEB electric toolkit remains the most likely platform, with the Cupra Born facelift and VW ID.3 Neo serving as potential reference points for what the A2 E-Tron will offer.
