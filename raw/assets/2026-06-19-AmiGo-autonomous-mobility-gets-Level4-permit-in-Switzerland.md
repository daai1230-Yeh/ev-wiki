# AmiGo autonomous mobility service gets Level 4 permit in Switzerland

**Author**: Chris Randall | **Date**: 22.10.2025 (updated 18.06.2026)

---

AmiGo, the on-demand autonomous mobility service from Apollo Go (Baidu) and PostBus, has received a Level 4 autonomous operations permit in Eastern Switzerland from the Swiss Federal Roads Office. Test drives on public roads began 1 June 2026.

The trials (with a safety operator aboard) cover ~80 km² across St. Gallen, Appenzell Ausserrhoden and Appenzell Innerrhoden cantons. The next phase: a closed user trial, then driverless journeys once safety requirements are met. Commercial operations remain scheduled for 2027.

**Background**: PostBus announced AmiGo in October 2025 as a bookable, on-demand service with electric autonomous vehicles (up to 4 passengers). Technology partner Apollo Go/Baidu operates 1,000+ autonomous vehicles with 14 million+ cumulative rides worldwide. The vehicle platform uses Baidu's RT6 with Apollo ADFM (Autonomous Driving Foundation Model).

The service aims to supplement public transport in areas where traditional scheduled services are insufficient — rural regions and off-peak times.
