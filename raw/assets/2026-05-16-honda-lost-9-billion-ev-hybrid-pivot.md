# Honda Lost $9 Billion On EVs. Now, It's Betting On Hybrid SUVs And Sedans

**Author**: Iulian Dnistran | **Date**: May 14, 2026

---

Japan's second-largest carmaker reported its first annual loss in nearly 70 years after scrapping its EV plans.

Honda posted its first annual loss (for the fiscal year ending March 2026) in nearly 70 years after a $9 billion bill for misreading the EV market. The company has canceled its long-term EV sales targets — scrapping the goal of EVs making up a fifth of new car sales by 2030 — and abandoned its planned shift to a fully electric or fuel-cell lineup by 2040.

**What went wrong:** Honda poured billions into the bespoke 0 Series EV platform, underpinning three models: the Honda 0 Series SUV, Honda 0 Series Saloon, and Acura RSX. All three were canceled before a single car rolled off the production line.

**Canada project suspended:** CEO Toshihiro Mibe confirmed Honda will indefinitely suspend its $11 billion Canadian EV project, which would have been Honda's biggest Canada investment ever.

**The hybrid pivot:** Honda is now focusing on ultra-efficient hybrids. The first two new models will debut in 2028 on a new platform powered by a next-generation hybrid setup Honda claims will be the "world's most efficient powertrain" — 10%+ more efficient than its current 2023-era system. A four-door Honda sedan and an Acura-branded crossover have been shown as concepts; both are coming to the U.S.

By March 2030, Honda plans to launch 15 models with the new hybrid powertrain, with the U.S. market receiving most of them — the majority being AWD SUVs.

**Investment:** Honda will invest 4.4 trillion yen (~$28 billion) in new gas and hybrid models over the next three years. Future EV development gets just $5 billion.

Despite the loss, Honda expects to return to profitability by end of 2026, supported by its booming motorcycle business and extensive cost-cutting.
