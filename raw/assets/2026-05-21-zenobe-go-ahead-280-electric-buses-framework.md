# Zenobe finances over 280 electric buses under new framework agreement with Go-Ahead

**Author**: Editorial staff | **Date**: 2026-05-18

---

Zenobē and Go-Ahead Group have launched a new **Committed Finance Framework Agreement (CFFA)** — described as the first arrangement of its kind in the bus sector — supporting the rollout of electric buses through a scalable, pre-agreed financing structure.

Already, the agreement has supported the financing of **more than 280 electric buses from four different OEMs** across Go-Ahead operations.

## How It Works

Under the CFFA, Go-Ahead can access a **pre-approved credit line** to finance EV deployments as needed, without negotiating separate leasing agreements for each procurement cycle. Key benefits:

- Reduces administrative complexity and accelerates funding access
- Funding drawn down under pre-agreed commercial terms when vehicles are ready
- Flexibility around vehicle delivery schedules and franchise award timing
- Greater cost predictability as fleet electrification scales across multiple projects

Go-Ahead awards contracts on a route-by-route or franchise basis, requiring financing that adapts to different timelines — this framework addresses that challenge directly.

## Background

- Zenobē and Go-Ahead have worked together since **2021** on electric bus projects
- Go-Ahead plans to electrify its **entire bus fleet by 2035**

## Quotes

Steven Meersman (Founder & Director, Zenobē): "This agreement marks a significant step forward in how public transport operators can finance the transition to zero-emission fleets... By removing repetitive negotiations and enabling funding to be drawn down rapidly under pre-agreed terms, we're making it easier, faster and more cost-effective to deploy electric vehicles at scale."

Gonzalo Velasco (Group Structured Finance Director, Go-Ahead): "This committed finance framework with Zenobē gives us certainty that funding is available when we need it, while removing unnecessary complexity and delay."
