# MAN unveils electric 16-tonne truck eTGM

**Author**: Cora Werwitzke | **Date**: 2026-05-13

---

MAN Truck & Bus unveiled the eTGM electric distribution truck at the Transpotec Logitec trade fair in Milan, completing its all-electric portfolio with coverage from 12 to 50 tonnes. Production begins next year at MAN's Kraków plant.

## Key Specs

- **GVW**: 16.01 tonnes (16.5t optional)
- **Payload**: up to 10.6 tonnes (smallest battery config)
- **Battery**: 2–4 packs × 80 kWh net = up to 320 kWh total (NMC)
- **Range**: up to 480 km (max battery config)
- **Electric motor**: eCD210, 210 kW, 800 Nm (adapted from heavy-duty eTGX/eTGS)
- **Transmission**: MAN TipMatic 2 (two-speed)
- **DC charging**: up to 375 kW (10–80% in 43 minutes with 4 packs)
- **AC charging**: to be added by end of 2027
- **GCW** (with trailer): up to 33 tonnes

## Target Applications

Urban and regional distribution, municipal services, food/supermarket logistics, construction, and waste management. Supports mechanical (22 kW) and electric PTOs.

## Platform & Technology

The eTGM shares its modular BEV platform with the heavier eTGX and eTGS trucks. Batteries (assembled in Nuremberg) are identical to those in other MAN trucks, the upcoming MAN coach, and the eTGL. The shared-component strategy enables significant cost/synergy benefits.

The 16.01-tonne GVW is strategically chosen: EU regulations grant electric trucks >16 tonnes significant toll advantages and regulatory credits. MAN explicitly documents this threshold.

## Competitive Context

Competitors in this segment include the Volvo FL Electric and Renault Trucks E-Tech D (both offering more modest ranges) and the newer DAF XB Electric. Renault and Volvo have also announced new 14-tonne LFP-battery trucks.

## Market Outlook

MAN Executive Board Member Friedrich Baumann emphasized that BEV drivetrains, with >70% efficiency, outperform all other propulsion types. Production of the eTGM begins in 2027 alongside diesel counterparts on a flexible manufacturing line in Kraków.
