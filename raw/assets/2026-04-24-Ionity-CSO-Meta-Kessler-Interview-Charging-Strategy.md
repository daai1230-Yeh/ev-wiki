# 'We always aim to offer the fastest and latest technology' – Ionity CSO Meta Kessler

**Author**: Sebastian Schaal | **Date**: April 21, 2026

---

Faster and faster charging on highways, more charging parks in cities, along with new partnerships and continuous technological advancements — the charging industry is not standing still. electrive spoke with Meta Kessler, Chief Strategy Officer at Ionity, about these decisions and the fast-charging provider's plans.

Ionity currently operates over 850 sites across 24 European countries, with nearly 50 additional sites under construction.

**On deploying 1,000 kW chargers (Alpitronic HYC1000):**
"In principle, we want to build the 1,000-kilowatt sites where there is particularly high utilisation, such as on high-traffic routes. Thanks to the dynamic distribution of charging power, we believe we can charge vehicles faster and make the charging points available for the next vehicle sooner."

**On technology diversity vs. standardisation:**
"We would prefer a single, standardised concept, but this is not currently the best solution. The hardware market is still evolving, with new suppliers, technologies and players continuously entering the space. We will always strive to offer the fastest and newest technology at our sites."

**On urban expansion:**
Ionity already has 60 charging stations in urban areas, with another 50 under construction or in planning. By 2030, the company aims for roughly 30% of its network to be in cities — roughly 490 urban sites out of ~1,300 total. This includes a partnership with Uber in London, where Uber drivers receive discounted access to Ionity fast-charging stations, while Uber provides utilisation guarantees for new sites.

**On dynamic pricing:**
A pilot test with selected haulage companies ran until end of March. "Greater price variability is desired in the market because there are different use cases. The energy price itself is inherently dynamic." In Germany, the ratio of BEVs to high-power chargers is currently around 60:1, but should ideally be 100–120:1.

**On Fleet Portal:**
In April, Ionity launched a Fleet Portal allowing business customers to centrally manage fleet charging processes, activate charging cards, and retrieve invoices, with the ability to define country-specific card coverage.

**Key quote:** "We want to enable the fastest vehicles on the market to charge at their maximum possible power. That is our branding, that is our customer promise."
