# UK: Ford cuts prices of Explorer and Capri EVs by up to £5,000

**Author**: Carla Westerheide | **Date**: 2026-04-21

---

Ford has reduced prices of its electric SUVs Explorer and Capri by up to £5,000 in the UK, while increasing range and equipment levels. But it's not all good news.

The move comes in response to increasing competition in the electric vehicle segment. According to Autocar, the price of the Explorer has fallen by between £4,035 and £4,800, depending on specification, bringing the entry-level rear-wheel-drive 58 kWh Style variant to £35,185. The all-wheel-drive 77 kWh Explorer Premium is now priced below £50,000. Prices for the Capri have dropped by £4,080 to £5,070.

Alongside the price adjustments, the carmaker upgraded standard equipment and increased the range of Standard Range variants by 43 miles. New features include an updated Sync infotainment system, enhanced adaptive cruise control, a reversing assistant, a driver monitoring system, and a three-pin power outlet with up to 2.3 kW output.

The "not all good news" aspect relates to Ford simultaneously restructuring its UK dealer network and reducing the number of authorised retailers, which could affect customer access and service.
