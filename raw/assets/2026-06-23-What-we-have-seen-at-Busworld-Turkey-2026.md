# What we have seen at Busworld Turkey 2026?

**Author**: Editorial Staff | **Date**: 18 June 2026

---

The 11th edition of Busworld Turkey ran in Istanbul from 17 to 19 June, bringing together 161 exhibitors across 11,358 square metres of exhibition space, a 15% increase compared with the previous edition. The event took place at a time when Turkey is strengthening its position as one of Europe's leading bus manufacturing centres — bus production reached 12,425 units in 2025 (up 25% year-on-year), and manufacturing grew 19.3% in the first four months of 2026.

One of the defining characteristics of Busworld Turkey 2026 is the coexistence of multiple propulsion technologies: battery-electric buses, diesel, CNG, hydrogen fuel cell, trolleybus, and autonomous driving.

## Key Exhibits

**Bozankaya** — Showcasing its Lamis 12T trolleybus with 240 kW DC fast charger. The 12-metre low-floor vehicle offers battery-assisted operation for up to 35 km away from overhead infrastructure and is already operating in Prague.

**Cummins** — Highlighting its hydrogen strategy via the B6.7H hydrogen internal combustion engine (290 hp, 1,200 Nm of torque). Earmarked for testing with Anadolu Isuzu on the Kendo bus model.

**HABAŞ (HBS)** — Presenting four vehicles including the battery-electric Comfort City-e and the hydrogen-powered Comfort City H2 — the only fuel cell electric bus displayed at Busworld Turkey 2026.

**Iveco Bus** — Turkish debut of the Crossway Low Entry Elec (13-metre, Class I, five battery packs, overnight charging). Also signed an agreement for supply of 20 articulated 18-metre Streetway buses to Turkish dealer FSM Demirbaş Otomotiv.

**Karsan** — Only Turkish manufacturer focusing entirely on battery-electric mobility. Displaying the Autonomous e-ATAK, pantograph-charging e-ATA 12m, and articulated e-ATA 18. Currently 149 electric buses operating in Turkey (69 delivered in 2025) — all electric buses registered in Turkey in 2025 were Karsan vehicles.

**Otokar** — Exhibiting the updated e-KENT, battery-electric e-TERRITO (11/12/13m), and the autonomous e-CENTRO (Level 4, already operating at Mercamadrid in Madrid). Reports over 66,500 buses in operation worldwide and projected turnover of USD 1.2 billion for 2025.

**TEMSA** — Returning with the Avenue Neo electric midibus (first shown at Busworld Europe 2025), Maraton coach, and new Prestij.

**ZF** — Presenting the AxTrax 2 LF electric portal axle (approaching series production), oil-free e-comp Scroll air compressor, GSR-compliant ADAS platform, and ZF Bus Connect telematics platform.
