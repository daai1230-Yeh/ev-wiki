# electrive TODAY — 4 June 2026 (1:30pm edition)

**Source**: electrive global (newsletter@electrive.com)

---

## News
- **VW starts production of ID. Polo and Cupra Raval** — Seat launched series production of both compact EVs in Martorell, initially with the larger battery version.
- **ICCT study flags high CO₂ emissions from PHEVs** — Plug-in hybrids in Europe emit far more CO₂ in real-world use than official figures, with the gap continuing to widen.
- **Palmer Energy Technology acquires Kleandrive** — Acquired UK bus repower specialist Kleandrive out of administration, securing electrification/retrofit expertise for heavy-duty vehicles.
- **Rolls-Royce upgrades Spectre with more range** — Revised all-electric Spectre coupé now delivers up to 628 km WLTP range with faster charging.
- **Tata Motors to license Chery JLR's Freelander platform** — Tata will use CJLR's new platform (developed for the Freelander brand) for EVs under its Avinya brand.
- **Polestar expands into the Baltic region** — Launching sales in Estonia, Latvia and Lithuania via Volvax Baltic dealer network; first Polestar Space opens in Tallinn in June.

## Number of the Day
- 59,969 electric passenger cars newly registered in Germany in May (+39.3% YoY); BEV share of new registrations was 25.0%.

## Technology
- **Spiro raises $215 million** — African electric mobility company will use funds to expand EV and battery-swapping infrastructure across Africa.
- **GaN4EmoBiL shows 800V bidirectional DC charger** — Fraunhofer IAF and Ambibox developed a GaN-based power electronics module for 800V bidirectional DC charging, to be demonstrated at PCIM Nuremberg.
- **Uber and WeRide plan robotaxi launch in Madrid** — First joint European robotaxi project, in cooperation with the regional government.
- **Bosch launches digital certificate for used e-bikes** — Shows battery and drive system condition, first introduced by refurbisher Rebike.

## Feedback
- Yesterday's most-clicked link: Daimler Truck introduces eActros Lowliner.

## Quotes of the Day
- Jim Farley (CEO, Ford): "We'll start with a mid-size truck, but we have lots of plans for other vehicles off the platform." — on Ford's new EV platform.
- Íñigo Trasmonte (Head of Marketing & PR, BYD Spain): "The first two models to be manufactured are the Dolphin and also the Atto." — on BYD's first European factory.

## Fleets + Infrastructure
- **Amphos builds Scotland's first MW charging hub** — Megawatt-scale charging site for battery-electric HGVs at Russell Group's Coatbridge logistics site.
- **i-charging launches 500 kW charger for cars and trucks** — AiO 500 supports CCS, NACS and GB/T standards.
- **Hubject welcomes software provider Road as new partner** — Dutch eRoaming transaction processing provider joins Intercharge platform.
- **Orange EV secures record order for 600 terminal trucks** — Full rollout scheduled for 2026.
- **Olectra deploys 60 electric buses in Hyderabad** — Rollout involves sister company Evey Trans.

## Service

- Reading tip: Chinese-built EVs begin arriving in Canada following new trade agreement; 2,910 vehicles imported in May, mostly Tesla.
