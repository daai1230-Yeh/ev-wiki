# electrive TODAY — 8 June 2026

**Source**: electrive global (newsletter@electrive.com)

---

## News
- **EU debates stricter EV quotas for company cars** — Social Democrats push for higher EV quotas for company car fleets than the European Commission's proposal.
- **Foxtron launches Cavira electric SUV** — Foxconn subsidiary launches its second own-brand model in Taiwan, Australia and New Zealand.
- **Sany delivers long-haul electric trucks in Europe** — e263 electric semi-truck marketing begins, with initial customer vehicles operating in Germany; new models and a 2027 preview planned for IAA Transportation.
- **Stellantis begins Leapmotor C10 assembly in Malaysia** — First locally built Stellantis model in the ASEAN region.
- **Updated Audi Q4 e-tron goes on sale in UK** — Priced from £46,260 (SUV) and £48,160 (Sportback); first deliveries expected this summer.
- **Lotus launches Eletre X PHEV in Europe** — PHEV version of the Eletre SUV with 70 kWh battery, up to 350 km electric range and 1,200 km total range; from €96,990.

## Number of the Day
- Tesla delivered its 300,000th vehicle in the UK, a year after passing 250,000; UK deliveries rose 44% YoY in May.

## Technology
- **Audi unveils hybrid super sports car Nuvolari** — 736 kW hybrid combining a V8 biturbo with three electric motors, limited to 499 units, deliveries from H1 2027.
- **B2U to reuse Waymo batteries for storage** — Partnership to repurpose decommissioned robotaxi batteries for second-life energy storage.
- **Nissan launches solid-state lithium-sulphur battery research** — CoRe-SoLiS project with University of Oxford and Gelion targets high energy density, fast charging and long lifespan.
- **Straubel joins QuantumScape advisory board** — Former Tesla CTO and Redwood Materials CEO JB Straubel joins the VW-backed solid-state battery developer's strategic advisory board.

## Quotes of the Day
- Oliver Blume (CEO, Volkswagen Group): "I think we should rather talk about a European policy of interests, as is pursued in other regions of the world." — calling for stricter rules for foreign carmakers, noting hybrids aren't covered by EU tariffs on Chinese EVs.
- Annick Roetynck (LEVA-EU): "This is a simple change of wording to recognise two vehicle categories that already exist in EU law — no new technical requirements, no new compliance costs." — calling for L6e-B and L7e-C small EV classes to be treated equally with M1 passenger cars.

## Fleets + Infrastructure
- **Prague opens 161-point EV charging hub** — Largest municipal charging hub in Praha 9, with AC chargers and Alpitronic Hyperchargers, operated by PRE.
- **Lidl to electrify Dutch truck fleet by 2027** — All 440 Netherlands stores to be supplied by electric trucks, three years ahead of schedule.
- **BYD to deploy Flash Chargers at Sinopec stations** — Partnership to install fast chargers across Sinopec's 30,000+ petrol stations in China.
- **DHL invests €160m in e-mobility in France** — Expanding electric vehicle fleet and charging infrastructure, mainly benefiting DHL Express.

## Service
- Reading tip I: Are EVs finally going mainstream in India? Electric car sales rose 25% in the year to March 2026, crossing 5% share of the passenger vehicle market.
- Reading tip II: Canadians have claimed over $122 million in EV rebates since Ottawa relaunched its program in February, but many dealers report payment delays.

## Short Circuit
- Elssway develops wireless charging for light EVs — Tel Aviv-based company's system works for e-scooters while stationary or in motion; a scooter-sharing operator will roll it out in Tel Aviv and Herzliya.
