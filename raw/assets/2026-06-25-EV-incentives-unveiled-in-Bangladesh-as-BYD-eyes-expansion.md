# EV incentives unveiled in Bangladesh as BYD eyes expansion

**Author**: Ciaran Daly | **Date**: 24.06.2026

---

Bangladesh has just announced a sweeping package of tax incentives aimed at accelerating EV adoption. It includes tax breaks for importers, manufacturers and buyers of BEVs and PHEVs - as well as machinery imports and charging stations.

Under the government's proposed 2026/27 budget, import taxes on EVs priced below $25,000 (~€22,000) will fall from 93% to 66–80% depending on value. EVs priced up to $50,000 (~€44,000) will see tax burden cut to 80%. Bangladesh also plans to raise the total tax burden on ICE vehicles from 132.36% to 155.88%, and reduce taxes for all plug-in hybrids.

The government plans to eliminate duties and taxes on chargers and infrastructure, extend incentives for electric buses and trucks through 2030, and reduce VAT for locally assembled EVs to 5% through 2030 (with complete VAT exemption for vehicles with key components manufactured in Bangladesh).

Bangladesh has set a 30% EV market share target by 2030. The policy shift comes as BYD strengthens its presence in the country. Since entering in 2024, BYD has expanded its local lineup and signed a manufacturing agreement with local company Runner Automobiles. They plan to open an EV manufacturing unit in Bhaluka, 113km north of Dhaka, with Runner's CFO Shanat Datta noting: "We plan to go for EV assembling by the middle of 2027."

Currently, Bangladesh has only 32 public charging stations and over 1,000 BEVs/PHEVs sold since 2024. Both Runner Automobiles (2.6 billion taka / ~€18.4M) and Rancon Motors (3 billion taka / ~€21.4M) plan investments in EV assembly and charging infrastructure.
