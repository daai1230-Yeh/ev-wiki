# smart returns to its roots — and scales up in Europe: Interview with CEO smart Europe Wolfgang Ufer

**Author**: Peter Schwierz | **Date**: 2026-05-01

---

smart is expanding its electric portfolio — but the return of the two-seater could redefine the brand. In his first major interview as CEO of smart in Europe, Wolfgang Ufer outlines growth plans, market dynamics and why the upcoming #2 marks a turning point for smart.

Since 1 March 2026, Wolfgang Ufer has been the new CEO of smart Europe, having spent 13 years with the smart brand and heading the German market for years prior.

## Key Topics

**On the smart brand identity:** Smart stands fully for electric vehicles — fully electric. The portfolio now spans from the #1 and #3 to the #5, with the upcoming #2 (the spiritual successor to the fortwo) set to reconnect the brand to its urban origins.

**On the #2 / fortwo return:** The #2 will be under three metres in length, fully electric, and more capable than its predecessors in every dimension. Wolfgang Ufer confirmed it will be a volume model, not just an emotional icon. It will launch for sale in 2027. Some 350,000–400,000 fortwo models are still in use in Germany alone.

**On smart's market position:** Germany is seeing the strongest momentum, aided by subsidies and rising fuel prices. Spain, France, and Norway (especially for the #5) are also strong growth markets. Smart operates across 17 European markets.

**On plug-in hybrids:** Smart has no plans for PHEVs in Europe. "Personally, I am also not a fan of plug-in hybrids because I believe they can only ever be a bridging technology."

**On customer profile:** For current models (#1, #3, #5): typically high-income customers aged 40–60 who consciously choose electric. The #2 will broaden that considerably — "from 18 to 86 years old."

**On growth target:** Significant growth in every European market in 2026. The #2 launch (sales from 2027) will create a "completely new situation in terms of volume."

**On EV market sentiment:** "I believe we are at the beginning of a new positive phase — and I believe it will not be short-lived." Charging times have halved generation-over-generation while range continues to improve.
