# Porsche considers moving Cayenne production to Leipzig

**Author**: Cora Werwitzke | **Date**: 2026-06-29

---

Porsche is reportedly considering relocating production of **all Cayenne variants** — including combustion-engine, hybrid and electric models — from Bratislava, Slovakia, to its plant in **Leipzig, Germany**. According to a *Frankfurter Allgemeine Zeitung* (F.A.Z.) report, the move would depend on employees agreeing to lower labour costs.

## Background

Porsche only began manufacturing the Cayenne at the VW Group's multi-brand plant in Bratislava in **February 2026** — making the planned relocation a very short production run in Slovakia. The Leipzig plant currently serves as Porsche's SUV production hub.

## Why Leipzig?

The relocation aims to **secure long-term utilisation** of the Leipzig site. Porsche CEO **Michael Leiters** (who succeeded Oliver Blume in early 2025) is working to improve capacity utilisation at German plants.

The challenge is that wage levels in Slovakia are substantially below those at Porsche's German sites — making the move conditional on labor cost concessions.

## Context: Porsche Under Pressure

Porsche delivered just **60,991 vehicles in Q1 2026**, down 15% year-on-year. Deliveries in China fell 21% in the same period.

Parent company VW is also reportedly considering closing **four German plants** and cutting up to 100,000 jobs.

Porsche CEO Leiters is expected to outline a broader turnaround strategy at a **capital markets day in October**, reportedly including reduced model derivatives, reorganised development operations, and a "low four-digit" number of job cuts in Zuffenhausen and Weissach.
