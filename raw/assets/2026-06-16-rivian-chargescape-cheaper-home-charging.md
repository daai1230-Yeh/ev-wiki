# Rivian's Latest Partnership Unlocks Cheaper Home EV Charging

**Author**: Iulian Dnistran | **Date**: June 16, 2026

---

Rivian partnered with ChargeScape to gain access to various utilities' managed-charging programs, enabling owners to charge their cars for less automatically.

## What's the Deal

Rivian inked a deal with ChargeScape, a Texas-based vehicle-grid integration platform, enabling owners of the **R1S, R1T, and R2** to automatically charge during off-peak hours — potentially saving thousands of dollars per year.

All Rivian EVs already support managed charging, so no settings change or software update is needed for R1S and R1T owners.

## How It Works

The car communicates with the electric company to charge when it's financially convenient (typically off-peak hours, when demand is lower). Because Rivian batteries are large, this also helps energy providers keep the grid stable.

The program also allows EV owners to charge with clean energy, as the car can replenish its battery when renewable power is at its peak during the day.

## Future: Vehicle-to-Grid (V2G) for R2

The new Rivian R2 will also be able to export power from its battery back to the grid via an over-the-air software update. This will transform the car's battery into an energy buffer:
- Takes energy from the grid when it's cheap
- Sells energy back when it's more expensive

## About ChargeScape

ChargeScape is backed by BMW, Ford, Honda, and Nissan. Tesla, Stellantis, and others also use ChargeScape. It works with leading US utilities including:
- PG&E
- TXU Energy
- Xcel Energy
- Con Edison
- DTE

ChargeScape claims to have delivered 89% of EV charging during off-peak hours.

> "Through this collaboration with ChargeScape and other partners, Rivian's software-enabled vehicles are a perfect, nimble partner to help balance the energy grid and enable drivers to get more value out of their vehicle." — Andrew Peterman, Director of Advanced Energy Solutions at Rivian
