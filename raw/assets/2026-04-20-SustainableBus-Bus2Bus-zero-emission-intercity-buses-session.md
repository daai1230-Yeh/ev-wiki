# Market, policy and operations in focus for zero-emission intercity buses, at Sustainable Bus Tour session in Bus2Bus

**Author**: Editorial Staff | **Date**: 2026-04-20

---

The transition to zero-emission buses in Europe is extending into the intercity segment, and this was the topic of the Sustainable Bus Tour session held during Bus2Bus 2026 in Berlin. The panel "From city to intercity: the next step for zero emission bus transition" gathered industry and policy voices including Malo Benoit (ICCT), Jean-Marc Boucheret (Iveco Bus), Javier Contijoch (BYD Europe), and Hakan Bubik (Otokar).

**Fragmented landscape:** Intercity bus transport in Europe is characterized by a fragmented operator landscape, including a high number of small and medium-sized companies, alongside less standardized operational patterns and frequent dual-use vehicle profiles. Service conditions differ from urban environments, with longer routes, higher average speeds and extended duty cycles — introducing specific requirements for vehicle autonomy, charging strategies and fleet utilization.

**EU regulatory framework:** The keynote outlined CO₂ standards establishing a 100% zero-emission target for urban buses by 2035 and a 90% emissions reduction requirement for intercity buses and coaches by 2040, alongside compliance mechanisms based on fleet-average emissions.

**ICCT market data:** According to ICCT data, zero-emission buses and coaches accounted for approximately 25% of total EU sales in 2025, with city buses exceeding the majority threshold for zero-emission adoption, while intercity buses and coaches remained largely diesel-based (~79% conventional powertrains). Between 2025 and 2030, approximately 20,000 electric intercity buses and coaches are expected to enter the European market, alongside 48,000 urban buses, requiring approximately 30,000 dedicated charging points for long-distance operations.

**Industry perspectives:** Iveco Bus presented a technology-neutral approach with offerings from diesel and CNG to battery-electric solutions. BYD Europe emphasized its integrated approach covering vehicle supply and financing solutions. Otokar highlighted operational constraints specific to the intercity segment.
