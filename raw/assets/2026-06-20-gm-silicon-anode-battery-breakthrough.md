# Forget Solid-State. This EV Battery Breakthrough Is Ready To Upend The Market Now

**Author**: Suvrat Kothari | **Date**: 2026-06-18

---

General Motors is betting on silicon anodes as the next leap in EV battery technology — a breakthrough that could be ready much sooner than solid-state batteries.

## GM's Battery Bet

"We believe silicon is the next anode technology," said Kurt Kelty, VP of Battery and Sustainability at General Motors, at the GM Empower conference in San Francisco.

Most automakers don't expect solid-state batteries to be ready for commercialization before the end of the decade. Silicon anodes, however, are already in early commercial production and offer significant near-term advantages.

## Why Silicon Anodes Matter

The anode stores ions during charging and releases them during discharging. Today, anodes are almost universally made from graphite, but silicon can store about 10× more lithium ions, enabling:
- **Higher energy density** → more range per pack
- **Faster charging** → silicon handles ion flow more efficiently
- **Better safety** profile

Key performance claims from silicon anode startups:
- **Amprius Technologies**: A 310-mile EV could reach 574 miles with silicon anodes
- **Sila**: High-silicon anodes boost range by 20% with no increase in pack size

## Already In Production

Silicon anodes are further ahead than solid-state. Sila's factory in Moses Lake, Washington is already operational, with supply agreements with Mercedes-Benz and Panasonic. The McMurtry Spéirling hypercar uses Group14's silicon anode batteries via Molicel cells.

## GM's Roadmap

GM is testing large-format automotive-grade silicon anode cells at its Wallace research center. The first application will be LMR (lithium manganese rich) prismatic cells launching in 2028 on a production truck — targeting 400+ miles of range with significant weight savings.

GM is pursuing silicon anodes as a bridge technology: delivering meaningful improvements to range and charging speed while solid-state batteries continue their longer development arc.
