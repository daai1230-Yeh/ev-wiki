# Volvo kicks off EX60 series production

**Author**: Sebastian Schaal | **Date**: April 22, 2026

---

Series production of the all-electric mid-size SUV has started at the Torslanda plant near Gothenburg in Sweden. First customer deliveries are scheduled for early summer.

Volvo unveiled the electric mid-size SUV in January. The EX60 is the first model based on Volvo's SPA3 platform — an 800-volt architecture for battery-electric vehicles.

At launch, the EX60 will be available in three versions:

- **Base model**: 275 kW rear-wheel-drive powertrain with an 80 kWh battery
- **AWD variant**: 375 kW powertrain with a 91 kWh battery (net capacity)
- **P12 AWD**: 500 kW of power, 112 kWh battery, and a range of 810 kilometres — the most powerful production Volvo ever

Thanks to a peak charging power of 370 kW, the all-wheel-drive models can recharge in just 18–19 minutes. Even in the highly competitive mid-size SUV segment — which includes the new BMW iX3, Mercedes-Benz GLC, and Chinese 800-volt SUVs — these specifications are impressive.

The EX60 holds national importance for Sweden: it is the first fully electric car designed, developed, and produced in Sweden. Its predecessor, the XC60 (which continues to be produced in hybrid form) is one of Sweden's most important export products.

To produce the EX60, Volvo Cars invested around ten billion Swedish kronor (approximately €930 million) in the Torslanda facility, introducing Megacasting technology and on-site battery assembly alongside a modernised paint shop and final assembly.

"Today is an important milestone for our company and for Sweden as a whole, as we start to build the first EX60 customer cars," said Håkan Samuelsson, Volvo Cars CEO. "We are now focused on a steady ramp-up of high-quality EX60 production, making sure this game-changing car will be a profitable growth driver in the coming years."
