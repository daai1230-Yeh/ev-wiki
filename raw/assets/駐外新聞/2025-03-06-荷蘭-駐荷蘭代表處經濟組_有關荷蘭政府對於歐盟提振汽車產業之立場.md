主旨：有關荷蘭政府對於歐盟提振汽車產業之立場，敬請卓

參。

說明：

一、歐盟執委會近期就提振歐洲汽車產業未來發展事進行公眾

諮詢，並於本(2025)年3月5日提出「歐盟汽車產業行動計

畫」(EU Industrial Action Plan for the Automotive 

sector)。荷蘭經濟部長Dirk Beljaarts前已向荷蘭眾議院

提送荷蘭政府立場文件(非正式英譯如附件)，表達將支持

歐盟制定該行動計畫。

二、本案荷蘭政府立場重點如下：

(一)荷蘭認為，具競爭力的汽車產業對維持私部門投資、創

造就業機會及更廣泛的經濟利益甚有幫助，並強調汽車

產業在經濟成長、流動性及技術進步等面向之作用。

(二)先進車輛之自動化與數位化技術發展至關重要，包括自

動緊急煞車及適應性巡航定速控制等可提高安全性及效

率之系統。當務之急應為零排放汽車建立適當條件，及

解決網路安全風險。

(三)為加速推廣零排放汽車，荷蘭政府提倡採取促進需求措

施、擴大充電基礎設施(特別是用於重型汽車)，並解決

電網擁塞問題。此外，確保交通暢通且價格合理亦相當

重要。

(四)加強電池價值鏈與支援零排放技術亦為關鍵。歐盟立法

應保持技術中立，允許公司選擇所偏好的推進系統，同

時鼓勵有利歐盟的投資。

(五)開放貿易必須與創造公平競爭環境之措施保持平衡。歐

盟車輛的型式認證制度，以及所伴隨的標準化法規可提

供法律確定性、降低成本，並提高市場效率。

(六)清楚且可預測的法規對長期投資至關重要。荷蘭政府呼

籲進行全面影響評估、提供充分的執行時間、邀請利害

關係人參與，並確保有關車輛上市與生命週期管理等法

規之調和一致。

Dear  Mr  President,

The  European  Commission  (hereinafter:  Commission)  has  launched  a  public  consultation

on  the  future  of  the  European  automotive  industry.  Given  the  importance  of  the  automotive  industry  for  the  Dutch  

economy,  prosperity  and  well-being,  the  government  considers  it  important  to  make  an  active  contribution  to  

this  consultation.

That  is  why  a  position  paper  has  been  drawn  up  in  which  the  Dutch  position  is  set  out.  In  line  with  the  agreement  

made  during  the  General  Consultation  on  12  February  2013  on  information  provision  with  regard  to  

European  decision-making1 ,  you  will  find  enclosed  the  Cabinet's  response  to  the  consultation  of  

the  European  Commission  on  the  future  of  the  European  car  industry.  2

Background

On  28  January  2025,  you  were  informed  about  the  expected  legislation  in  the  field  of  sustainable  mobility.3  This  

letter  mentioned  the  Commission’s  announced  EU  Industrial  Action  Plan  for  the  Automotive  Sector  

(hereinafter:  the  Action  Plan)  and  the  Strategic  Dialogue  that  would  be  conducted  with  the  automotive  industry.  

This  Strategic  Dialogue  was  launched  on  30  January  with  22  stakeholders  from  the  European  automotive  industry.4  

Member  States  were  not  invited  to  this  Strategic  Dialogue.  The  Strategic  Dialogue  feeds,  among  other  things,  the  

Action  Plan,  which  will  be  presented  on  5  March  by  Commissioner  Tzitzikostas  (Sustainable  Transport  and  

Tourism).  This  Action  Plan  will  then  be  discussed  in  the  Council.

In  parallel  to  the  Strategic  Dialogue,  the  Commission  has  opened  a  public  consultation  on  the  future  of  the  

European  automotive  industry,  to  which  the  government  submitted  the  accompanying  input  on  13  February.  The  

Commission  invites  stakeholders  to  give  their  views  on  the  future  of  the  European  automotive  

industry  in  the  public  consultation.  The  results  of  the  public  consultation

Netherlands'  commitment

The  Netherlands  warmly  welcomes  the  initiative  to  draw  up  an  action  plan.  A  strong  and  
competitive  European  automotive  industry  contributes  significantly  to  employment  and  
earning  capacity.  A  strong  automotive  industry  is  also  of  great  importance  to  retain  
private  investments  and  related  spillover  effects  to  other  sectors.  The  shrinking  of  
these  investments  and  the  possibility  of  spillovers,  employment  and  earning  capacity  
could  in  the  long  term  pose  a  risk  to  the  European  position  in  the  automotive  sector  and,  
above  all,  automotive  and  broad  prosperity.  This  could  also  have  a  possible  negative  effect  
on  risky  strategic  dependencies.

The  Minister  of  Infrastructure  and  Water  Management  indicated  in  the  Transport  Council  
of  5  December  that  he  was  looking  forward  to  the  Strategic  Dialogue.  He  
emphasized  that  there  is  a  great  sense  of  urgency  among  all  parties  involved  and  that  
future-oriented  solutions  must  be  sought.5  The  Minister  of  Economic  Affairs  also  called  for  
attention  to  the  automotive  sector  during  the  Competitiveness  Council  of  28  November.6

For  the  input  of  the  Netherlands  in  the  public  consultation,  a  position  paper  has  been  drawn  
up  to  clarify  the  Dutch  view  to  the  Commission.  In  line  with  the  Action  Agenda  Auto7,  this  
position  paper  emphasises  that  the  car  is  and  will  remain  important  for  our  economy,  
prosperity  and  well-being  in  the  Netherlands.
The  Dutch  value  the  freedom,  comfort  and  flexibility  that  a  car  offers.  In  addition,  trucks  
and  buses  are  essential  for  a  smoothly  functioning  and  strong  economy.  In  addition,  
digitalisation  and  automation  of  vehicles  contribute  to  our  road  safety,  
accessibility  and  flow.  The  Netherlands  has  an  interest  in  a  competitive  car  industry  in  the  
EU  and  must  actively  work  towards  this  at  European  level.  The  position  paper  consists  of  the  
following  five  main  themes  that  correspond  to  the  themes  from  the  Commission's  concept  
note  for  the  Strategic  Dialogue  8:

1.  Innovation  and  leadership  in  future  technologies:
The  position  paper  states  that  the  Netherlands  has  significant  interests  in  a  healthy  
vehicle  industry,  partly  because  of  our  truck  manufacturers  and  first  and  second-tier  highly  
innovative  suppliers,  such  as  producers  of  semiconductors,  high-quality  steel  and  
advanced  composite  materials.  The  Netherlands  sees  that  the  transition  to  zero-emission  
road  transport  goes  hand  in  hand  with  vehicle  automation  and  digitalisation.  The  
automotive  industry  has  undergone  a  major  transition  in  recent  years,  partly  thanks  to  
digitalisation  and  automation.  This  technological  progress  has  led  to  concrete  
improvements  in  the  areas  of  road  safety,  accessibility  and  traffic  flow.  Advanced  
driver  assistance  systems  –  such  as  automatic  emergency  braking,  adaptive  
cruise  control  and  lane-keeping  assist  –  are  being  developed  to  increase  safety  and  comfort  
for  occupants  and  to  contribute  to  the  safety  of  other  road  users.  Thanks  to  
innovations  from  the  automotive  industry,  we  are  able  to  inform  road  users  in  real  time  about  
critical  situations.

2.  Clean  and  sustainable  transport:

The  Netherlands  sees  that  there  is  a  great  need  for  demand-stimulating  measures  for  the  automotive  industry.  

These  promote  the  growth  of  zero-emission  vehicles,  but  above  all  give  confidence  and  clarity  to  vehicle  

manufacturers  that  there  is  a  demand  for  the  zero-emission  vehicles  that  they  produce.  In  this  way,  these  

types  of  measures  strengthen  the  competitiveness  of  the  automotive  industry.  Earlier,  the  State  Secretary  for  

Infrastructure  and  Water  Management  wrote  a  letter  to  the  Commission  together  with  colleagues  from  other  Member  

States  calling  for  proposals  for  European  measures  to  accelerate  the  growth  of  zero-emission  vehicles.11  In  

the  position  paper,  the  Netherlands  indicates  that  a  further  roll-out  of  charging  infrastructure  is  necessary,  especially  

for  heavy  commercial  vehicles,  and  should  go  hand  in  hand  with  European  solutions  for  grid  congestion.  

Furthermore,  the  position  paper  mentions  the  importance  of  objective  information  about  developments  in  the  road  

vehicle  market.  Only  in  this  way  can  insight  be  gained  into  developments  in  the  market,  what  the  potential  

consequences  are  for  market  parties  and  how  this  relates  to  long-term  developments.

It  must  be  ensured  that  mobility  remains  accessible  and  affordable  for  citizens.

3.  Competitiveness  and  resilience

The  position  paper  underlines  the  need  for  measures  to  increase  the  competitiveness,  innovation  and  resilience  

of  the  European  automotive  industry.  Part  of  this  is  the  battery  value  chain,  about  which  you  have  been  

informed  previously12,  and  vehicle  automation.  It  is  important  that  the  fruits  of  investments  in  innovation  

and  zero-emission  technologies  benefit  the  EU  as  much  as  possible  and  that  private  capital  leads  to  targeted  

investments  to  strengthen  the  European  automotive  industry.  The  position  paper  states  that  

existing  legislation  already  provides  for  technological  neutrality,  whereby  market  parties  are  free  to  choose  a  form  

of  zero-emission  propulsion  of  road  vehicles.

5.  Streamline  regulations  and  optimize  processes

The  automotive  industry  has  an  interest  in  stable  and  predictable  regulations,  so  that  investing  in  innovation  

continues  to  pay  off.  The  position  paper  identifies  several  points  of  attention  for  streamlining  regulations  

and  optimising  processes,  including  introducing  an  impact  assessment,  where  relevant,  on  the  existing  

infrastructure  for  new  regulations,  sufficient  time  for  the  implementation  of  regulations  for  the  Member  States,  

involving  all  relevant  stakeholders  and  more  coherence  between  regulations  for  the  admission  of  vehicles  

to  the  market  and  regulations  for  the  entire  life  cycle  of  vehicles.

Finally

future  of  the  European  automotive  industry.
