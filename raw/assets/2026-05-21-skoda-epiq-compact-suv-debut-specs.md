# Introducing the Škoda Epiq: the beloved compact SUV

**Author**: Christoph Schwarzer | **Date**: 2026-05-19

---

The Volkswagen Group is introducing the **Škoda Epiq** as the third model in its new family of electric small cars, following the VW ID. Polo and the Cupra Raval. World premiere: May 19, 2026 in Zurich. Starting price: **€25,900** (though current order-ready price starts at **€32,100** for the Epiq 55).

The Epiq is built on the **MEB+ platform** alongside the Cupra Raval and VW ID. Polo, produced in **Spain**. It is Škoda's first model to fully implement the Modern Solid design language.

## Key Dimensions

- Length: **4.17 m** (qualifies for planned EU M1e class)
- Height: 1.58 m | Width: 1.80 m (excl. mirrors)
- Boot: **475 litres** + 25-litre frunk (more than Kamiq's 400L, Golf 8's 381L, or Elroq's 470L)
- Towing capacity: up to 1,200 kg (braked, Epiq 55)
- All variants: **front-wheel drive**; no rear motor enables larger rear boot space

## Variant Specs

| | Epiq 35 | Epiq 40 | Epiq 55 |
|--|---------|---------|---------|
| Power | 85 kW | 99 kW | 155 kW |
| Torque | 267 Nm | 267 Nm | 290 Nm |
| 0–100 km/h | 11.0 s | 9.8 s | 7.4 s |
| Top speed | 150 km/h | 150 km/h | 160 km/h |
| WLTP range | 310 km | 310 km | 440 km |
| Battery (net) | 37 kWh | 37 kWh | 52 kWh |
| Cell chemistry | LFP | LFP | NMC |
| DC charge power | 50 kW | 90 kW | 105 kW |
| DC charge (10–80%) | – | 28 min | 24 min |
| Starting price | €25,900 | €27,900 | €32,100 |

The Epiq 55 is **€695 cheaper** than the equivalent VW ID. Polo at €32,100.

## Notable Features

- **Bidirectional charging ready** across all variants (V2G, V2H — hardware/tariffs not yet available)
- Škoda Simply Clever storage: bottle holders, optional door umbrella
- Trim levels: Essence (base) + optional packages; Selection from €35,600
- Tow hitch standard with 75 kg nose weight (e-bike rack compatible)

## Market Outlook

Sales begin **H2 2026**, deliveries likely **early 2027**. The Elroq is already Germany's best-selling EV; the Epiq targets an even broader, price-sensitive audience. The €25,900 entry price qualifies for German purchase subsidies for many households.
