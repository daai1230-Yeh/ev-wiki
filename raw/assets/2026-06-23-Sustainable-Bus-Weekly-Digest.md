# Sustainable Bus Weekly Digest – June 23, 2026

**Source**: Sustainable Bus Weekly | **Date**: June 23rd, 2026

---

## This Week's Overview

Two new recently-released reports by BloombergNEF and ICCT offer a clear picture of where bus electrification stands. Against this backdrop, the issue brings extensive coverage from **Busworld Turkey 2026**, including launches from Iveco Bus, Otokar and ZF. Also covered: Solaris' North American ambitions, SONGZ's strong growth in HVAC for e-buses, and major investments from JBM Ecolife and Zenobē, plus electric order intake by Tata Motors and divestment from The Mobility House.

---

## Key Stories

**BNEF and ICCT Reports on Bus Electrification** — Two new reports from BloombergNEF and ICCT offer a clear picture of where global bus electrification stands today.

**Busworld Turkey 2026 Photo Story** — The 11th edition of Busworld Turkey ran in Istanbul from 17 to 19 June, bringing together 161 exhibitors across 11,358 square metres of exhibition space (a 15% increase from the previous edition). Launches from Iveco Bus, Otokar, and ZF.

**SONGZ HVAC for E-Buses** — SONGZ presented its annual business data at Bus2Bus Berlin 2026. The firm posted total bus HVAC deliveries of 40,151 units in 2025, with electric heat pump systems reaching 30,394 units — 75% of full-year sales.

**Yutong at Next Mobility Exhibition 2026 in Milan** — Yutong showcased four battery-electric vehicles with a focus on long-life components, integrated electric architecture and fleet digitalization.

**Hamburg/vhh.mobility: 6.6 MW Charging Depot** — vhh.mobility inaugurates a 6.6 MW charging depot with capacity for 60 buses.

**Tata Motors Secures 500 E-Bus Orders** — Electric commercial vehicle bookings from Tata Motors exceed expectations.

**JBM Ecolife Investment** — Major new investment in electric mobility from JBM Ecolife.

**Zenobē Energy Storage Investment** — New financing round for Zenobē supporting electric bus fleets.

**Solaris North American Ambitions** — Solaris outlines plans for the North American market.
