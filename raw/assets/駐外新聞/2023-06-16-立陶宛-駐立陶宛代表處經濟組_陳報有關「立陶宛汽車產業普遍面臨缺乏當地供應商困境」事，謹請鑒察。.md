Representatives of the automotive industry companies in Lithuania claim that there are very few suppliers in the

country who can serve them, which prevents the sector from being competitive.

According to Giedrias Valuckas, chairman of the board of the Baltic Automotive Components Cluster (BACC), the

ecosystem of the automotive sector in Lithuania is still broken, so most contractors and suppliers have to be hired

from abroad.

"In order to ensure further development, we need to solve the problem of the lack of suppliers." In countries where

the automotive industry has been developing for decades, such as Germany and Romania, the automotive sector

now has a strong ecosystem around it, with a proven supplier for every part or service. What is missing most is

production line installation and service providers, companies providing automation and robot integration solutions -

both the equipment itself and its maintenance and programming services," G. Valuckas is quoted in the report. 

The automotive industry is strictly regulated, so not only manufacturers, but also their suppliers must meet special

requirements. Rytis Bortkevičius, the equipment maintenance manager of CIE LT Forge, which produces parts for

cars, assures that due to the lack of local service providers, the company is forced to abandon some projects. The car

component manufacturer misses mechanical processing, refrigeration equipment service providers the most.

Additional logistics

Lithuanian automobile industry companies mainly cooperate with suppliers in Germany, Romania, Hungary, and

China. 

"Naturally, most of the suppliers are from those countries where the automotive sector is very strong and has been

operating for many years. However, at the same time, we see that Romania and Hungary can oﬀer more competitive

prices for the ﬁnal product, because the ecosystem of suppliers is highly developed, so the supply chain is short and

eﬃcient," says G. Valuckas.

He is also supported by Aidas Andziulis, head of purchases at "Continental" in Lithuania. According to him, costs

increase quite signiﬁcantly due to additional logistics.

"If we have a service to order from Germany, and the Romanians can have a local supplier, our product automatically

becomes 30-40% more expensive", A. Andziulis calculates.

Suppliers in Lithuania simply lack experience in cooperation with the automotive industry. 

"Companies don't even think that they can turn their activities in such a direction to provide services to companies in

the automotive industry. The growth of the entire automotive ecosystem must take place - only then will we have

sustainable growth as a sector", thinks Shayan Ali, Continental's manager in Lithuania.

Maximas Zakletskis, manager of Hella Lithuania, holds a similar opinion.

According to him, there are suppliers in Lithuania, but they are not yet at such a level that they can supply

components to car manufacturing companies. 

"In the automobile industry, the bar for quality assurance conditions has been raised very high, and Lithuanian

manufacturers still need to step up a bit. Both Hella Lithuania and other BACC members are ready to help such

companies meet the requirements. I think that it would be a great help for Lithuanian companies, and the beneﬁts

would be mutual", says M. Zakletskis.

About 15,000 employees work in Lithuanian automotive industry companies, the total annual turnover of the sector

in 2021 amounted to about 875 million Eur.
