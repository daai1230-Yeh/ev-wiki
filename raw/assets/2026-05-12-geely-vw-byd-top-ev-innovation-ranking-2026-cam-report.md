# Geely, VW and BYD retain lead in EV innovation ranking – CAM Electromobility Report 2026

**Author**: Florian Treiss | **Date**: May 12, 2026

---

The German Center of Automotive Management (CAM) has published its Electromobility Report 2026, examining market and innovation trends of 35 leading automotive groups in electric mobility. As in January's ranking, Geely, Volkswagen Group, and BYD occupy the top three positions.

## Methodology

The ranking assessed innovation strength in the battery-electric vehicle sector, evaluating 874 series-production innovations identified between 2020 and 2025 (162 of them introduced in 2025 alone). CAM categorised innovations into: range, charging power, energy consumption, and EV ecosystem, scored using index points (IP).

## Top 10 Rankings

1. **Geely Group** (Lotus, Polestar, Volvo, Zeekr) — 209 IP
2. **Volkswagen Group** — 200 IP
3. **BYD** — 171 IP
4. SAIC Group (incl. MG Motor)
5. **BMW** (biggest climber: rose from 10th to 5th)
6. **Tesla** (biggest faller: dropped from top 3 to 6th)
7. Mercedes-Benz
8. Hyundai
9. Renault
10. General Motors

## Standout Innovations

**Geely**: Lotus Emeya — 450 kW segment-leading charging; Zeekr Mix — 450 kW charging, 10–80% in just 10.5 minutes.

**Volkswagen**: Porsche Cayenne Electric — structurally integrated 113 kWh battery that enhances body rigidity, lowers centre of gravity; Turbo variant delivers 850 kW, 0–100 km/h in 2.5 seconds.

**BYD**: Han L — 1,000 kW dual-gun charging, Blade Battery 2.0 with low-temperature charging at -30°C, motor spinning at 30,511 rpm; Tang L and Yangwang U7 also achieve 1,000 kW and 500 kW charging respectively.

**BMW iX3** (Neue Klasse): up to 805 km range, AI-powered auto-opening charging flap, Plug & Charge Multi Contract supporting up to 10 simultaneous charging contracts.

## Country Analysis

- **China**: 32.4% of all innovation points
- **Germany**: 31.9% of all innovation points (up from ~20% in previous years)

Tesla's decline is attributed to not having introduced a new mass-production model for some time, while Chinese and German manufacturers have accelerated their EV innovation pace.

CAM study leader Stefan Bratzel: "Germany is making significant strides in electric innovation strength: with a share of nearly 32% of global electric innovation strength, German OEMs are now almost on par with Chinese manufacturers—just a few years ago, the gap was much larger."

## Q1 2026 EV Registrations

After a weak 2025, Tesla regained the lead in global EV registrations in Q1 2026, followed by BYD, Geely, VW Group, and SAIC Group.
