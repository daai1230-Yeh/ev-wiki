# SVOLT to start semi-solid-state battery production in September

**Author**: Cora Werwitzke | **Date**: 2026-05-20

---

SVOLT plans to start series production of semi-solid-state batteries in **September 2026**, CEO Yang Hongxin announced via Weibo. Fully solid-state batteries remain far from commercialisation.

## Timeline

- **September 2026**: Series production begins, followed by integration into multiple vehicle models
- **100 kWh battery version** will also achieve large-scale mass production at the same time
- Production costs expected to match those of conventional liquid-electrolyte lithium-ion batteries

This is an acceleration from earlier plans, which called for trial production in Q4 2025 and series production in 2027.

## Technology

Semi-solid-state batteries use a combination of liquid and solid electrolyte materials (solid-liquid hybrid), positioned between traditional lithium-ion and fully solid-state batteries. They promise:
- Improved safety
- Higher energy density
- Longer lifespan vs. conventional lithium-ion

SVOLT's first-generation semi-solid-state batteries target **300 Wh/kg** energy density. A second generation currently in development targets **360 Wh/kg**.

## Market Context

SVOLT is currently China's tenth-largest battery manufacturer. CEO Yang Hongxin believes 2026 will be the year hybrid solid-liquid batteries gain broad acceptance and achieve large-scale market introduction.

SVOLT is reportedly set to supply semi-solid-state batteries for the next generation of **BMW Mini** models. (SVOLT was spun off from Great Wall Motor in 2018; GWM runs a JV with BMW producing the Mini Cooper Electric and Mini Aceman.)

All-solid-state battery commercialisation is described as a "very long-term effort" — semi-solid-state is the dominant path for now.
