# QuantumScape signs Honda as solid-state battery partner

**Author**: Cora Werwitzke | **Date**: 2026-06-19

---

QuantumScape has entered a research cooperation with Honda to advance solid-state battery development. The multi-year collaboration follows a rigorous technology evaluation by Honda R&D and adds to QuantumScape's existing partnership with Volkswagen.

The partners intend to further develop QuantumScape's QS battery platform. The agreement has been signed with Honda R&D, the development arm of Honda Motor, and is structured as a multi-year research programme aimed at advancing solid-state battery technology and associated manufacturing processes.

According to QuantumScape, the current agreement follows a 'technology evaluation agreement with QS, which included an in-depth, hands-on technical study of QS's solid-state technology platform as well as competitive benchmarking across a range of standard technical tests.'

"QS technology demonstrated compelling and unique advantages during our evaluation," said Atsushi Ogawa, Chief Operating Officer, Research Center of Excellence, Honda R&D Co., Ltd. "We see potential for QS technology to add value across a range of applications, including automotive."

QuantumScape is primarily backed by Volkswagen. At the end of 2025, the company completed installation of key equipment for pilot production of its QSE-5 solid-state cells in San Jose, California, with the pilot line inaugurated in February 2026.

The QSE-5 cell is set to become QuantumScape's first commercial product—with a capacity of 5 Ah, an energy density of over 844 Wh/l, and a charging time of 12.2 minutes from 10% to 80% SoC. QuantumScape does not plan to engage in large-scale production itself; instead, it aims to realise gigawatt-hour-scale projects through licensing agreements with partners.

As Honda is not only a car manufacturer but also one of the world's largest motorcycle producers, the research collaboration could potentially place emphasis on two-wheeler applications, though neither company has confirmed such a focus.
