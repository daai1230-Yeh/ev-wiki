# Lucid Lays Off 18% Of Its Workers And Cuts Production At Arizona Plant

**Author**: Suvrat Kothari | **Date**: Jun 22, 2026

It's the company's second round of layoffs this year.

---

Lucid Motors will reduce its U.S. workforce by 18%, the company said in an SEC filing on Monday. The maker of the Air and Gravity electric vehicles has been burning billions amid a slow year of growth for electric vehicle sales, while competition has been steadily growing.

The layoffs are part of a broader restructuring plan that also includes eliminating the second production shift at its AMP-1 factory in Arizona. The company expects the restructuring to save around $158 million, and anticipates cash charges of roughly $32 million for severance, employee benefits, and transition costs.

Lucid also said COO Marc Winterhoff has departed the company, following the elimination of the COO position itself. His exit is the latest in a string of high-profile management changes at Lucid this year. Longtime CEO Peter Rawlinson departed earlier this year; the company eventually brought in Silvio Napoli, the former CEO of Schindler Group, as its new chief executive.

Several high-ranking engineers also departed recently: Emad Dlala (SVP of powertrain and engineering) exited in early June, and Zach Walker (chief engineer of the upcoming midsize platform) departed last month.

This is Lucid's second round of layoffs in 2026, following a 12% workforce reduction in February.

The company is now betting its turnaround on two upcoming mass-market electric crossovers: the Cosmos (starting ~$50,000, to rival Tesla Model Y and Rivian R2, production starting before end of 2026) and the Earth (expected by end of 2027).
