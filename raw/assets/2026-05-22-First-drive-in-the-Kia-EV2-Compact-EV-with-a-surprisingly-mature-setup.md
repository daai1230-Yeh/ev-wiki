# First drive in the Kia EV2: Compact EV with a surprisingly mature setup

**Author**: Daniel Bönnighausen | **Date**: 2026-05-21

**Key specs (EV2 Earth tested):** Battery: 42.2 kWh | Range: 317 km (WLTP) | DC charging: 118 kW | Base price: €26,600

---

With the EV2, Kia is bringing its smallest electric car to Germany so far. The B-segment SUV focuses on efficient packaging, high comfort levels and features previously reserved for the brand's larger models. During an initial test drive, the 4.06-metre-long EV appeared significantly more mature than its compact dimensions suggest. However, the right configuration will determine how suitable the compact Kia is for everyday use.

## Overview

Kia laid the foundation for its new generation of electric vehicles in 2021 with the EV6. Since then, the E-GMP-based model family has expanded rapidly: the EV9 positioned itself as a large SUV with an 800-volt architecture, while the EV3 transferred much of the design language into a more compact format. Kia later added the EV4 and EV5 for additional vehicle segments, while the PV5 marked the manufacturer's entry into the electric van market.

With the EV2, Kia is now completing the lower end of its electric model range. The new B-segment SUV is the brand's smallest battery-electric vehicle to date. The EV2 is built in Žilina, Slovakia, making it Kia's second E-GMP model manufactured in Europe following the EV4.

Since April, Kia has been delivering the first EV2 units to customers in the 'Air' and 'Earth' trim levels. From July, Kia will add variants with the larger battery, including the GT-Line trim, while the entry-level 'Light' version is scheduled to follow in October.

## Design & Exterior

Externally, the EV2 aligns clearly with Kia's current electric design language. The vertical daytime running lights in the Star Map design, the enclosed front grille, and the angular basic shape make the electric SUV instantly recognisable as a Kia electric car. At the same time, the EV2 appears less imposing than its larger siblings. With a length of 4.06 metres, a width of 1.80 metres, a height of 1.58 metres, and a wheelbase of nearly 2.57 metres, the EV2 is clearly positioned in the B-segment.

## Driving Dynamics

The EV2 is initially offered with a smaller LFP battery (42.2 kWh), paired with a 108 kW electric motor delivering 250 Nm of torque. The sprint from 0 to 100 km/h takes 8.7 seconds. From July, a long-range variant with a 61 kWh NMC battery will also be available at 99.5 kW.

The suspension setup consists of MacPherson struts at the front and a torsion beam axle at the rear – clearly tuned with comfort in mind. It absorbs rough surfaces and smaller imperfections effectively, but the comfort-oriented calibration results in slight body roll during more dynamic cornering. The steering remains light, with less road feedback than ideal.

## Efficiency & Charging

Kia states a WLTP range of up to 317 km for the smaller 42.2 kWh battery, while the larger 61 kWh battery extends this to 453 km. During the test drive mix of urban roads, country lanes and motorway sections at single-digit temperatures, energy consumption was 16.2 kWh/100 km, suggesting a real-world range of up to 260 km.

Both battery variants support DC fast charging at up to 118 kW. The smaller battery is expected to charge from 10 to 80% in 29 minutes. The EV2 comes equipped with an 11 kW AC onboard charger as standard, with an optional 22 kW charger available from the 'Earth' trim.

## Interior & Technology

Inside, the EV2 features a triple panoramic display with a 12.3-inch digital instrument cluster, a 5.3-inch climate control display, and a 12.3-inch infotainment screen. The boot offers 362 litres of space, expanding to 1,160 litres with rear seats folded. There is also a small 15-litre frunk.

The main drawback is that the standard system lacks built-in navigation, meaning no integrated EV route planner or intelligent regenerative braking without upgrading trim levels.

## Verdict

Kia's EV2 is a strong contender in the lower-priced EV segment, delivering a higher level of comfort and refinement than typically expected in this class. However, key EV-specific features — navigation with charging planning, the heat pump and the optional 22 kW onboard charger — are tied to higher trim levels and bundled option packages. A well-equipped EV2 is more likely to be priced between €30,000 and €35,000. Even so, the EV2 remains competitively positioned, combining comfort, interior space and range.
