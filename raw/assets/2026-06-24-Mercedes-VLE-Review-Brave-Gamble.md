# The Mercedes VLE Is A Brave Gamble. Will It Pay Off?

**Author**: Chris Rosales | **Date**: June 19, 2026

---

Mercedes' ultra-luxury van could crack Americans' van stigma. But that's only if they don't balk at the price.

## Key Specs (2028 Mercedes-Benz VLE 400)

- **As-tested price**: ~$135,000 (estimated)
- **Battery**: 115 kWh NMC
- **Motor**: Dual permanent-magnet synchronous
- **Output**: 415 hp / 490 lb-ft torque
- **0-60 mph**: 6.4 seconds
- **EV Range**: 435 miles (WLTP)

---

It's a rare thing to see a safe, stable megacorporation take a huge swing. The fact that the Mercedes VLE, an electric luxury van, exists at all is unbelievable. Even stranger? It's coming to the United States.

Mercedes has decided the time is ripe to take a swing at a product category that doesn't currently exist, and to sell three versions: the VLE, VLS, and VLS Maybach. All of them are fully electric, with brand-new platforms. Despite Mercedes saying it won't hit U.S. shores until 2028, the reviewer drove the VLE in Spain.

Mercedes engineered the VLE as a luxury product from the ground up on an all-new electric van platform. The company upped structural rigidity to reduce NVH and packaged the rear suspension tightly to allow for a completely flat floor, baking in more sound insulation than usual. US-bound trim levels will include standard panoramic roof, Burmester stereo, and a triple-screen MBUX infotainment system.

## Driving Impressions

The seating position is high and commanding, but the driving feel is distinctly van-like — the party is in the back. The second row reclining seats are exceptional: materials and quality are impressive, squeaks and rattles were nonexistent (almost unheard of in a modern Benz), whisper-quiet at highway speeds, and ride quality was soft and floaty.

In terms of range and efficiency: the 435-mile WLTP range is impressive, with real-world observed efficiency around 3.6–3.8 mi/kWh in mixed city/expressway driving. On American highways, it's likely to be in the low 3s and high 2s.

MBUX infotainment is improved, with twin 14-inch displays and better accessibility. Most functions require only a single menu jump. Heated/cooled seats were slightly buried in submenus.

## The American Question

Can the VLE succeed in the US? A decently trimmed one will cost around $100,000. For that answer, we'll have to wait and see. But in full Benz spec with AMG Monoblocks and two-tone paint, it's arguably cooler than any SUV.
