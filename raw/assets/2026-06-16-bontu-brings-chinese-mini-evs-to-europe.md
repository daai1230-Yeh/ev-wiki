# Bontu brings Chinese mini EVs to Europe

**Author**: Florian Treiss | **Date**: 2026-06-15

---

Chinese manufacturer Bontu has begun marketing its electric light vehicles in Europe at an event in Milan. The vehicles - two microcars and a mini transporter - will be classified as L6e and L7e vehicles following EU homologation.

Bontu was founded as recently as 2020 in Xintai, in China's Shandong province. Since then, the company has focused on battery-electric micro vehicles, offering a broader range of mini commercial vehicles than passenger models. Bontu's mini transporters are reportedly already highly popular among delivery services in China's megacities.

The company is now entering the European market. At its launch event in Milan, the manufacturer presented three models: the BTE03 mini transporter (L7e, 7.5 kW, 8.35 kWh, 80-100 km range), the BTE05 passenger microcar (L7e, 3 metres long, 13.9-18.1 kWh, 170-222 km range), and the BT09 minicar (L6e/L7e, 2.75 metres, 7.68-10 kWh, 130-150 km range).

The BTE03 mini transporter measures 3.56 metres in length with a payload capacity of 340 kg and a top speed of 81 km/h. The BTE05 offers as much space as an A-segment car and includes ABS, airbags, air conditioning, a 12-inch touchscreen, and regenerative braking. The BT09 is available in two-seat (L6e) and four-seat (L7e) configurations.

EU member states apply different minimum driving ages for these vehicle categories — L6e vehicles can be driven from age 14 in France and Italy but only from age 15 in Germany, while L7e vehicles require age 16 in France/Italy but 18 in Germany (or 17 under accompanied driving).
