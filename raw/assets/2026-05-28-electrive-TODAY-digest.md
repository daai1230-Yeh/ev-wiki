# electrive TODAY — 28 May 2026

**Source**: electrive global (newsletter@electrive.com)

## News
- **EU electric car registrations jump 38% in April** — ACEA: BEV registrations +37.7% YoY to 200,117 units in April; 746,899 units and 19.7% market share so far in 2026.
- **Stellantis to invest €1bn in Mulhouse EV production** — new-generation EVs from 2029, part of France's national electrification strategy (Macron).
- **Scania opens orders for extended-range electric trucks** — seventh battery pack pushes range "far beyond 800 km"; MCS fast-charging now orderable.
- **Xiaomi posts EV business loss in Q1 2026** — ~€400 million quarterly loss despite higher revenue, after an annual profit in 2025.
- **SAIC to launch locally assembled MG4 EV Urban** — assembly started in Thailand, first build outside China.

## Number of the Day
- Turkey now has over 420,000 EVs (423,793 in April, up from 370,591 a year earlier; TurkStat).

## Technology
- **Ex-VW boss Diess plans electric tractor startup** — Diess E-Agrartechnik AG (Munich); mid-range electric tractor with swappable battery next year.
- **China opens first truck battery swap station using Bosch BaaS** — Chizhou, first customer deployment.
- **Dongfeng unveils 400 kW hydrogen fuel cell system** — "Hydrogen Core" for 49-tonne trucks, up to 1,700 km.
- **Orbia wins UK funding for graphite recycling** — £1.4m for a Runcorn pilot to reduce UK dependence on imported graphite.

## Quotes of the Day
- Toyota's Andrea Carlucci: the next Yaris will likely include a BEV version alongside hybrid and petrol.
- Polestar CEO Michael Lohscheller: EV buying motivation has shifted from idealism to money/running costs.

## Fleets + Infrastructure

- **Voltera and Revel merge for US EV charging expansion** — targeting 1,000+ stalls across 11 major metros for fleets and AVs.
- **Citybus orders 20 Wrightbus electric double-deckers** — built in Malaysia, delivered 2027.
- **Wisconsin opens new NEVI funding round** — ~$40 million remaining for fast-charging expansion.
