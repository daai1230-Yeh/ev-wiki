# The 2027 BMW iX5's Monster Battery Pack Dwarfs The Competition

**Author**: Suvrat Kothari | **Date**: June 15, 2026

---

The electric X5 is coming soon. Expect it to be a range king thanks to one of the biggest battery packs in the segment.

## Key Points

- BMW iX5 will pack **144 kWh usable** battery (US) / 141 kWh (Europe)
- The iX5 xDrive 60 will have the largest battery ever installed in an electric BMW
- BMW hasn't officially confirmed range figures yet, but the iX3 and i3 both deliver 400+ miles from a 112 kWh pack
- The iX5 uses **800-volt architecture** and sixth-generation cylindrical cells from Neue Klasse technology
- Curb weight of roughly **3,000 kg (~6,600 lbs)** — one of the heaviest EVs in its segment

## Battery Context

To put 144 kWh in context:
- Porsche Cayenne Electric: ~113 kWh gross
- Lucid Gravity Grand Touring: 123 kWh gross, delivers 400+ miles EPA
- Rivian R1S Max pack: 140 kWh usable (closest rival)

The iX5 out-batteries all of those by a significant margin.

## Platform and Technology

The iX5 pushes the boundaries of BMW's Cluster Architecture (primarily a combustion engine platform re-engineered for EVs). Unlike the iX3 which rides on the wholly new Neue Klasse platform, the iX5 sticks with previous-gen underpinnings but borrows key Neue Klasse technologies:
- Sixth-generation cylindrical cells
- 800-volt architecture
- Cell-to-pack technology (eliminating modules and structural layers to reduce weight)

BMW applied clever suspension technology to mask the weight: decoupled springs and dampers with a larger air chamber internal volume. The adaptive dampers and anti-roll bars were also repositioned for better balance.

## Powertrain Options

BMW plans to offer five powertrain options for the X5:
1. Battery electric (iX5)
2. Mild hybrid gasoline
3. Mild hybrid diesel
4. Plug-in hybrid
5. **First-ever hydrogen fuel-cell X5**

BMW said it's now completing final calibration drives at its Spartanburg plant.
