# This Is The Future Of Hyundai Software—And Yes, It Comes With Buttons

**Author**: Mack Hogan | **Date**: 2026-04-20

---

Hyundai's new "Pleos" operating system just debuted on the Ioniq 3. It's coming to a Hyundai near you soon.

The new Hyundai Ioniq 3 runs on Pleos OS, the company's new operating system for software-defined vehicles. With zonal controllers, physical controls, and a new UI, Pleos could match legacy-car ergonomics with modern software advantages—a needed upgrade, as Hyundai's software experience has lagged behind rivals.

**What is Pleos?** Pleos is Hyundai's new operating system for software-defined vehicles. It is based on Google's Android Automotive OS—not Android Auto phone projection. Android Automotive underpins cars from Ford, Honda, BMW, and Porsche. Hyundai is pushing its own App Market rather than full Google Automotive Services, suggesting a more in-house approach.

The combined home screen features a massive map and persistent controls below and to the left, similar to Tesla or Rivian—but with a key difference: Hyundai still offers physical controls below the screen for essential functions including volume, temperature controls, and heated/cooled seats. There are also physical buttons on the steering wheel and a driver information display above it. Hyundai maintains it will always offer physical controls for key functions.

**Why it matters architecturally:** Pleos is a true software-defined vehicle platform with zonal controllers. This reduces wiring, decreases complexity, and improves serviceability and OTA upgradeability. Currently, Hyundai EVs use patched-together software from countless vendors, limiting meaningful OTA updates. Pleos is designed to solve this.

The Ioniq 3 is a subcompact EV unlikely to reach the United States. However, Pleos will form the basis for all future Hyundai EVs—making this debut significant for the brand's global software transition.

The author notes: Hyundai already makes efficient, quick-charging, comfortable EVs—the Ioniq 5 and Ioniq 9 are top of class. The software experience has been the weak link. Pleos looks visually modern and structurally superior. "It proves that Hyundai isn't standing still, and is instead marching toward a software-defined, fully electric future."
