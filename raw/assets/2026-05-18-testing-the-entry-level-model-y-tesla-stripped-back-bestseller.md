# Testing the entry-level Model Y: Where and how Tesla stripped back its bestseller

**Author**: Sebastian Schaal | **Date**: 2026-05-16

**Facts**
- Model: Tesla Model Y Rear-Wheel Drive
- Battery: 60 kWh
- Range: 534 km (WLTP)
- DC Charging Power: 175 kW
- Price: 39,990 Euro

---

Tesla's new registrations in the EU surged by 59.6 per cent in the first quarter of the year. The company has evolved from a niche manufacturer into a mainstream player, and the significant increase in European sales at the start of 2026 is primarily due to the simpler and more affordable versions of the Model 3 and Model Y, available since autumn. The base Model Y Rear-Wheel Drive starts at 39,990 euros in Germany, with subsidies potentially reducing it to around 30,990 euros.

**Powertrain & Efficiency**

The base Model Y is equipped with a roughly 60 kWh LFP battery, rated for up to 534 kilometres of WLTP range. In testing, consumption ranged between 14 and 16 kWh/100km, sometimes dropping to 12 kWh/100km in urban traffic. The charging peak reached around 140 kW at Superchargers (vs. stated 175 kW maximum), and the battery charged from 10 to 80 per cent in 25 minutes.

The power delivery is significantly smoother than previous rear-wheel-drive models — the electric kick that characterized earlier versions is gone. The 0-100 km/h sprint of 7.2 seconds is adequate but no longer exhilarating.

**Key Cost-Cutting Measures**
- Simpler LED headlights (no matrix function), different front end design
- Fabric seats instead of vegan leather, manual steering wheel adjustment
- Single-glazed side windows (vs. double-glazed in Premium)
- 7-speaker audio system (vs. 15 speakers in Premium)
- No ambient lighting or rear passenger display
- Smaller frunk with textile lining (instead of plastic tray)
- No HEPA filter for air conditioning
- No parcel shelf (available as 135-euro accessory)

**What's Retained**
- Spacious boot with deep side storage
- Generous rear legroom
- 1,600 kg towing capacity (with optional 1,350-euro tow bar)
- 75 kg roof load capacity
- Two inductive smartphone charging pads
- Full practicality of the larger Model Y

**Conclusion**

Tesla has achieved a practical family vehicle with an excellent price-to-performance ratio. The functional differences are primarily "different" rather than worse. The Model Y Rear-Wheel Drive succeeds as a volume model targeting buyers not willing to pay for premium features, though the clear distinction in bodywork between variants (different front/rear sections) raises questions about whether such visible differentiation was necessary.
