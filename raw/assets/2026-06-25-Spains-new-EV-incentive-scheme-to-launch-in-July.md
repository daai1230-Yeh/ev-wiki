# Spain's new EV incentive scheme to launch in July

**Author**: Florian Treiss | **Date**: 24.06.2026

---

Spain's new 'Plan Auto+' subsidy program is nearly ready for launch. Applications can reportedly be submitted from July, and retroactively for EVs purchased from January 1, 2026. The maximum grant is €4,500 per vehicle, but complex conditions apply.

Spain has restructured its electromobility incentives at the beginning of 2026, dividing them into three pillars. This article focuses on the previously announced 'Plan Auto+' programme, which is now managed by the central government rather than the autonomous regions, unlike its predecessor, Moves III, which expired at the end of 2025. Although the programme was approved in February – similar to the new German EV incentive scheme – Spaniards had to wait several months before applications could be submitted.

However, the scheme now appears set to launch. According to the automotive trade publication La Tribuna de Automoción, the detailed funding conditions and application process for Plan Auto+ will be published in the Spanish Official Gazette in early July. Applications can then be submitted retroactively for battery-electric vehicles, plug-in hybrids, and range-extended electric vehicles purchased since 1 January 2026.

According to the report, more than 50 per cent of the total €400 million in allocated funds may already be accounted for by vehicles purchased since the beginning of the year. As a result, La Tribuna de Automoción anticipates that the funds could be exhausted as early as September or October.

The structure of the incentive scheme is relatively complex. Unlike the German programme, it does not include income-based tiers, income thresholds, or a child bonus. Nevertheless, Spain has established a set of criteria that result in varying levels of funding depending on the application.

The maximum state funding for a battery-electric passenger car is €4,500 euros, with an additional mandatory discount of €1,000 euros from the manufacturer or dealer (resulting in total savings of up to €5,500 euros). The new system no longer includes a separate scrappage bonus for old vehicles. The actual amount of funding depends on a new four-pillar system:

- **Powertrain type** (50% / €2,250): Full points only for pure electric vehicles (CERO label). PHEVs/REEVs receive significantly less (€1,125).
- **Purchase price** (25% / €1,125): Only vehicles with net price up to €45,000 eligible. Under €35,000 net gets full 25%; €35,001–€45,000 gets 15% (€675).
- **European manufacturing** (15% / €675): Only vehicles assembled in the EU qualify.
- **Battery origin** (10% / €450): Battery assembly in Europe is sufficient (not cell production).

For example, an affordable, fully European-built EV like the Cupra Raval or VW ID. Polo under €35,000 net secures the full €4,500 grant plus the €1,000 dealer discount. Vehicles over €45,000 are not eligible.

The scheme also supports electric commercial vehicles up to 3,500 kg, electric motorcycles, and light vehicles in L6e and L7e categories. Private individuals, self-employed persons, and small businesses can all apply.
