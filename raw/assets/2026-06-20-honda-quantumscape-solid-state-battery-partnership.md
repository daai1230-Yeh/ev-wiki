# Honda Has A New Partner For Solid-State EV Batteries

**Date**: 2026-06-19

---

Honda is teaming up with QuantumScape, a battery startup, to develop solid-state batteries for EVs and other applications.

## Partnership Details

Honda and QuantumScape entered a "joint research agreement" that includes a multi-year plan focused on solid-state battery development and associated manufacturing processes. Honda had been quietly evaluating QuantumScape's technology before making the partnership public.

## Context

Honda recently scrapped plans for a series of next-generation EVs that were supposed to start hitting the US market this year. Despite that setback, the QuantumScape partnership shows Honda still views EVs as a significant long-term opportunity, with next-generation battery technology as a key enabler.

## QuantumScape Background

QuantumScape is an American solid-state battery startup that has been developing its lithium-metal solid-state technology for over a decade, with Volkswagen Group as a major investor. In early 2026, QuantumScape inaugurated its Eagle Line — a suite of highly automated equipment incorporating its proprietary Cobra process, representing a blueprint for production-scale manufacturing.

## Honda's Solid-State Battery Plans

Honda has been independently developing its own all-solid-state battery technology. Its performance targets are ambitious:
- 50% smaller than current lithium-ion batteries
- 35% lighter
- 25% cheaper
- Driving range exceeding 620 miles (potentially growing to 700+ miles)

Honda operates a demonstration production facility for solid-state cells and aims to install the technology in production vehicles in the second half of this decade.
