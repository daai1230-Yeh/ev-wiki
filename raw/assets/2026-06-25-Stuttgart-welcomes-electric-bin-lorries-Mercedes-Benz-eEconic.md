# Stuttgart welcomes four electric bin lorries

**Author**: Cora Werwitzke | **Date**: 24.06.2026

---

Mercedes-Benz Trucks has handed over the first five of a total of ten eEconic vehicles to the Stuttgart waste management authority (AWS). The electric refuse vehicles are stationed at a newly established depot in Stuttgart-Wangen, and are performing well despite the city's challenging topography.

The five Mercedes-Benz eEconic 300 trucks are equipped with approximately 300 kWh of usable battery capacity each. They feature a Faun 'Variopress' rear loader (21 m³) and a Faun Skylift tipping mechanism. To support this fleet (plus five more arriving later), AWS designed a dedicated new site at Gingener Straße 2 in Stuttgart-Wangen, equipped with nine ceiling-mounted truck charging units, passenger car chargers, two DC fast-charging points, and one of the largest photovoltaic systems on a municipal building in Stuttgart.

Alongside the ten electric eEconic trucks, AWS also procured ten diesel-powered Mercedes-Benz Econic models. Of AWS's ~1,100 vehicle fleet, roughly 120 refuse collection vehicles are currently in use across the city.

Markus Töpfer, Managing Director of AWS: "The electric refuse collection vehicles represent a major leap forward for Stuttgart. Despite the city's challenging basin topography, the eEconic trucks have proven their performance. Their quiet operation makes communication in the cab easier and reduces the physical strain of daily work."
