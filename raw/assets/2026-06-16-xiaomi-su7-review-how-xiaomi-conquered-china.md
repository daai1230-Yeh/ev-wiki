# The Xiaomi SU7 Made Me Understand How Xiaomi Conquered China

**Author**: Kevin Williams | **Date**: 2026-06-15

---

The updated SU7 Sedan is great, and the way it got here should strike fear in the hearts of every global auto manufacturer.

Xiaomi flew a group of journalists to Beijing to drive both the facelifted SU7 sedan and the SU7 Ultra — a car that, in prototype form, is the fastest four-door car to ever lap the Nürburgring. The visit included a tour of Xiaomi's phone factory (83% automated, one phone every 6 seconds) and R&D facility.

**The Philosophy**: "Everything we make, we think to ourselves, is it cool enough?" — VP and CMO Xu Fei. Xiaomi targets younger buyers who want a premium experience without being affluent. It dominates by delivering coolness at competitive prices.

**Updated SU7 key changes (2026 "second generation"):**
- Staggered tires (wider rear) for improved handling
- Roof bisected by support beam (stiffer structure)
- All except base trim get CDC (continuous damping control) and adjustable air suspension
- LIDAR now standard across all trims
- 800V+ architecture standard; Max trim uses 897V
- Max trim: 530 kW peak charging (up from 235 kW on lower trims)
- Max trim: 680 hp dual motor, 101.7 kWh NMC battery; ~$50,000–53,000 USD equivalent

**Driving impressions (Max trim)**: Neutral handling, stiff chassis, grippy on Michelin Pilot Sport tires. Comfortable air suspension for daily use, yet 3.1 second 0–60 mph. Interior is spacious, premium materials, extremely fast and responsive infotainment. Wireless Apple CarPlay supported.

**The manufacturing edge**: Xiaomi's car body shop is 91% automated with only 100 of its ~2,000 auto workers there. At full capacity the line produces one car every 76 seconds — the same hyper-automated philosophy from its phones applied to EVs. This is why legacy automakers are scared.

**Xiaomi's global ambitions**: Already Europe's third-largest phone brand (after Apple and Samsung). Has opened a European R&D center and poached talent from BMW and Lamborghini. Next model likely a three-row extended-range SUV for the premium China market.

> Ford CEO Jim Farley famously said he "didn't want to give it back" after driving the SU7 for competitive analysis. After a day with the car, the author felt the same.
