# VW software firm Cariad opens AI Hub in Berlin

**Author**: Cora Werwitzke | **Date**: 24.06.2026

---

Volkswagen's software subsidiary Cariad is consolidating part of its team from seven locations into a single central hub — an 'Automotive Software Campus' in Berlin — as part of ongoing cost-cutting measures.

Around 1,000 AI experts are now working at the Berlin campus, developing AI technologies for software-defined vehicles (SDVs). Cariad was originally founded in 2020 to develop software for VW Group vehicles, but faced significant setbacks (delays, errors), and was subsequently downsized under CEO Oliver Blume. Software partnerships with Rivian and Xpeng were established. Today, Cariad operates in a reduced, coordinative role, focusing on autonomous driving, infotainment, and cloud computing.

**Key AI projects at the Berlin campus:**
- **CARIAD-ADAS-Stack**: An integrated automated driving system; first version to debut in the VW ID. EVERY1
- **CARIAD Digital Assistant / Voice Pilot**: AI-based voice assistant debuting in the Porsche Macan and Cayenne Electric, understanding driver intentions and handling complex route planning

VW Group CEO Oliver Blume: "The AI-defined vehicle stands for the next generation of mobility. At the Automotive Software Campus in Berlin, technology is being created that makes driving even safer, more comfortable and more attractive."

Cariad currently employs ~5,000 staff across software centres in Germany, USA, China, Estonia, and India (down from 6,000+ at peak). Volkswagen plans three SDV architectures going forward: Global Architecture (Cariad), SDV East (Xpeng partnership), and SDV West (Rivian partnership).
