# Forget Solid-State. This EV Battery Breakthrough Is Ready To Upend The Market Now

**Author**: Suvrat Kothari | **Date**: Jun 18, 2026

---

General Motors is betting on silicon anodes as the next leap in EV battery technology, while solid-state batteries remain years from commercialization.

"We believe silicon is the next anode technology," said Kurt Kelty, VP of battery and sustainability at GM, at the GM Empower conference in San Francisco.

## Why Silicon Anodes

Today's anodes are almost universally graphite — stable but energy-constrained. By replacing graphite with silicon (in part or fully), batteries can store more ions. The challenge: pure silicon anodes swell; blended silicon-graphite anodes manage this.

**Key benefits vs. solid-state timeline**: Silicon anode batteries are already in production now — not waiting until "end of the decade" like solid-state. High-end silicon anodes already power smartphones and select vehicles.

## Examples In Production

- **Amprius Technologies**: Claims a 310-mile EV could achieve 574 miles with its silicon anode battery
- **Sila**: High-silicon anodes boost range by 20% with no larger pack. Factory in Moses Lake, WA operational (initial capacity: 50,000 EVs/year; expansion potential: 2.5M EVs). Supply agreements with Mercedes-Benz and Panasonic (Tesla's primary supplier)
- **Group14**: Producing silicon-anode materials at South Korea factory (up to 10 GWh, 100,000+ EVs). Currently powers the McMurtry Spéirling hypercar (0-60 mph in 1.55 seconds)
- **Mercedes-Benz AMG GT**: Silicon-containing anodes enable 10–80% charge in 11 minutes at 600 kW

## GM's Multi-Chemistry Approach

GM continues pursuing multiple chemistries:
- Silicon anodes (near/mid-term)
- Lithium-manganese-rich (LMR) batteries for large SUVs/pickups (2028)
- High-nickel batteries (current lineup)
- LFP on Chevy Bolt
- Sodium-ion for grid-scale storage
- Solid-state (prototypes in labs, testing underway)
