# Stellantis plans 29 new electric vehicles and a new platform for all drive types

**Author**: Florian Treiss | **Date**: 2026-05-21

---

Stellantis has unveiled its strategic plan for the next five years called 'FaSTLAne 2030.' The European-American automotive group intends to invest €60 billion during this period to accelerate its growth and increase profitability. The focus will be on the four brands: Fiat, Peugeot, Jeep, and Ram, as well as a new modular architecture called STLA One.

## Key Announcements

At its Investor Day at the company's North American headquarters in Auburn Hills, Michigan, the 15-member Stellantis Leadership Team, led by CEO Antonio Filosa, unveiled the 'FaSTLAne 2030' programme.

CEO Antonio Filosa stated: "FaSTLAne 2030 is the result of months of disciplined work across the Company and is designed to drive long-term profitable growth."

## Six Strategic Pillars

**1) Brand Portfolio Realignment:** Jeep, Ram, Fiat, and Peugeot will serve as global powerhouses, receiving 70% of brand and product investments. Chrysler, Dodge, Citroën, Opel, and Alfa Romeo will be managed as regional brands. DS Automobiles and Lancia positioned as more local brands.

Stellantis plans to launch more than 60 new models and introduce 50 major model updates across all brands by 2030, including:
- 29 battery-electric models
- 15 plug-in hybrid or range-extended models
- 24 hybrid vehicles
- 39 vehicles with internal combustion or mild-hybrid powertrains

**2) STLA One Platform:** A new modular vehicle architecture that will replace five existing platforms, covering segments B, C, and D and supporting all drive types. It will serve as the foundation for 30 models across different brands by 2035, reaching a volume of 2 million units. Key features:
- 800-volt architecture for ultra-fast charging
- Cell-to-Body concept integrating the battery into the vehicle structure
- STLA Brain scalable central computing and software architecture
- STLA SmartCockpit for enhanced driver interaction
- STLA AutoDrive for scalable autonomous driving
- Steer-by-Wire integration
- All technologies expected to launch in 2027

**3) Strategic Partnerships:** Stellantis plans to expand existing collaborations including: Leapmotor (with production at Zaragoza and Madrid plants), Dongfeng/Voyah brand in Europe, Jaguar Land Rover for US market synergies. Technology partners include Applied Intuition, Qualcomm, Wayve, NVIDIA, Uber, Mistral AI, and CATL.

**4) Production Optimization:** In Europe, production capacity will be reduced by more than 800,000 units by repurposing plants and leveraging partnerships.

**5) Execution Excellence:** Vehicle development cycles targeted at 24 months (vs. up to 40 months today). Annual cost savings of €6 billion expected by 2028. Over 120 AI applications already in use.

**6) Regional Strengthening:** Decision-making shifted from headquarters to regional level. A new E-Car project will introduce a new generation of affordable urban EVs from 2028.
