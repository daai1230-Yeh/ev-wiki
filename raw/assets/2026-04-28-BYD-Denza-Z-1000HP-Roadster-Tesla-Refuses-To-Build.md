# BYD's Denza Z Is The 1,000-HP Roadster Tesla Refuses To Build

**Author**: InsideEVs | **Date**: 2026-04-27

---

BYD has revealed the Denza Z, an electric convertible with over 1,000 horsepower that debuted in production form at Auto China 2026 in Beijing. The car positions itself squarely against the Tesla Roadster 2.0, which has been continually postponed by the Elon Musk-led automaker.

## Performance

The Denza Z features a combined output of approximately 1,000 horsepower, enabling a zero-to-60 mph sprint in under two seconds. The vehicle is equipped with DiSus-M electromagnetic suspension and BYD's latest "God's Eye" advanced driver assistance system. Its battery pack is compatible with BYD's Flash chargers, which deliver up to 1,500 kilowatts of power for sub-10-minute top-ups.

## Design and Variants

The Z will be offered in three versions: a traditional coupe, a soft-top convertible, and a "track" variant. The design was led by renowned German automotive designer Wolfgang Egger, now BYD's Global Design Director, who previously worked at Audi and Alfa Romeo.

## Global Launch Plans

BYD is prioritizing Europe over its home market for this car. The Denza Z is set to make a dynamic debut at the Goodwood Festival of Speed in the United Kingdom later this year. This move underscores BYD's ambitions to compete at the luxury performance segment globally — a space Tesla has so far left uncontested.

Ford CEO Jim Farley recently noted: "Nothing against Tesla—they've been doing great—but they really don't have an updated vehicle," reflecting the broader industry sentiment that Chinese manufacturers like BYD are setting a new competitive bar.
