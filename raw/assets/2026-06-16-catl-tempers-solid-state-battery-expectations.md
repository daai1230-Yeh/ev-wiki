# CATL tempers near-term expectations for solid-state batteries

**Author**: Cora Werwitzke | **Date**: 2026-06-15

---

Chinese battery giant CATL says we're still several years away from mass adoption of pure solid-state batteries in EVs. CATL CEO Robin Zeng also considers it likely that batteries with solid electrolytes will initially only be installed in premium automobile platforms.

In an interview with Caijing magazine, Zeng explained that commercialisation of solid-state batteries in vehicles still requires technological breakthroughs. He considers mass commercialisation by 2030 unlikely and expects initial applications will only be feasible for vehicles priced above 250,000 yuan (~€32,000).

CATL's all-solid-state technology is currently at level 4 of the 9-level Technology Readiness Level (TRL) scale — still in laboratory validation and prototype development. CATL has been targeting a first small batch of solid-state batteries by 2027. The company is focusing on sulphide-based solid electrolytes, with pilot production reportedly reaching 500 Wh/kg energy density. Cost remains a key hurdle: sulphide-based solid-state cells are currently 3-5x more expensive than conventional lithium-ion cells.

In early 2024, CATL joined the China All-Solid-State Battery Collaborative Innovation Platform (CASIP) — a state-initiated consortium including CALB, EVE Energy, SVOLT, Gotion, BYD's FinDreams Battery, and major automotive manufacturers — aiming to develop competitive solid-state batteries and a supply chain by 2030.
