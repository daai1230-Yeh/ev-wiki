# Stellantis accelerates European small EV offensive

**Author**: electrive | **Date**: 2026-05-19

---

Stellantis will start building a new generation of compact electric vehicles from 2028 at its Pomigliano plant near Naples, Italy. The models, grouped under the "E-Car" project, will rely on technologies developed with selected partners.

Key points:

- The E-Car is positioned to be "produced in Europe for Europeans" from 2028 — described as small, innovative, affordable, fully electric and modern in design.
- CEO Antonio Filosa framed it as a natural fit for Stellantis' deep European small-car heritage, as the company tries to strengthen its position in the affordable BEV segment.
- Pomigliano currently builds the Fiat Panda/Pandina and Alfa Romeo Tonale, with no EVs produced there yet.
- The move is part of Stellantis' broader response to demand for affordable, European-made small EVs.
