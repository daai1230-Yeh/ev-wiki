# Royal Enfield Plans a New Andhra Pradesh Manufacturing Plant with Rs 2,500 Crore Investment

**Author**: Aditya Yadav | **Date**: 2026-05-20 | **Source**: Motoroids

---

Royal Enfield is preparing for another big expansion in India. The company has announced plans to build a new manufacturing facility in Andhra Pradesh with an investment of around Rs 2,500 crore. The new plant is expected to come up in Tada, located in the Tirupati district.

For Royal Enfield, this step comes at a time when demand for its motorcycles continues to stay strong across India and international markets. From the Classic 350 to the Interceptor 650, the brand has steadily grown over the last few years, and its current production setup is already running close to full capacity.

The new project will move ahead in phases and will depend on future demand and market conditions. Final approval from the company's board is still pending.

## Capacity Context

Royal Enfield currently has an annual production capacity of about 14.6 lakh motorcycles, which is nearing full utilisation. In February 2026, the company had already announced a separate Rs 958 crore investment to expand capacity at its Cheyyar plant in Tamil Nadu, taking total annual production capacity to 20 lakh units.

The Andhra Pradesh facility in Tada (Tirupati district) will add significant further capacity, positioned to serve both domestic demand and growing international exports.

## Strategic Significance

This expansion underscores Royal Enfield's confidence in sustained demand growth. The brand has been expanding internationally with models like the Himalayan and Interceptor 650 gaining traction in Europe and the Americas. The new facility will help the company scale production to meet both domestic and global demand targets.
