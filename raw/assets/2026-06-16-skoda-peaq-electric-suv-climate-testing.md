# Škoda puts upcoming electric SUV Peaq through extensive climate testing

**Author**: Cora Werwitzke | **Date**: 2026-06-15

---

Around one week before a world premiere on June 23, Škoda has shared insights into the test program for its large electric SUV, the Peaq. This nearly 4.90-metre-long EV will round off Škoda's all-electric lineup at the top end starting from mid-2026.

Prototypes of the new Škoda flagship model have covered over 1.5 million kilometres during testing across three continents, including the Arizona desert and locations 200 kilometres north of the Arctic Circle where temperatures drop to −40 °C.

The Škoda Peaq is built on the Volkswagen Group's MEB+ platform and features new electric motors, batteries offering over 600 kilometres of range, and an adaptive DCC suspension system. It accommodates five or seven passengers depending on configuration.

Powertrain specifications:
- **Peaq 60** (RWD): 150 kW, 63 kWh, >460 km WLTP, 0-100 in 8.6s
- **Peaq 90** (RWD): 210 kW, 91 kWh, >600 km WLTP, 0-100 in 7.1s
- **Peaq 90x** (AWD): 220 kW, 91 kWh, >600 km WLTP, 0-100 in 6.7s

At nearly 4.90 metres in length with a wheelbase of 2.97 metres, the Peaq will be the largest and most spacious model in Škoda's portfolio. The world premiere is set for June 23 at 18:25 via Škoda's official channels.
