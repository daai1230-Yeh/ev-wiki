## Plug In: Big Moves In Battery Recycling
Two developments highlight growing momentum in EV battery reuse and recycling. Waymo announced a deal with energy storage company B2U to repurpose retired robotaxi batteries (from end-of-life Jaguar I-Pace packs) into "hundreds of megawatts" of grid-scale stationary storage, starting in Texas and California — following similar deals from Rivian and General Motors with Redwood Materials. Separately, Colorado passed a law requiring automakers to handle end-of-life management for EV/PHEV batteries: landfill disposal will be banned starting 2029, and recyclers must capture 80% of a battery's lithium by 2035 — the first state policy of its kind. A related RMI report suggests that with sufficient recycling capacity, the world may not need to mine new battery minerals after 2050.

## Driver's Seat: A Hyundai Ioniq 5 Road Trip
Suvrat Kothari drove a Hyundai Ioniq 5 over 560 miles in Upstate New York, spending $80 on public fast charging versus an estimated $100+ in gas for a comparable 25-mpg SUV (gas at $4.58/gallon). Findings: EV rentals are cheaper on holiday weekends; 20-minute fast-charge stops paired well with meal breaks; the Ioniq 5 drives smoothly with strong torque. Downsides: a hybrid like the RAV4 would cost slightly less to refuel but is far pricier to rent; the older Ioniq 5 model lacked NACS, wireless Android Auto, and USB-C; and charging costs varied significantly by location (0.65 cents/kWh near NYC vs. 0.47 cents/kWh upstate).
