# Kia EV5 review: Not the fastest, but one of the most impressive

**Author**: Daniel Bönnighausen | **Date**: 18.06.2026

**Model**: Kia EV5 GT-Line | **Battery**: 81.4 kWh | **Range**: 503 km (WLTP) | **DC charging**: 150 kW | **Base price**: 51,990 Euro

---

With the EV5, Kia has launched an electric compact SUV that does not seek to stand out through headline-grabbing specifications. Instead, the 4.61-metre-long model focuses on the qualities that matter most in one of Europe's largest vehicle segments.

The EV5 fills a key gap in Kia's line-up. While the carmaker has steadily expanded its electric vehicle portfolio, it has lacked a conventionally styled battery-electric SUV in the same segment as the Sportage. The EV5 now takes on that role, serving as the electric counterpart to one of Kia's most important models.

## Driving Experience

The front-wheel-drive EV5 delivers a composed driving experience. The powertrain provides smooth and controllable power delivery, without the typical abrupt acceleration associated with electric vehicles. The low noise levels—especially on the motorway—and the overall comfort-oriented tuning are among the most striking strengths of this variant.

Kia has implemented the regeneration system particularly well. Drivers can choose between several recuperation levels using the steering-wheel paddles, and the EV5 retains the last selected setting after the vehicle is switched off. The i-Pedal function enables true one-pedal driving and reliably brings the EV5 to a complete stop.

## Energy Consumption and Range

On a longer motorway journey, the EV5 recorded an average consumption of 22.5 kWh/100 km in cold, wet conditions. In more favourable conditions: 20.3 kWh/100 km. In urban/rural driving: under 17 kWh/100 km.

## Charging

The EV5 uses a 400-volt system (not 800V like EV6/EV9), with maximum DC charging power of 150 kW. Kia quotes 30 minutes for 10–80%. AC charging: 11 kW standard, 22 kW optional (€990 for GT-Line).

## Space and Practicality

Rear space is exceptionally generous — even behind a 1.90m driver, rear passengers have ample knee and headroom. Boot: 566 litres, expanding to 1,650 litres with seats folded.

**Conclusion**: The EV5 is not a class leader in every discipline, but it is a very well-rounded representative of its segment. The Earth trim is particularly recommendable. For those seeking an electric SUV as a comfortable family, company, or long-distance car, the EV5 is a serious alternative to the established competition.
