# BYD launches the Great Tang electric SUV in China

**Date**: 2026-06-18

---

BYD is launching its seven-seat eSUV, the Great Tang, in China. The 5.26-meter-long electric model achieves a range of up to 950 km according to Chinese (CLTC) standards and costs the equivalent of €35,500–€39,800. The new Great Han flagship sedan will follow with a slight delay.

## Overview

BYD has celebrated the market launch of the Great Tang, a new seven-seater flagship SUV in the D-segment. It is now the premium model in the 'Dynasty' series, measuring 5.26 metres in length and available in four variants. Prices range from 239,900 to 309,900 yuan (approx. €35,500–€39,800).

- Entry-level RWD: 800 km CLTC range
- Single-motor long-range: 950 km CLTC range
- AWD versions: 850 km CLTC range

BYD first unveiled the Great Tang at Auto China in April, and demand has been extraordinary — 150,000 pre-orders were recorded, prompting BYD to delay the market launch twice to ensure sufficient production capacity.

## Technical Highlights

- **Battery**: 105.7 kWh or 130.1 kWh
- **Architecture**: 1,000-volt high-voltage
- **Fast charging**: BYD 'Flash-Charging' — 10% to 97% in 9 minutes
- **Motor output**: 300 or 370 kW (AWD up to 585 kW)
- **Top speed**: 250 km/h
- **Suspension**: Dual-chamber air suspension with 100 mm adjustment range + predictive chassis control ('Road Preview')
- **Steering**: Rear-axle steering, max 7° angle; turning circle 5.2 m; enables 'Crab Walk' diagonal movement
- **ADAS**: BYD 'God's Eye 5.0' with roof-mounted LiDAR, supports assisted driving in urban traffic and on motorways

## Market Context

The Great Tang fills a gap in BYD's 300,000-yuan price segment, creating "a clearly tiered product matrix alongside the Denza and Yangwang brands," according to CN EV Post. The model competes in China's premium electric SUV space against domestic rivals.

BYD also confirmed the related Great Han flagship saloon will follow the Great Tang to market with a slight delay.
