# Škoda Peaq makes its world debut – offering practical space for seven

**Author**: Christoph Schwarzer | **Date**: 23.06.2026 (updated 24.06.2026)

---

Hot on the heels of the compact Epiq, Skoda introduces the Peaq - its largest SUV to date with an expected base price of €49,900 euros. The Peaq is positioned as Škoda's EV flagship: 4.87m long, with a wheelbase of 2.97m and seating for up to seven.

**Specifications:**

| | Peaq 60 | Peaq 90 | Peaq 90x |
|---|---|---|---|
| Drive | RWD | RWD | AWD |
| Output | 150 kW | 210 kW | 220 kW |
| WLTP range | 456 km | 642 km | 609 km |
| Battery (net) | 59 kWh | 86 kWh | 86 kWh |
| DC Charging | 160 kW | 199 kW | 199 kW |
| Price | €49,900 | €57,900 | €60,400 |

Key highlights:
- 935L of boot space (five-seater), 37L frunk
- Towing: up to 2,000 kg (braked) on AWD model
- Supports V2L, V2H, and Vehicle-to-Grid (V2G) via Moon (not Elli)
- NMC battery chemistry (not LFP)
- Heat pump is standard in Germany
- UK orders open September at £51,980 (~€60,283) for seven-seater

The Peaq competes against the Kia EV9 (from €63,690) and Hyundai Ioniq 9 (from €69,850), offering comparable space and range at a more accessible price point.
