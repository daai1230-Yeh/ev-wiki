EMP: The Izera factory is not enough. Poland needs a production and development 

hub 

April 8, 2025 

ElectroMobility Poland CEO Tomasz Kędzierski wants the Polish electric car project to 

give way to a vision of a modern production and development hub. The goal is not only 

to produce a European car brand together with a global partner, but to create a market 

entity with headquarters in Poland. 

According  to  the  EMP  president,  the  industry's  situation  in  Europe  is  deteriorating 

significantly. This is caused by strong competition from Chinese automotive companies 

and geopolitical changes. The turbulent market situation is additionally intensified by 

the introduction of tariffs on cars, parts and components by the United States. 

In his opinion, for Poland as one of the key suppliers for car manufacturers, this may 

be a big challenge, but also an opportunity for a strategic change of position in the 

international arena. This is conditioned by the location of new car production plants, 

which will influence the development of competences in areas such as ICT, automation, 

engineering and innovation. 

–  Successful countries do not limit themselves to importing solutions, but actively 

participate  in  their  creation,  implementation  and  improvement.  Economic  growth 

based on technology requires long-term strategies in the area of education, research 

and  development  infrastructure  and  cooperation  between  the  public  and  private 

sectors – said Tomasz Kędzierski, president of Electromobility Poland, quoted in the 

company's press release. 

More than a factory 

It presents a modified vision of the project, in which ElectroMobility Poland, together 

with  a  global  partner,  will  co-create  a  European  car  brand.  These  cars  will  be 

manufactured in a modern factory in Jaworzno, the company's headquarters will be 

located in Poland. New ventures such as an innovation center, a local supply chain and 

research and development units are to be created around the investment, creating a 
production and development hub. 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
–  The  holistic  approach  makes  the  project  an  effective  tool  of  industrial  policy  – 

conducted  by  the  state  and  constitutes  a  proactive  response  to  dynamic  market 

changes – said Tomasz Kędzierski. 

According to President Kędzierski, the EMP project in its new formula is consistent with 

the assumptions of the government's investment strategy "650 billion+", focused on 

the energy and technological transformation of the country . Thanks to the investment 

in Jaworzno, a local supply chain will be created, covering up to 80 percent of the value 

of car components. In addition, 20-30 thousand jobs will be created in the region, of 

which  about  1,000  in  the  R&D  area.  The  ability  of  Polish  companies  to  advance  in 

global value chains will also increase. 

EMP needs a partner for a new project 
–  The  flagship  project  of  Polish  electromobility  –  which  Izera  undoubtedly  was  – 

ultimately  ran  aground,  to  which  it  was  led  by  delays  caused  by  the  lack  of  stable 

financing. According to previous assumptions, the funds from the KPO enabling the 

implementation of the project were to reach the company at the beginning of 2023. 

The  failure  to  launch  them  by  the  end  of  2023  resulted  in  a  break  in  the  project 

continuity and the need to update almost all assumptions – said Kędzierski. 

The new EMP concept assumes the joint responsibility of a strategic partner. Instead 

of developing a car brand from scratch, EMP will act as a co-investor and coordinator 

of joint ventures . This is to minimize market risks. 

Talks are currently underway with potential EMP partners who could participate in the 

project.

What to replace Izera in Jaworzno? ElectroMobility Poland presented a plan for a 

hub 

2025-04-08 

ElectroMobility Poland (EMP), which was to build a factory for the Polish electric car 

Izera in Jaworzno (Silesia), announced that it intends to implement a "broader vision 

of a modern production and development hub". 

According to the information posted on the company's website, the main goal is to 

launch production of a European car brand in cooperation with a global partner and 

to create a market entity with headquarters located in Poland. 

EMP wrote that it will be a "modified vision of the project", in which the company, 

together with a global partner, will co-create a European car brand. The cars are to be 

manufactured  in  a  factory  in  Jaworzno,  and  the  company's  headquarters  is  to  be 

located  in  Poland.  Around  the  investment,  projects  such  as  an  innovation  center,  a 

local supply chain and research and development units would be created  - together 

creating a production and development hub. 

The  company  emphasized  that  its  venture  in  its new formula  is  consistent  with the 

concept of proactive industrial policy of the state, as well as with the assumptions of 

the  government's  investment  strategy  "650  billion+"  regarding  the  energy  and 

technological transformation of the country. 

According to EMP, the investment in Jaworzno will create a local supply chain covering 

up  to  80  percent  of  the  value  of  car  components,  and  20,000–30,000  jobs  will  be 

created  in  the  region,  of  which  about  1,000  in  the  R&D  area.  The  ability  of  Polish 

companies to advance in global value chains is also to increase. 

As the source of the failure of Izera EMP, she indicated delays caused by the lack of 

stable financing. According to previous assumptions, the funds from the KPO for the 

project were to reach the company in early 2023. Their lack by the end of 2023 resulted 
in a break in the project continuity and the need to update almost all assumptions.

In a text by EMP CEO Tomasz Kędzierski published on the EMP website, he assessed

for  only  one  year  –  from  the  signing  of  the  agreement  with  Geely  as  part  of  the 

technological partnership to the end of the preparatory phase in November 2023. 

EMP  assessed  that  its  new  concept  assumes  the  joint  responsibility  of  a  strategic 

partner. "Instead of developing a car brand from scratch on its own, EMP will act as a 

co-investor  and  coordinator  of  joint  ventures.  This  is  to minimize  market  risks," the 

company explained. 

It said it is currently preparing to submit an application for project financing under the 

KPO. Talks are also underway with potential EMP partners who could participate in the 

project. The company also signals that its project in its new formula may cover not only 

the automotive industry, but also energy and digital mobility services. 

The Izera project was endorsed by the previous government, establishing the company 

ElectroMobility  Poland  (EMP)  for  this  purpose.  At  the  end  of  2023,  the  company 

became the first owner of a 117-hectare plot of land in the Jaworzno Economic Area 

(JOG), which is a joint project of the city and the Katowice Special Economic Zone. 

The  JOG  area  is  approximately  300  ha  of  mainly  post-industrial  land in the  area  of 

Wojska  Polskiego  Street,  near  the  S1  expressway junction  and  a  railway siding. The 

electric car factory originally planned for this location was to occupy approximately 

120 ha. A sewage system is being built on the JOG area and the construction of the 

ordered road system is being designed.

is  responsible  for  preparing

the  local  government  of  Jaworzno  exchanged  land.  So  far,  the  area  has  been 

deforested, funds for its development have been secured, and related work has begun. 

At  the  end  of  November  last  year,  Minister  of  State  Assets  Jakub  Jaworowski 

announced that the EMP project, previously conducted in cooperation with, among 

others,  a  Chinese  technological  partner,  is not  being  continued  -  however,  talks  are 

ongoing with the Chinese side on an electromobility cluster.
