# Stellantis plans 29 new electric vehicles and a new platform for all drive types

**作者**：Florian Treiss　｜　**日期**：2026-05-21

---

Stellantis has unveiled its strategic plan for the next five years called 'FaSTLAne 2030.' The European-American automotive group intends to invest €60 billion during this period to accelerate its growth and increase profitability. The focus will be on the four brands, Fiat, Peugeot, Jeep, and Ram, as well as a new modular architecture called STLA One.

The new strategy is structured into six pillars:

**1) Brand portfolio reorganization:** Stellantis aims to maximise capital efficiency and avoid duplicate spending. The US brands Jeep and Ram, together with the European brands Fiat and Peugeot, will serve as the global powerhouses. Stellantis will direct 70 per cent of brand and product investments to these brands and to Pro One, the commercial vehicle business unit.

Chrysler, Dodge, Citroën, Opel, and Alfa Romeo will be managed as regional brands. DS Automobiles and Lancia will be positioned as more local brands. Stellantis plans to launch more than 60 new models and introduce 50 major model updates across all brands by 2030. The programme includes **29 battery-electric models, 15 PHEV/range-extended models, 24 hybrid vehicles, and 39 ICE/mild-hybrid vehicles**.

**2) STLA One platform:** Stellantis plans to invest €24 billion in global platforms and technologies over five years. The new STLA One platform will replace five existing platforms, covering segments B, C, and D and supporting all drive types. It will serve as the foundation for 30 models by 2035, reaching 2 million units annually.

Key features of STLA One:
- 800-volt architecture for ultra-fast charging
- Cell-to-Body concept integrating battery into vehicle structure
- Expansion of LFP battery use for affordability
- STLA Brain, SmartCockpit, AutoDrive, and Steer-by-Wire technologies (launching 2027)

**3) Partnerships:** Stellantis will further strengthen its Leapmotor partnership, with the Madrid plant soon starting EV production for Leapmotor to meet EU Made-in-Europe requirements. Technology partners include Applied Intuition, Qualcomm, Wayve, NVIDIA, Uber, Mistral AI, and CATL.

**4) Production optimization:** In Europe, production capacity will be reduced by more than 800,000 units by repurposing and leveraging partnerships, preserving manufacturing jobs while significantly increasing factory utilisation.

**5) Execution excellence:** Product development cycles targeted at 24 months (vs. 40 months today). Annual cost savings of €6 billion expected by 2028. Over 120 AI applications already in use.

**6) Regionalization:** Decision-making authority shifted from headquarters to regional teams to align more closely with customer preferences.

For Europe, an "E-Car project" will introduce a new generation of stylish and affordable urban electric vehicles from 2028.
