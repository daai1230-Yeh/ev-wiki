# Belgium approves Tesla FSD (Supervised) — fifth European country

**Author**: Florian Treiss | **Date**: Updated 2026-06-15

---

Tesla has now received approval in Belgium for its Full Self-Driving (Supervised) driver assistance system, making it the fifth European country to do so after the Netherlands, and three others including Denmark.

The system allows the vehicle to take control with hands-off driving, but requires the driver to continuously monitor the road and remain ready to intervene. It is not a fully autonomous system.

Belgium's Flemish Mobility Minister Annick De Ridder explained: "The Dutch approval was preceded by an intensive testing phase. We therefore did not have to start from scratch. Thanks to the smooth cooperation with our Dutch colleagues at the RDW, we had access to all their test data, and a shortened testing phase was sufficient to assess the differences in road infrastructure and traffic regulations between the Netherlands and Belgium."

**Background**: Tesla received its first EU approval from Dutch authority RDW in April 2026 after 1.6 million km of test drives in Europe, including demonstration drives with 13,000 Tesla customers and over 4,500 test scenarios on race tracks. Tesla uses cameras and AI exclusively (no additional sensors like radar/lidar) for its driver assistance systems. As pan-EU approval through the European Commission has not yet been secured, each country's authorization remains provisional.
