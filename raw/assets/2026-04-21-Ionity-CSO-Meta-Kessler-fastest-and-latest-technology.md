# 'We always aim to offer the fastest and latest technology' – Ionity CSO Meta Kessler

**Author**: Sebastian Schaal | **Date**: 2026-04-21

---

Faster and faster charging on highways, more charging parks in cities, along with new partnerships and continuous technological advancements — the charging industry is not standing still. But how does a company make decisions in such a dynamic environment? An interview with Meta Kessler, Director Strategy & Transformation at Ionity.

Meta Kessler has been Director Strategy & Transformation at Ionity since March 2024. In this role, she is responsible for the teams 'Market Research and Business Development,' 'PMO and Processes,' and 'Regulatory Affairs and Public Funding.'

**On 1,000 kW charging (HYC1000):** Ionity has integrated the first Alpitronic HYC1000 units into its network, selecting sites on high-traffic routes. The first site was opened on the Route du Soleil in France. Thanks to dynamic distribution of charging power, vehicles charge faster and charging points become available sooner. Vehicles that cannot charge as quickly do not occupy as much of the available power, as the distribution can be well managed.

**On urban expansion:** Ionity aims for roughly 30 per cent of its network to be located in cities by 2030. Based on a target of around 1,300 stations overall, this would translate into close to 490 urban sites. In North Rhine-Westphalia alone, three new urban stations have been opened in recent months. Ionity is also expanding through partnerships, including a cooperation with L'Osteria to deliver 15 sites in Germany and Austria, many of them in city locations.

Ionity's urban concept focuses on installing at least six charging points per site wherever possible — a small installation with just one charger and two points does not meet customer expectations. Customers need certainty that a space will be available when they arrive.

**On technology decisions:** The company always aims to offer the fastest and latest technology, integrating innovations where they deliver the most value to customers with the highest-charging vehicles on the busiest routes.
