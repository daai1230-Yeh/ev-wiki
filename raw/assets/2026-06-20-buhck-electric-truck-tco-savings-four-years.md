# Four years of electric trucks: how Buhck assesses the cost-effectiveness of its electric fleet

**Date**: 2026-06-18

---

German environmental services provider Buhck has analysed the economics of its electric truck operations after four years, calculating that an e-truck saves around **€125 per day** compared with a diesel vehicle.

## Key Findings

- Electric trucks generated **~15% lower costs** than comparable diesel vehicles in 2025, after all expenses including higher depreciation
- With average daily mileage of 250 km, savings amount to **~€30,000 per vehicle per year**
- Over 9-year service life, the higher purchase price is "definitely justified"
- Electric fleet covered **500,000+ km** in 2024, avoiding **~550 tonnes of CO₂e**

## Critical Success Factor: Depot Charging

The economics depend heavily on access to low-cost depot charging. Using public fast-charging stations would significantly increase operating costs and undermine the business case.

## Fleet & Investment

- Current fleet: 53 vehicles
- Target price for 2026 investments: **€250,000 per vehicle**
- Buhck is moving to turnkey solutions from single suppliers rather than sourcing components separately

## Market Dynamics

Managing Director Markus Horstkötter attributes falling vehicle prices to increasing pressure on manufacturers from stricter EU CO₂ targets. This has shifted the market toward buyers: "This is shifting the balance of power. It has become more of a buyer's market."

## Outlook

Buhck continues to expand its electric fleet, viewing the technology as economically viable for its waste management and environmental services operations. The four-year assessment confirms that scale and depot infrastructure are the key enablers of profitability.
