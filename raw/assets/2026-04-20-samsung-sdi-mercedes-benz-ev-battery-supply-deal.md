# Samsung SDI secures first EV battery supply agreement with Mercedes-Benz

**Date**: 2026-04-20

---

Samsung SDI has signed a multi-year agreement with Mercedes-Benz to supply NMC batteries for electric vehicles — the first such deal between the two companies, signed on April 20, 2026.

The cells will be high-nickel NMC battery cells, which offer extended driving range, long lifespan, and high power density. Mercedes-Benz plans to use the batteries in future compact and mid-size electric SUVs and coupe models as part of its next-generation EV strategy.

Samsung SDI's supply volumes are expected to reach tens of gigawatt-hours, with the total contract value set to exceed 10 trillion won (~€5.8 billion), though exact figures have not been disclosed. The two companies also agreed to jointly develop next-generation battery technology.

Samsung SDI is expected to supply battery cells for Mercedes-Benz's MMA (Mercedes Modular Architecture) platform starting in 2028.

The deal broadens Samsung SDI's European customer base, where it already supplies BMW and Stellantis, and represents Mercedes' first procurement partnership with the Korean battery giant. It comes as the automotive industry continues to diversify battery supply chains amid intensifying competition from Chinese manufacturers.
