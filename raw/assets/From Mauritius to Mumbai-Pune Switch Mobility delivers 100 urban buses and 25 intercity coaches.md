# From Mauritius to Mumbai–Pune: Switch Mobility delivers 100 urban buses and 25 intercity coaches

**Author**: Editorial Staff | **Date**: 20 April 2026

---

Mauritius is going ahead with the rollout of 100 electric buses funded by the Indian government, while Switch Mobility has deployed its first intercity electric coaches in the country.

Mauritius has indeed taken delivery of 90 additional electric buses for the National Transport Corporation (NTC), completing a grant initiative supported by the Government of India. The handover ceremony took place at Rose Belle Market Fair, where India's Minister of External Affairs, Subrahmanyam Jaishankar, formally presented the vehicles to Prime Minister Navinchandra Ramgoolam.

The delivery follows an initial batch of 10 buses introduced in December 2024, bringing the total fleet to 100 units. Manufactured by Switch Mobility, the buses are part of a broader partnership between India and Mauritius focused on sustainable public transport. The initiative is funded entirely through a $72 million grant from the Indian government and includes the construction of a new dedicated depot in the Côte d'Or area.

The buses will be deployed across the Mauritius national network to improve route coverage and public transport frequency. According to NTC, the rollout is expected to reduce the island's dependence on diesel-powered vehicles and cut carbon emissions in the transport sector.

Meanwhile, Switch Mobility has achieved another landmark with the delivery of 25 EiV 12 electric intercity coaches to Purple Mobility, a new intercity service operator running on the Mumbai–Pune Expressway. These 12-meter coaches are the first purpose-built electric vehicles to enter scheduled intercity service on one of India's busiest highway corridors.

The coaches are designed for intercity operations, featuring a range of over 250 kilometres on a single charge. They are equipped with high-capacity battery packs tailored for expressway speeds and sustained highway driving conditions. Purple Mobility plans to operate the fleet on a fixed schedule between Mumbai and Pune, with charging infrastructure installed at both terminal points.

Switch Mobility stated that the EiV 12 platform has been engineered specifically for Indian road and climate conditions. The company's Pune plant, which recently completed a production expansion, will continue to serve as the manufacturing base for both domestic and export orders.
