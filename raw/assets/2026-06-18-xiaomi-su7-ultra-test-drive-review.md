# I Drove One Of China's Fastest EVs And Found It Surprisingly Easy To Drive—Despite Its 1,500 Horsepower

**Author**: Kevin Williams | **Date**: Jun 16, 2026

---

The Xiaomi SU7 Ultra could be the easiest 1,526-horsepower car I've ever driven. And starting at $75,000, it's a bargain.

**Specs**: Price ~$75,000 (as-tested ~$88,000 with track package) | 1,526 hp / 1,305 lb-ft | 0-62 mph in 1.98 seconds | 93.7 kWh battery (CATL) | 391 miles CLTC range | Top speed 217 mph | Tri-motor AWD

The SU7 Ultra is a high-performance version of Xiaomi's blockbuster SU7 sedan with bigger wheels, retuned aerodynamics (rear wing, splitter, redesigned bumper), and carbon ceramic brake discs that Xiaomi claims are the largest of any production sports car. The three-motor setup (two HyperEngine V8s in back, one V6s in front) produces a combined 1,526 hp. The 93.7 kWh CATL battery supports a maximum discharge rate of 1,330 kW.

At Tianjin V1 Automobile World near Beijing, the car proved fantastically easy to drive at the limit. Throw it into a corner at speed and the car sorts itself out. Accelerate hard out of a turn, or brake too late, and it forgives minor mistakes. The traction control and suspension work together to guide you back without any rear-end scuttling or insecurity.

At $75,000 base, it rivals the Porsche Taycan Turbo (which costs $230,000 in China and is actually slower to 60 mph). This proves China absolutely can tune a car to be engaging — and Xiaomi's home team nailed it on the first attempt.

The catch: given anti-China sentiment in the U.S. and an impending ban on Chinese connected car tech, this car will almost certainly never reach American buyers.
