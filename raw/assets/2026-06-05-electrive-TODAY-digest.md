# electrive TODAY — 5 June 2026

**Source**: electrive global (newsletter@electrive.com)

---

## Exclusive
- **First drive in the MG4 EV Urban – Not exciting, but surprisingly good** — The new MG4 EV Urban prioritizes everyday usability and straightforward electric mobility over flashy design. A first drive through urban traffic, country roads and motorway toward Lake Starnberg tests whether the pragmatic concept works.

## News
- **Nissan explores contract manufacturing for Chery in Sunderland** — Negotiations underway for a potential agreement to build Chery passenger cars at Nissan's UK plant, after Nissan said it would cut its own production there to a single line.
- **Xpeng to produce fourth model at Magna Steyr in Graz** — Expanding collaboration with Magna Steyr beyond the G6, G9 and P7+; confirmed by CEO He Xiaopeng.
- **Autel Energy Europe highlights integrated charging solutions** — At the Amsterdam summit, presented updates across its MaxiCharger AC and DC portfolio, including all-in-one and distributed charging plus software/service capabilities.
- **Ireland launches €10m EV scrappage scheme** — Buyers can get up to €5,000 on top of existing grants when scrapping an ICE vehicle registered in 2013 or earlier.
- **KIA UK expands PV5 lineup** — Adds entry-level Cargo variant and seven-seat Passenger model for MY2027, plus tweaks to existing models.

## Number of the Day
- UK battery-electric car registrations rose 34.2% in May, lifting EV market share to 27.3% (SMMT) — the highest monthly EV share in the UK so far in 2026.

## Technology
- **Samsung SDI tipped as VW Unified Cell supplier** — VW reportedly adding Samsung SDI as another supplier for its Unified Cell battery format, with production prep in Hungary alongside PowerCo and Gotion.
- **Moment Energy opens second-life battery plant in Vancouver** — New facility repurposes used EV batteries into stationary storage, targeting 1 GWh annual capacity.
- **Wattlab standardises ship battery installation** — New WEstack system for inland vessels installs in one day, aiming to cut generator runtime by up to 90% and fuel consumption by 30-50%.
- **Fraunhofer ITWM advances digital battery production tools** — Developing simulation and inline measurement systems to reduce scrap rates and stabilise production.

## Feedback
- Yesterday's most-clicked link: VW starts series production of ID. Polo and Cupra Raval.

## Quotes of the Day
- Martin Sander: "You can buy a horse today... over time, more and more people realised that for actually getting from A to B, a vehicle is much better than a horse." — argued EV adoption will happen "organically" and called the ICE phase-out debate counterproductive.
- Steve Beattie (BYD): "I think we've almost got 98% of the network covered... We've got 142 sites now live," expecting ~165 UK locations by year-end.

## Fleets + Infrastructure
- **Zenobē and Vectalia agree €120m e-bus deal** — Framework agreement to accelerate electric bus deployment and depot electrification in Spain.
- **EZO wins £176m Midlands charging contract** — 15-year deal to deploy 250 rapid/ultra-rapid chargers across four Midlands councils for over two million residents.
- **Rivian hits 1,000 fast charging ports in US** — Adventure Network now exceeds 1,000 DC fast charging ports, with four new sites adding 44 stalls in the past month.
- **Mercedes F1 team adopts eActros 600** — Mercedes-AMG PETRONAS will run the electric truck at all nine European F1 races this year, the first team to do so for a full European campaign.
