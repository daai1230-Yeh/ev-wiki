# São Paulo introduces 265 BYD e-buses within a 500-units delivery

**Author**: Editorial staff | **Date**: 2026-06-26

---

São Paulo, Brazil has introduced **500 new battery-electric buses** and announced it will **no longer procure buses powered exclusively by diesel**.

## Fleet Status

- Municipal electric fleet now totals **1,759 buses** (including 189 trolleybuses)
- Largest electric bus fleet in Brazil
- Fleet growth: 460 → 1,095 buses during 2025; 1,271 by Feb 2026; 1,759 after this delivery

## BYD Delivery (265 of 500 buses)

- BYD supplied **265 buses** — the largest single heavy-duty EV bus delivery in Brazil
- Models: **D9W** (standard) and **BC22** articulated (Blade Battery)
- BYD now has ~**550 e-buses in São Paulo**, ~**700 in Brazil total**
- Manufactured locally: chassis at **Campinas**, batteries at **Manaus**
- Each bus avoids **125–204 tonnes CO₂/year**; 265 buses ≈ planting 378,000 trees/yr

## Policy Context

- SPTrans **banned new diesel bus procurement in 2022** — this delivery is the first consequence
- Brazil committed at COP30 (Nov 2025) to **100% ZEV medium/heavy vehicle sales by 2040** (30% by 2030)
- **€80M Brazil E-Bus Credit Enhancement Fund** established to support further deployments
- Latin America's electric bus fleet surpassed 9,000 vehicles in 2025, likely now 10,000+
