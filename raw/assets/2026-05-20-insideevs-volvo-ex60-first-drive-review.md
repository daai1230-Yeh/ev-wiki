# I Drove The 2027 Volvo EX60 And Think It's The Best Volvo Ever. All Because It's Electric.

**Author**: Mack Hogan | **Date**: 2026-05-20

---

After troubled EV launches with the EX30 (killed by tariffs) and EX90 (delayed by software/quality issues), Volvo's EV future hinges on the EX60. Over five hours of back-country and city driving around Barcelona, the verdict is in: it's exceptionally smooth, nearly silent, and utterly charming — the most relaxing car on the road.

## Interior

The cabin is minimalist, high-quality, and airy. Volvo struck a good balance on physical controls: no hard buttons for climate but temperature is always one tap away on screen; physical buttons for track skip, volume, and power. Negative: software-controlled air vents (a pointless trend). A standout feature: the under-trunk storage with a removable liner bucket doubles as a trash bin.

The **Bowers & Wilkins 28-speaker Dolby Atmos stereo** (standard on Ultra trims) is described as "one of the greatest in-car experiences" of the reviewer's 10-year career. Headrest-mounted speakers can isolate audio to individual passengers — perfect for sleeping passengers.

## Software & Tech

Runs Google Android Automotive with Google Built-In. Highlights the new **conversational Google Gemini navigation**: can handle complex requests like "find a 350 kW charging stop along my route near a park to walk my dog" almost instantly. Tech is polished but unobtrusive — unlike some rivals, the EX60 doesn't constantly ping alerts or push comfort features at you.

## Driving Experience

The EX60 is compliant, precise, and quiet. The reviewer's key takeaway: "Everything that is great about this car is great because it's electric." The silent drivetrain makes the stereo hit harder, the seats feel more comfortable, and the cabin feel more refined. Reliability expected to be higher due to mechanical simplicity.

## Verdict

"It may not be the flashiest, or the fastest, but it is an absolutely serene place to spend time. That's exactly what a Volvo is supposed to be." In the luxury midsize crossover segment, price parity with ICE equivalents has arrived — the EX60 is cheaper than a plug-in hybrid XC60 AWD and simply a better car. Competes against Mercedes GLC EV, BMW iX3, and Rivian R2.
