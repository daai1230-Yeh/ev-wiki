# The Mercedes-Benz C-Class EV Is Not What I Expected

**Author**: Andrei Nedelea | **Date**: 2026-04-20

---

Mercedes brands its new electric sedan as being "the sportiest C-Class to date," but it's also really luxurious and packed with tech.

Mercedes wants its new line of EVs to look as conventional and as close to its gas cars as possible. The new electric C-Class is the third Mercedes in this new line of EVs, after the new CLA and GLC, and the manufacturer promises that it "elevates the segment to an entirely new level."

The front design follows the direction set by the GLC EV, with a retro-homage grille reinterpreted for the LED era, featuring hundreds of light-up points and an illuminated surround. The most daring part is the rear, which isn't a traditional sedan but more of a fastback. Mercedes calls this an "athletic GT rear design" and says the shape plays an active role in giving the car its impressive drag coefficient of 0.22—lower than the combustion C-Class.

Seeing the car in person, it looks quite a bit taller than its sleek combustion counterpart. It rides on a 116.6-inch wheelbase, 3.8 inches longer than the gas C-Class. Mercedes says it gives the EV 0.5 inches of additional front legroom; the rear, however, felt pretty cramped for a six-foot frame.

The driving position is fantastic—the seat goes nice and low, and you can bring the steering wheel closer. Like the GLC, the C-Class EV's dashboard is all-screen, with an optional 39.1-inch display running from pillar to pillar. The infotainment runs the latest MB.OS system with native Google Maps and multi-agent AI integration (ChatGPT or Google Gemini depending on query type). Material quality was notably better than in the GLC EV, with soft-touch plastic further down the door panels and center console.

**Platform and powertrain:** Built on the same MB.EA 800-volt platform as the GLC EV. The C400 4Matic Electric produces 482 hp from its dual-motor setup, accelerating 0–62 mph in 3.9 seconds. The rear drive unit has a two-speed transmission; the front motor can mechanically decouple for efficiency. Rear-wheel steering (up to 4.5 degrees opposite direction) reduces turning circle radius by almost 3 feet to 36.7 feet.

**Battery and charging:** Shares the 94.3 kWh battery pack with the GLC SUV, but with a lower, sleeker body achieves a claimed maximum WLTP range of 473 miles (762 km)—29 miles more than the GLC SUV. Peak charging power is rated at 330 kW (the GLC has been observed at 353 kW). A 10-minute charge can add up to 202 miles (325 km). Regenerative braking can recover up to 300 kW using the motors rather than friction brakes.

**Air suspension** with Car-to-X communication gathers road condition data from other Mercedes models (potholes, etc.) and adjusts suspension settings proactively.

**Trunk:** 16.6 cu-ft (up from 16.1 in the combustion version), plus a small frunk (~4.5 cu-ft estimated).

Mercedes boss Ola Källenius said: "The all-new electric C-Class is redefining the mid-size segment for electric vehicles. Customers will get exactly what they expect from this new version of the beloved model: the perfect blend of performance, comfort, dynamics, and intelligence."

On first impressions, the C-Class EV ticks many important boxes; a full drive evaluation is pending.
