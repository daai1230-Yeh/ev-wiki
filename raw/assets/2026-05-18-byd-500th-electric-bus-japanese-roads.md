# BYD puts 500th electric bus on Japanese roads

**Author**: Sagar Parikh | **Date**: 2026-05-13

---

BYD has reached a milestone of 500 electric buses operating on Japanese roads, following the delivery of six K8 electric buses to Nankai Rinkan Bus in Wakayama Prefecture. The buses will serve the Koyasan Inland Line connecting Koyasan Station and Okunoin-mae.

## BYD K8 Specs (Japan)

- **Dimensions**: 10.5 m length × 2.5 m width × 3.3 m height
- **Wheelbase**: 5.3 m
- **Motor**: 2 × 100 kW = 200 kW combined
- **Top speed**: 70 km/h
- **Grade climbing**: up to 15%
- **Battery**: 314 kWh LFP
- **Range**: 240 km
- **Charging**: up to 90 kW (~3.5 hours)
- **Suspension**: Air suspension on both axles
- **Turning radius**: 8.3 m
- **Capacity (urban version)**: 80 people (22 seated, 4 folding, 53 standing + driver)
- **Capacity (suburban version)**: 76 people (22 seated, 8 folding, 45 standing + driver)
- Both versions include 2 wheelchair spaces

## Context

BYD started selling electric buses in Japan in 2015, with its first deployment in Kyoto. This Wakayama deployment is the first BYD electric bus service in the prefecture. The operator specifically cited the bus's range and cold-weather performance (Koyasan temperatures can drop to -15°C in winter) as key selection criteria.
