# Tesla Owners Are Using Doll Heads To Fool Full Self-Driving

**Author**: Rob Stumpf | **Date**: Jun 17, 2026

---

Some Tesla owners have found a new way to fool their cars into thinking that their eyes are on the road: using a doll head affixed in front of the cabin-facing mirror camera.

Chinese eCommerce sites have started selling "travel companions" marketed specifically for Tesla owners — ping pong ball-sized doll heads, some resembling celebrities like Dwayne "The Rock" Johnson. The idea is to stick them directly in front of the car's cabin-facing camera to trick Tesla's Autopilot and Full Self-Driving into thinking the driver is attentive.

Both Autopilot and FSD are Level 2 driver assistance systems that require drivers to pay attention to the road. Tesla monitors driver attentiveness by watching the position of the driver's head and eyes — meaning a carefully-positioned doll's head is enough to trick the system.

One driver told Wired he used the doll head method for 250 miles of a 400-mile trip, allowing him to go 30 minutes without interruption while eating sunflower seeds with one hand and filming with the other.

This is part of a years-long game of cat and mouse. Earlier methods included photos to trick the camera, weights on the steering wheel when Tesla used torque-based monitoring, and now lenticular prints and small screens. Each patch leads determined drivers to find new workarounds.

Driver monitoring requires careful tuning: too noisy and people stop using the feature; too lax and it's easily defeated. Tesla's ultimate goal is to remove the need for driver monitoring altogether by having cars drive themselves — but that reality is still some way off.
