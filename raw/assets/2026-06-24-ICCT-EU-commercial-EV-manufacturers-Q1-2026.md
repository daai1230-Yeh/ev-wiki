# ICCT rankings reveal EU's bestselling commercial EV manufacturers

**Author**: Cora Werwitzke | **Date**: June 23, 2026

---

The International Council on Clean Transportation has evaluated which commercial vehicle manufacturers led the European market in electric drivetrains during Q1 2026, with Mercedes-Benz Trucks coming out on top. Plus: for the first time, a Chinese player led the e-bus segment.

The ICCT recorded 6,355 zero-emission commercial vehicles sold in the EU in Q1 2026, including roughly 1,600 heavy trucks, 2,000 light and medium-duty trucks, and 2,700 buses. Notably, the number of heavy trucks nearly doubled compared to Q1 2025.

## Heavy Trucks (12t+)

In the heavy battery-electric truck segment (12 tonnes and above), Mercedes-Benz Trucks maintained its leading position in Q1 with a market share of just over 30 per cent, selling 530 units (3.9% ZE share, up from 0.9% in Q1 2025). MAN also performed strongly, doubling its market share to 411 units (3.6%), up from 130 units in Q1 2025. DAF started series production of its electric XD and XF series in September 2025, selling nearly 200 units in Q1 2026. Iveco sold just six heavy battery-electric trucks. Former market leader Volvo Trucks now ranks fourth with just over 10 per cent share.

Key markets: Germany remains the largest (536 trucks, 3.5% share), but the Netherlands (338 vehicles, 10.3%), Sweden (125, 9.7%), Denmark (100, 10.0%), and Austria (138, 8.0%) show more dynamic growth.

## Medium Trucks and Vans (3.5–12t)

Mercedes, Iveco, and Ford comprised roughly 70% of the total ZE medium truck and van market. The Nordic countries are among the largest buyers — Denmark, Sweden, and the Netherlands each recorded an electric share of just under or over 60 per cent of all registrations.

## Electric Buses

Zero-emission city bus sales saw a slowdown at end-2025 but rebounded slightly to 55% in Q1 2026. Notable surges: Portugal (50% electric share, up from 3% in Q1 2025), Italy (34%, up from 13%), and Lithuania (59%, up from 40%).

Chinese manufacturers made significant gains in Q1 2026. **Yutong led in terms of ZE sales share — the first time a Chinese brand has done so** — with BYD and King Long also placing in the top eight. Yutong narrowly leads Solaris with a 15% market share, with MAN and Daimler Buses following at around 10%.

Overall market shares: registrations of electric heavy trucks accounted for 2.3% market share across all drive types in Q1 2026. In the light/medium-duty segment, ZEVs achieved 19.7% market share. Electric bus sales stabilised at around 24.1%.
