# Sustainable Bus Weekly — June 9th, 2026

**Source**: Sustainable Bus Weekly (info@sustainable-bus.com)

---

## Top News
- The finalists for the Sustainable Bus Award 2027 have been announced, with twelve vehicles shortlisted across the Urban, Intercity and Coach categories.

## Main News
- Electric bus operating costs are falling, but purchase prices remain the main barrier to achieving full cost parity with diesel fleets, according to HEAG mobilo Managing Director Arne Rath.
- Former Aston Martin CEO Andy Palmer's PETL acquires bus repowering specialist Kleandrive from administration.

## Highlights
- Solaris is positioning North America as a strategic growth market within CAF Group's urban mobility activities, combining CAF's established US rail presence with Solaris' zero-emission bus portfolio. Interview with Félix Fernández, CEO of CAF USA and President of Solaris North America.
- Bus thermal management system supplier SONGZ released its annual business data at Bus2Bus Berlin 2026: total bus HVAC deliveries of 40,151 units in 2025, of which electric heat pump systems reached 30,394 units (75% of full-year sales).
- Yutong presented its latest battery-electric buses and EV technologies at Next Mobility Exhibition 2026 in Milan, focusing on long-life components, integrated electric architecture and fleet digitalization; showcased four battery-electric vehicles.

## Photo Story
- Karsan Autonomous e-JEST enters passenger service in Atlanta ahead of FIFA World Cup 2026.

## There's More
- Solaris, VDL, Hess and Beulas call on EU to support European bus manufacturing.
- First Bus deploys first repowered electric buses in Bath.
- Zenobē and Vectalia sign €120 million leasing framework for electric buses in Spain.
- Solaris cuts operational emissions 70% since 2019 as zero-emission vehicles reach 86% of deliveries.
- UITP: European preference should not compromise decarbonisation targets and timelines, increase costs or restrict competition.
- Bus Simulator 27 adds intercity routes and licensed coaches ahead of September release.
- Sydney's Brookvale depot deploys 13 Kempower pantograph chargers for 229-bus electrification project.

## Photo Story (2)
- Type-approved new-generation Irizar hydrogen coach to begin passenger service with Alsa this summer.
