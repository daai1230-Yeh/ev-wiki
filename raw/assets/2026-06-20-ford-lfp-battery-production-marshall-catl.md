# Ford to produce first LFP cells this year – thanks to CATL

**Date**: 2026-06-18

---

Ford is on schedule to begin series production of LFP battery cells this year at its BlueOval Battery Park Michigan plant in Marshall. The technology license comes from CATL.

## Production Status

Ford's 'D-Sample' cells are already in production using materials ready for mass manufacturing and are undergoing final tests and validations. The goal is to integrate these prismatic LFP batteries into Ford's new, affordable electric pickup based on the Universal EV Platform, set to launch in 2027. Ford expects the plant to begin producing market-ready battery cells before the end of 2026.

## CATL Partnership

Ford licensed LFP technology from CATL, giving it greater control than a joint-venture structure. The plant is fully Ford-owned. However, CATL plays a crucial role in the ramp-up: CATL employees have assisted in Marshall, providing training and manufacturing expertise.

## Plant Details

- **Location**: Marshall, Michigan (BlueOval Battery Park Michigan)
- **Ownership**: Fully Ford-owned (not a JV)
- **Target employment**: 800 workers by end of 2026, growing to 1,700
- **Cell type**: Prismatic LFP
- **Target vehicle**: Affordable electric pickup on Universal EV Platform (2027 launch)

## Context

This represents Ford's move toward greater vertical integration in battery production, reducing dependence on external cell suppliers while still relying on CATL's established manufacturing know-how for the critical ramp-up phase.
