# MAN and Dettendorfer launch the 'Green Brenner Initiative'

**Author**: Cora Werwitzke | **Date**: 2026-05-30

---

Manufacturer MAN, Dettendorfer Energy GmbH, and other partners are founding the 'Green Brenner Initiative.' The initiators aim to develop the Brenner Corridor as a model region for emission-free logistics. The project builds on initial practical experiences from last summer.

MAN and Dettendorfer Energie are leading this initiative, with the Green Brenner Initiative also supported by additional partners from the business, energy, and infrastructure sectors. The shared vision: to accelerate the adoption of battery-electric trucks on the Brenner route between Austria and Italy. In alpine regions, zero-emission transport is 'not only ecologically sound, but also economically attractive.'

"The Brenner is a stress test for European freight transport – and at the same time concrete evidence that electric mobility in heavy goods transport can already make a significant contribution to alleviating the traffic problem today. Until the completion of the Brenner Base Tunnel, e-trucks can make an important contribution to easing the burden," said Frederik Zohm, Executive Board Member for Research and Development at MAN Truck & Bus.

The initiators aim to establish the Brenner corridor as a model region for zero-emission logistics — 'explicitly not as competition, but as a complement to rail.' Until the completion of the tunnel, the concept is intended to serve as a fast transport alternative. Scaling up the initiative depends in particular on the expansion of charging infrastructure and grid capacities along the European transit corridor.

MAN is aware of this from a four-week field trial conducted last year with the large haulage company Dettendorfer. The partners demonstrated that an electric truck on the Brenner route can save significant amounts of CO₂ and, due to high recuperation and lower toll costs, can be particularly economical.

Dettendorfer has partnered with the energy provider Energie Südbayern to establish the joint venture Dettendorfer Energy, which coordinates charging hub ambitions. Two truck charging hubs are now operational: one at the Inntal motorway service area in Raubling and a second in Langenbruck, south of Ingolstadt.

Georg Dettendorfer, Managing Director, is convinced that hauliers operating electric trucks on the Brenner Pass can achieve significant cost savings from an annual mileage of around 110,000 kilometres. "Energy costs for electric trucks are around 40 per cent lower compared to current diesel prices." On the Brenner route, the e-truck can recover up to around 40 per cent of energy through recuperation on downhill stretches.

The stakeholders also point to toll savings of around 80 per cent. The total cost of ownership for a battery-electric truck over three years should be around 20 per cent lower than a comparable diesel truck. MAN further emphasises lower brake wear and reduced noise emissions: electric trucks produce 12.6 dB less noise than diesel counterparts during acceleration. MAN states that the daily use of 300 electric trucks would correspond to an annual CO₂ saving of around 28,000 tonnes.
