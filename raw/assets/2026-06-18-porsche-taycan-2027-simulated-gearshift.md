# Porsche's new Taycan model year gets simulated gearshift – for an additional charge

**Author**: Cora Werwitzke | **Date**: 17.06.2026

---

Porsche has unveiled the 2027 Taycan, with the new optional 'E-Shift' mode among the most notable updates. The refreshed electric sports car gains a virtual gearbox, though customers must pay extra for the feature. New tyres are claimed to increase range by up to 20 kilometres. The updated Taycan is available to order now.

Porsche has introduced a series of updates for the new Taycan model year. Single-motor entry-level variants are now available with low rolling-resistance summer tyres, increasing the vehicle's range by up to 20 kilometres. Combined with the Performance Battery Plus, the Taycan can now achieve up to 700 kilometres of WLTP range for the first time.

The infotainment system has also been refined, with the Porsche Communication Management (PCM) system now offering up to five times the computing power of its predecessor. Porsche has also enhanced voice control and wireless smartphone charging (now up to 25 watts).

The update does not alter the model structure or pricing. The Taycan remains available as a coupé saloon, Sport, or Cross Turismo in 18 variants, with power outputs ranging from 300 to 760 kW and prices ranging between €102,600 and €241,100.

One new feature—available for an extra €1,000—has attracted attention: the Taycan now includes a virtual gearbox that simulates gear shifts with noticeable jerking sensations. This nod to the petrol era is paired with a reinterpreted sports sound and a virtual rev counter.

The so-called E-Shift mode is operated via two shift paddles on the steering wheel and offers eight driving modes, complete with the familiar traction interruption of a combustion engine. It is included as standard in the top-of-the-range Turbo GT model, while in all other versions it is available as an optional extra for around €1,000.
