# I Drove The 2027 Volvo EX60 And Think It's The Best Volvo Ever. All Because It's Electric.

**Author**: Mack Hogan | **Date**: 2026-05-20

---

This is a first-drive review of the 2027 Volvo EX60, the mid-size electric crossover Volvo is counting on after the troubled EX30 (killed by tariffs and pricing) and the buggy EX90 launch. After about five hours driving around Barcelona, the reviewer concludes the EX60 is the best Volvo yet — smooth, near-silent and genuinely polished — and a strong contender against the Mercedes GLC EV, BMW iX3 and Rivian R2.

Key details captured:

- **Lineup & pricing**: Base rear-wheel-drive P6 Plus starts at $59,795; the AWD P10 Plus is $62,145. Volvo notes that undercuts the equivalent plug-in hybrid XC60 (~$62,545 with standard AWD), though a base mild-hybrid XC60 is still roughly $8,000 cheaper.
- **Specs**: P6 — 369 hp, 354 lb-ft, 0–60 in 5.7s, up to 307 miles range from an 83 kWh pack (80 usable). P10 — 503 hp, 0–60 in 4.4s, 322 miles. A range-topping AWD P12 arrives later in 2026 with a 117 kWh pack (112 usable), ~400 miles EPA, 670 hp and 0–60 in 3.8s. All trims charge 10–80% in about 16 minutes (DC up to 320 kW on P6, 370 kW on P10).
- **Interior**: Praised for ditching the EX90's disliked window-switch design (now four individual switches), plush and well-built materials, and a smartly divided under-trunk storage area. Criticized for touchscreen-controlled air vents.
- **Software**: New Volvo UI on Google Android Automotive with Google Built-In, plus conversational navigation via Google Gemini that handles natural-language queries (e.g. finding a fast charger near a dog park). The reviewer notes the same Gemini tech is also coming to Mercedes, GM, other Volvo/Polestar models and Android Auto.
- **Standout feature**: The optional 28-speaker Bowers & Wilkins Dolby Atmos stereo (standard on Ultra trims), including headrest speakers that can isolate audio to individual seats.
- **Verdict**: The reviewer argues that in the luxury midsize segment, EVs like the EX60 and iX3 have reached the point of being simply better cars than their gas counterparts. He stops short of calling it best-in-class (hasn't yet driven the iX3 or R2) but says it delivers exactly the serene experience a Volvo buyer wants.
