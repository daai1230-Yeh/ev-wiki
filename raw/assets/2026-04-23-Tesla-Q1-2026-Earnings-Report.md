# Tesla (TSLA) Q1 2026 Earnings Report

**來源**: CNBC | **日期**: 2026-04-22

---

Tesla reported first-quarter 2026 earnings that beat analysts' estimates even as revenue came in weaker than expected. Shares initially rose about 4% in extended trading but gave up their gains after the company said spending this year will be $5 billion above prior guidance.

## Key Numbers vs. Estimates

| Metric | Actual | Estimate |
|--------|--------|----------|
| EPS (adjusted) | **$0.41** | $0.37 |
| Revenue | **$22.39B** | $22.64B |
| Net Income | **$477M** ($0.13/share) | — |
| Automotive Gross Margin (ex-credits) | **19.2%** | — |

## Highlights

**Revenue**: Increased 16% YoY from $19.3 billion. Automotive revenue rose 16% to $16.2 billion.

**Margins**: Automotive gross margins (excluding regulatory credits) came in at 19.2%, higher than any quarter last year. Margins were helped by higher average selling prices and lower material costs. Profits were also boosted by one-time benefits related to tariffs and automotive warranties.

**Deliveries**: 358,023 vehicles in Q1 2026, up ~6% YoY but lower than the prior quarter.

**Energy Segment**: Revenue of $2.41 billion, down 12% from $2.73 billion in Q1 2025.

**Capex**: Jumped 67% to $2.49 billion. CFO Vaibhav Taneja said capex will top $25 billion for full-year 2026, up from prior guidance of $20 billion (and up from $8.6 billion in 2025).

## Strategic Updates

- Tesla confirmed plans to make "more affordable trims" of its Model Y SUV and Model 3 sedans.
- Tesla will end production of Model S and X, using the Fremont factory to build **Optimus humanoid robots**. Preparations for the "first large-scale Optimus factory" will begin in Q2, targeting 1 million robots/year.
- Older Model 3/Y vehicles with **Hardware 3** computers will not support future unsupervised FSD systems. Tesla plans a discounted trade-in program for hardware upgrades.
- Musk plans to unveil the next-gen Optimus robot closer to production, estimated for "late July–August."

## Context

Tesla's stock has dropped 14% YTD, underperforming all megacap peers. The company faces ongoing headwinds from Chinese competitors (BYD, Xiaomi) offering higher-tech, lower-cost models, as well as consumer backlash linked to CEO Elon Musk's political activities. The core automotive business continues to struggle against global competition.

Tesla is the first of tech's trillion-dollar companies to report Q1 2026 results. Alphabet, Amazon, Meta, and Microsoft are scheduled to report the following Wednesday.
