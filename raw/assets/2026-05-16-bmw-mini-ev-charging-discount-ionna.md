# BMW And Mini EV Drivers Get A Sweet Discount At Ionna

**Author**: Iulian Dnistran | **Date**: May 15, 2026

---

One of the best DC fast charging networks in the U.S. is slashing prices for BMW and Mini EV drivers until September 30.

BMW and Mini EV drivers in the United States can now get a 20% discount when charging at Ionna stations. BMW is a founding partner of Ionna, which is rapidly expanding its footprint in the U.S.

Owners who initiate charging with Plug & Charge or the My BMW app will get a 20% discount applied directly to the final bill — without needing additional cards, subscriptions, or manual activation. Note: the discount will not apply if drivers use a debit or credit card directly at the stall.

Ionna charges between $0.33–$0.39/kWh for everyone, so the 20% discount brings prices down to $0.27–$0.32/kWh for BMW and Mini EV owners. This is Ionna's second special pricing campaign, after a 10% discount offered to GM EV drivers earlier this year.

"BMW firmly believes in an electric future, and it's no secret that reliable high-speed charging infrastructure remains essential to the continued growth of the electric vehicle market," said Shaun Bugbee, Executive Vice President, BMW of North America.

Ionna also announced it will charge just $0.20/kWh for the first week at every new location it opens this year. This year alone, Ionna turned on 212 new charging bays, bringing the total to nearly 1,000 bays across 100 stations. Ionna uses 400 kW DC fast chargers exclusively.

By end of decade, Ionna plans to have 30,000 DC fast charging bays capable of delivering up to 400 kW via CCS or NACS. Currently it has 4,700 bays contracted with roughly 1,500 already in construction. Ionna was founded in 2024 by several automakers and energized its first stalls in December 2024.
