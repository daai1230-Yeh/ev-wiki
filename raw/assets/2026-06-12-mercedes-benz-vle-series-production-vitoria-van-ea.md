# Mercedes-Benz launches series production of the VLE

**Author**: Florian Treiss | **Date**: June 12, 2026

---

Mercedes-Benz has started series production of the all-electric VLE, the first model based on the company's new VAN.EA electric vehicle platform. The vehicle is now rolling off the production line at Mercedes-Benz's van plant in Vitoria, Spain.

## Key Points

- VLE is the first model on the new **VAN.EA platform** (800-volt architecture, up to 300 kW charging)
- Series production started **June 12, 2026** at the Vitoria plant (Basque Country, Spain)
- Starting price: **€82,260** (more affordable ~€70,000 versions expected later)
- Range: ~**700 km WLTP** total; 355 km recovered in a 15-minute charge stop
- Theoretically capable of 1,000 km with a single 15-minute stop

## Platform & Technology

The VAN.EA platform features:
- 800-volt architecture
- Up to 300 kW charging capacity
- 15-minute charge = ~355 km WLTP range

## Plant Background

The Vitoria plant (opened 1954, part of Mercedes-Benz since 1981) employs ~5,000 people. It underwent extensive modernisation for VLE production, including new body-shop, paint and assembly facilities plus upgraded logistics and IT. The plant now runs "highly flexible, multi-powertrain production on one line." Pre-series production began in September 2025.

The VLE succeeds the EQV. Blind orders (without prior test drives) have been available since April 2026.

The start of production was attended by Thomas Klein (Head of Mercedes-Benz Vans), Ola Källenius (CEO, Mercedes-Benz Group), Spain's Minister for Industry and Tourism Jordi Hereu, and Imanol Pradales (Acting President of the Basque Country).
