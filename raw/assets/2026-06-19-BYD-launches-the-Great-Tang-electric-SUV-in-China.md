# BYD launches the Great Tang electric SUV in China

**Author**: Cora Werwitzke | **Date**: 18.06.2026

---

BYD is launching its seven-seat eSUV, the Great Tang, in China. The 5.26-metre-long electric model achieves a range of up to 950 km under the Chinese CLTC standard and costs the equivalent of €30,800–€39,800.

BYD has celebrated the market launch of the Great Tang, a new seven-seater flagship SUV in the D-segment. It ranks as the premium model in the 'Dynasty' series and is available in four variants (239,900–309,900 yuan). BYD opened reservations at Auto China in April and has since recorded 150,000 pre-orders.

**Key specs:**
- Dimensions: 5.26 x 2.0 x 1.79 m, wheelbase 3.13 m
- Configuration: 7-seat (2+2+3)
- Battery: 105.7 or 130.1 kWh, second-gen LFP Blade Battery
- Voltage: 1,000V high-voltage architecture
- Charging: 10–97% in 9 minutes ('Flash-Charging')
- Motor output: 300–370 kW (single); up to 585 kW (AWD)
- Top speed: 250 km/h
- Range: 800–950 km CLTC (entry RWD to long-range)

Additional features include dual-chamber air suspension with 100 mm adjustment, rear-axle steering (7-degree max, 5.2 m turning circle), 'Crab Walk' capability, and BYD's 'God's Eye 5.0' ADAS with roof-mounted LiDAR as standard.

The Great Han flagship saloon will follow with a slight delay.
