# UK tax authority to appeal ruling on EV charging VAT

**Author**: Carla Westerheide | **Date**: 23.04.2026 - 09:31

---

The UK's tax authority has confirmed it will appeal a landmark ruling that could reduce VAT on public electric vehicle charging from 20% to 5%. If the appeal fails, charge point operators are expected to file claims for overpaid VAT, potentially covering several years.

This comes after the First-tier Tribunal ruled in favour of non-profit community charging provider Charge My Street in February, which argued that electricity supplied via public charge points should qualify for the reduced domestic rate. Because of HMRC's appeal, the ruling will not take effect unless upheld by a higher court.

The case centres on the interpretation of existing VAT legislation in the UK. The legislation defines electricity as 'always for domestic use' if consumption does not exceed 1,000 kWh per month at a single supply point — a threshold that most individual EV chargers would fall below.
