# 14 new electric buses hit the road in Northumberland

**Author**: Ciaran Daly | **Date**: 2026-06-15

---

14 new BYD BD11 battery-electric buses are set to enter service in Northumberland, UK. Funded in partnership with the North East Combined Authority, the buses will be deployed from Arriva's depot in Blyth on the 43, 44, and 45 routes between Morpeth, Dinnington, and Newcastle-upon-Tyne.

The £6.1 million investment by Arriva is part of a wider £37 million investment in the North East region, replacing one-third of its fleet with new vehicles. These are the first provincial-spec BYD BD11 buses for a UK customer outside London since the model launched in 2024.

Technical specs of the 10.9-metre BYD BD11: 532 kWh blade LFP battery (cell-to-pack or cell-to-body), up to 500 kW pantograph charging (full charge in ~2 hours), two 150 kW wheel-hub motors (600 Nm total), SiC technology. The bus features Northumbria Electrics branding alongside standard Arriva livery.

North East Mayor Kim McGuinness stated she wants every bus in the region to transition to zero-emission technology within the next decade. BYD is also manufacturing 191 electric buses for Arriva in London and 39 more for outside London.
