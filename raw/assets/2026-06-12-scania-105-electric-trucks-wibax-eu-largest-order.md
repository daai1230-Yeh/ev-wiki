# Scania lands 105-electric-truck order from Wibax

**Author**: Carla Westerheide | **Date**: June 12, 2026

---

Scania has secured an order for 105 battery-electric trucks from Swedish logistics specialist Wibax — described as the **EU's largest electric truck deal for bulk transport** to date.

## Key Points

- **105 battery-electric trucks** ordered by Wibax from Scania
- EU's largest electric truck order for bulk transport to date
- Wibax transports bio-oils and liquid chemicals across Sweden and Finland
- Order includes charging solutions, solar energy generation, and fleet optimisation services
- Will make Wibax one of the **largest operators of electric heavy-duty vehicles in the Nordic region**

## Background

This builds on a 2022 pilot project where Wibax operated a three-axle Scania tractor-trailer combination on an 80 km route between Piteå and Skellefteå in northern Sweden. The pilot vehicle used nine battery packs (~300 kWh capacity) and weighed ~10.8 tonnes (1.5 tonnes more than a comparable diesel truck).

Wibax identified heavy-duty routes with short, repetitive journeys as most suitable for electrification — consistent with the pilot experience.

## Quotes

> "We need to work with a supplier with whom we can work with strategically and for the long-term, and that's Scania. We want to electrify our fleet quickly." — Jonas Wiklund, CEO of Wibax Group

> "We are delighted to sign this landmark agreement with Wibax, who share Scania's goals of decarbonising transport in combination with enhanced operational efficiency." — Tobias Ejderhamn, Head of Global Sales, e-truck solutions at Scania

## Package Includes

- 105 battery-electric Scania trucks
- Charging infrastructure solutions
- Solar energy generation
- New business models to reduce total operating costs
- Fleet optimisation services for large-scale electrification
