# Microlino AG battles for equal treatment of light electric vehicles

**Author**: Cora Werwitzke | **Date**: 2026-06-15

---

Microlino AG has filed a complaint with the Swiss Federal Administrative Court against the Swiss Federal Office of Energy, arguing that the exclusion of light electric vehicles (L6e/L7e class) from CO₂ fleet regulations constitutes systematic discrimination.

Switzerland's Federal Office of Energy does not classify light EVs like the Microlino as full-fledged cars, meaning they are excluded from CO₂ fleet regulations and receive no direct or indirect promotion. As a result, larger EVs (Dacia Spring, Fiat 500e, Renault Twingo E-Tech, Tesla) can be offered at more attractive prices through fleet regulation incentives, while the Microlino cannot.

Microlino AG's demand: "The contested ruling should be overturned, and electric vehicles of the Microlino type should be permitted to form emissions communities and be included in the calculation of the fleet average." The company argues the Microlino is comparable to other BEV passenger cars in function and purpose, and has potential to contribute significantly to transport-related climate goals.

The L7e class (to which Microlino belongs) permits 15 kW rated power, max 450 kg (excl. battery), up to 90 km/h, and requires a B driving licence.
